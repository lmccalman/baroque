145.
                                                R. H. d'Azay. N°1


                        Toulon, the 20th April 1810.

                                The Knights
d'Oppède Ship Captain, commanding
the Atalante, M. de St Cécille Ship
Lieutenant, the Schuassardy, and M. de Cornulier
Ship Lieutenant also, the Ringo.
    The King's Flute, the Courageuse, is ready
to go to Genoa to disembark the
[Matelos] of Or and Magenta with which she is
loaded for the [Convière] of this City.
    The Regiment of Réinwall Swedish
Infantry of the Rg, and that of Luzerne the 2nd [Mage],
these 4 Battalions not yet in Campaign.
    The Rg. of Dauphiné the Réinwall which
comes from Marseille, is destined for
service of the King, and the second comes from
Belfort.
    The Knight d'Ogonnwage Commanding
the Corvette the Pléïade, at anchor in Livorno,
writes from the [St.] to M. de Brangmont, that a
Prussian vessel had arrived there
the day before, to bring Orders to the four
Vessels which were in Livorno, to
depart, but they had set sail
from the 14th.
