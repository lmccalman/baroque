Brest the 22nd June 1778.

In the absence of the orders
of the Minister, we are going to arm
for S. Avril, Twelve of [Varjol a],
and Six [Bregulles]. In addition you will have
put there, M. at the [barmes], six [groselles] with
all pieces preferably, and we will be
punctual; we are working forcefully
in the necessary Preparations.
The official news from Paris, depart,
speak then of Tranquility; what
then do these Movements mean?
time will teach us.
The Squadron of Resolution must
return at the end of July to this
Port, and Join the new
Armament.
We await funds, to pay
all the [Caparctifs].
We are assured, that it is necessary: to fortify the
at the Order to arm two [Longueurs],
the three Frigates, and two other
Vessels in the [Pierre] at L'Orient.
