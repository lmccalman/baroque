About the war no.2

Paris, April 4, 1783

According to the Letters from
Cadiz of the 11th past, the bad Weather
still prevents Mr de la Motte
Piquet, with the Division from Brest
from setting Sail.

The Letters from Madrid report, that
Don Galvez, Minister of the Navy,
died there.

The Corvette which brought the
Dispatches from India, anchored at Cadiz
on the 8th of last month; She had left
Trincomalee on September 24 last
and had touched at the Isle de France,
and at the Cape of Good Hope.

Mr de Suffren was to set
Sail again, a few Days after the Departure
of this Aviso: Only Three of his Vessels
having needed major
Repairs. If the English Fleet
finds itself reunited, we could be
totally destroyed in India.
