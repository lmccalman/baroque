The Dutch have named two [Carrs.] of the [Rép.de] who must go to Bombay [releuvement] of the City of Naples, but in the outcome, the Artillery of [Rampants] has intimidated the Europeans, that they have been obliged to retire. The Water [Magasin P.] from there have made a descent. The [Buttes] who commands there, has reported that it would not have been carried out, if he had eight men more, he could make many prisoners.

The Dutch send a Convoy from Holland to Curaçao, of which they had great need, but it has not had [Appelite] and it is said 14th March to all the Crews the [Bacs] united under their Flag and are discontented.

The advanced Season does not permit the Fate of the Dardanelles. The Turks in a horrible fall at Constantinople, they can receive neither from the Black Sea nor from the White Sea, to make with them the Capital, it is feared that all this may bring some Catastrophe.
