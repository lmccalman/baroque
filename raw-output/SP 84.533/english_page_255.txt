N°. 15

Paris the 25th October 1773.

Our Letters from Basel
announce the Return of the Frigate la Belle
Paule, commanded by Mr Bonieu, arrived on the
19th at Alexandria in Egypt, to pass from there to
[Melee], & this Frigate has reported that the
Towns [pour pu faire les forts] the Beginning
of the arrival, & the opposition to the Aid of the Enemies
by a change of Vessels, keeping [arrets] for
near their arrival.

The Frigates armed at Basel, are ready, & they
will all depart in the Course of this month.

Our Letters from Basel of the 18th 8ber & from Grone of the 8th
8ber inform us that the King, of whom we
have already spoken:

The [Saufrance] of action, of the Company of the Indies,
[tires], has arrived the Barque at Lorient: the
Great number of Rank which was of the Number [à voné]
seek their salt, since it is in Garrison
at the Cape of Good Hope & the three Vessels are
for the Account of the [Nego].

Our Letters come by this Vessel, have nothing
remarked of interest touching the Islands of
[Hounes] and of Bourbon.

The Spanish Regiment in Spain these two
[lors] of decree, in Consequence of the Cargoes
will depart from [Pourchatin] & selling [Vanfleue]
to [parfin] there.

We pray to see [parmons Oremances],
but no [tourré], that the King does not show arrived
[Ronties] arrived. It is a [Humance]
Ducluzeau
