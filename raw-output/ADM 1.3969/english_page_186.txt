We have just published: Two Decrees
of the King's Council of State, one
Dated last June 28, permits
Foreign Vessels arriving from the
Coasts of Africa, with Cargoes
of Negroes, that is to say, with One Hundred
Eighty Blacks at Least, to land in
the principal Port of each of the Islands
of Martinique, Guadeloupe,
St. Lucia and Tobago, until August 1,
1781, and to sell there the said Blacks,
paying for each head of Black,
a Duty of One Hundred Livres, Money of
France.

The second Decree, Dated last
July 9, orders starting from
next September 1 the execution
of the Dispositions of the Decree of
June 6, 1767, concerning the Transport
of foreign Riche.

oneself currently, with the Russians and the Turks,
some want that the War [doume].
