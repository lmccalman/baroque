Madrid the 19th July 1784.

The King received the day before yesterday
at eleven o'clock in the morning by a
special courier, dispatches
from Monsieur the Count de Montmorin which [couroit]
from the frigate Aigrette, it appears that on that
day began the first
attacks, by a bombardment and
general cannonade of Ste Ombierlitz
and [Sa.] cannon barges that lasted
about eight hours, and caused
very violent fires in various places
of the Town, and damage to the ramparts
and to the fortifications of their [mots].
The Algerians made the utmost efforts
to prevent entry into their bay,
they presented themselves proudly with several
cannon barges and other vessels
of their navy in which our
cannon did much damage,
