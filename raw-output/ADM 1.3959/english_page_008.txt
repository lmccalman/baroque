128.


        Brussels the 2nd March 1772.

        [SEAL/STAMP] Several French Officers

are leaving for this City, to
go to Poland, but all under
borrowed Names, who are going
to serve all the Flags of the Confederates.

The Generals newly drawn
out of the Bastilles of Prague Regiment,
compose a Corps of 800 Men,
they are called the Volunteers of
Poubent, they take an oath to
to go join at Luxembourg a
Corps of Generals that there is in the
said City, and which bears the Name of
the Imperial Legion, it is also said that
it is the Prince de Ligne who will
command them.
