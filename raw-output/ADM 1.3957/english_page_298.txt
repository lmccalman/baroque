                                        R. 4 Aug.t N° 2.

                    Bastieules July 1770.

            I have been very exposed
until now in this Peace, because it
was not very interesting to speak of
some Stories of Bandits, although
there was no lack at all, neither of Murders
nor of warring, done with the Help of the Escorts,
but for some time the Number of them
has so greatly increased, that it became [s'en fille] to enter
into Blame with both of them.
            There were at the Beginning of
this Month in the Vendée that the King had
sent to Boulogne, who were going there to
[contrebasser] all the Villages, they have taken
Posts to close the Passages to them,
they marched against them; but
unfortunately there were only
thirty Men in an Ambush
which they forced, losing Two of
theirs as well as Two Soldiers.
There was an Officer whose name had been
killed in the [fens], and whom they have since
made war upon.
