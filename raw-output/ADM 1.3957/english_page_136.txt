Abbé Bonjean has been judged, who had experienced
enough success with the Holy Father in another
[Depeine], and with whom this goes more clearly
the future; the eleventh Judgment has been rendered
on Father le Frenne, Jesuit, Father O'Huany,
who escaped, will be treated more rigorously.

The Duke of Chevreuse has [d'enfleur] Parliament
of Rennes, to recommend to it Father
Verger, who was his Class Prefect, and that he
would answer for him. Two Jesuits have
performed Lent [ou nestra] in the Province.

Several [de Calons], de Blefield, and
Master have provided to the Duke of Aiguillon
for this affair, and master Jesuits
to the Court of Peers.

It is at Versailles that the Parliament, the
Princes and Peers will assemble the day after Tomorrow.

The King has granted to the Wishes of the Assembly
of Clergy, the Bishopric of Roseles for Abbé
de Cicé.

The Archbishop of Bourges has been received
in the Position of Chancellor of the order of the Holy
Spirit, whose Chancellor had been named
after having proposed it & then.
