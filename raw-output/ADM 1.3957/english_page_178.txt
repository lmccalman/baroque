109.                                                    H. de May. No 2

Paris the 4th May 1770.

                                    There is nothing
yet ordered here for Brazil, except
the provisions. They are preparing in addition
Supplies an Embarkation of 400
men & women to make passage
to St Domingue.

It is tomorrow that the Court of Peers will be
called to Versailles; 80 Boxes are
already loaded with documents for the Supporters, have
returned to Brittany. Nothing more is
known of Mr de la Chalotais.

The Regiment of Clermont Prince
Cavalry, which was at Saintes, has been ordered to
go to Limoges, because a Revolt is feared there,
the Misery there is Frightful,
there are designs in Limousin but I
know nothing of it.

Bread is increasing in price here, and everything is
more expensive than ever, our Capital is
so blind that one no longer knows where to house
the people coming from all Parts.

Here are two Reviews in Succession that it freezes to Ice,
which losing in the Loire, the Mulberry trees,
& the Trees are very damaged, the vines also
by the frosts after the great heats.
    At Metz. The Army leaves us in Peace
however at this time.
                                                        Mr Joppe
