N° 55.

Paris the 4th October 1775.

The Letters from our
Ports speak only of the ordered Armaments
which are being carried out, the chartered Vessels await
only their Instructions to set
Sail.

There is much talk here of the prompt Departure
of the young Marquis de Vries Jones, he is
said to be charged with the Execution of a Commission
with the Batavians who have also been
charged by the Crowns of France
which makes known here the Troubles
of Geneva. We could perhaps
speak at greater length in the next
Post about the Batavian [lesquels], he has been
[un heur des conspirés].

The son of Vries Jones the Admiral, is
honored, his father, by the esteem of [M. les hommages]
and will be the Vessel of Youth,
and proposes all things for the next
Assembly of the States, one can always [say]
that this Assembly will be tumultuous
The Count d'O will attend it

It is expected that Tomorrow the News
of what will have happened at Metz, where it was yesterday,
that the First President was erected yesterday
[à-call] in the forms as Burgomaster,
after the General Tournament [Me ds Hauthous]
is named at Avignon.
