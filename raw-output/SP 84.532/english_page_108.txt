Brest, January 18, 1793.

We learned
last Friday that they were going to draw
lots for the Details of the Meurthe for
the Garrison, and We believed it our
duty to write to you to know
if it would be necessary to make His replacement.
The Union, commanded by Mr. des
forges sols, and the Loire by Mr. des
Villacoublays, having on Board the 4th
Battalion of the Normandy Regiment
set Sail, as well as the Second
Battalion of Artois, on two Merchant
Ships. The Union and the Loire
go directly to the Isle of France;
it is believed that the Battalion of Artois
goes there as well; but many People think
that it goes to Ouchoa.

We saw the Roy [investis]
of Auxonne and Kellerie, and set
Sail for its former desti-
nation of Malacca, where the 4th
Battalion of Anjou. The Regiment
Cuirassiers touches at Sartellerie, La pertle,
at the [Carennes] for the Service of the Ports,
with the 3rd Battalion of Normandy.

Cambruivis
