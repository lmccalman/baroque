Rennes the 20th September 1770.

The three orders of the Estates
assembled on the 3rd of this Month at Rennes, and I do not doubt
that there will be held a Morning, & Object of
their Deliberations, which has been lively but without
[éclat], has been the demands made by various emigrants
to return [a porvée allusions] for the maintenance or
[acte Mobilisée].

The President of the Church was claiming 1600
livres permanently, & for & Michel de
Royer & The Marquis Le Belle amount to 1800
together, & The Marquis Le Bois has been well.

The Commissioners of the King have entered at
[Menuil] to make Observations, The Speech
of the Duke of Duras has been general and has for object
[plusieurs personnes] in Civil State, nearly 22
Notable families.

The & a Morning, [Voeuvres] and new Suites,
[Messias] established for the Rule of the Religious, after
the orders the Development of the Months, and the
has expressed to the & to the of State, & Upon order of the
Nobility with project to postpone these against
the Regulation of 1768.

But the Branch and and Master Lemieux [Duranns]
alone at State of affairs in Brittany, the ready
[enseigne Vappui] in his ready [obser] Duke
of Duras, for the ready and [franc joues] on to the
King the Wishes of the Estates, & expressly the affairs
of the [Lt Chevalus], & these two Magistrates [ditemas]
what the Duke learned said strong.
