Madrid the 29th July 1781.

This last day arrived
an extraordinary courier from Cadiz,
with the news that the [R.u] current
should arrive in the bay coming
from Havana, the two vessels
of the King the St. Dominique, and the St. Philippe,
departed the 12th June; the latter having loaded
at Vera Cruz, after having left at
Havana five hundred thousand strong piastres
for the construction and payment
of troops, comes still richly
laden; and one estimates its cargo
as much in gold, silver, as in other goods,
at nearly four million strong piastres,
as much for the King, as for
private individuals. A small Catalan vessel
which had put itself in convoy with the said
vessels, was shipwrecked, and all on board
perished there, among others, sixteen officers of the
Regiment of Asturias.
