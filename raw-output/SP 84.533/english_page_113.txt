N.o 67.

                                                Paris the 18th April 1791.

                                                                        Gentlemen

My Deputy and I, according to certain circumstances and the superior Orders of the Events which should hasten our part in the Journey, we have only yet seen the Registers and the Establishment of Beauvoisin, we have dispatched only the Secretary, and since tomorrow, and 87. [taillés] Members, there is a [Persévertion] of these [Cailles] deposited on the hospitals.
    The Merchant of the Chamber of arts, who was between the Chamber and the General Account of the Messrs. carrying these Members of Parliament who were not exiled, the [Bernaye épître], the Marshal and Mr. Burton de [bombivante], the 8 and 30. Hours at Relay, where they are between [Portante] were today, of the Order of the Countries, Mr. Burton of the hospitals of the Order of [Mornéation]. We were [Portente portent] of their [écution] of [Enges], Inspector Salon of the Countries who [tentoit] their [Épisces], [devant] for the [Sortier] Grand Chamber in Paris where he could the your Grand the land of the Grand Chamber, the common [tournée], and the two who [dois] of a [Perséer], President, 4 Presidents de Mortier, 2 Presidents for the two [quêtes], 2 Clerical Counselors, and 32 Lay Counselors.
