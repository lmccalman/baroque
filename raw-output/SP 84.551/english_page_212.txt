N° 70.

Paris, April 26, 1776.

It is reported from Brest
that on the preceding Wednesday the [Boudeuzes]
of the [Curieés], and [tonacées] the next day.
[Jeno vefonto] was two days later
& Departure from [la Homme].

Many workers have been dismissed
[Milhers], and the blacksmiths, but they are still
keeping the carpenters.

Daily there arrives in this port
construction wood, the project is,
to have up to two vessels, and one
fifty [Myades] armed. They are [feu-
uitant] in the other ports.

Orders are given, to have
built at [Bourly que], a frigate,
in the [vette], whose plans
the [Memoires] has approved.

There is nothing from Toulon. Our letters
from Corsica announce nothing
of interest.                                    JL
