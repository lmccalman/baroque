Paris, October 5, 1784.

Word from Rochefort,
that several Sailors, Postillons, and [de
Manche], Marine officers, who had
been placed in the Royal Prison at [Caufoix]
of Cayenne, left the Prison on the 18th
of last month.

Knight Turget, appointed Governor
of Cayenne, has arrived at Rochefort where he
will embark on a Frigate, which will be armed
expressly to carry him to America.

It would have been definitively decided
regarding the fate of the Company of the Indies,
that is to say, regarding the Measures to be taken
to continue its Commerce. It is
however very doubtful, that It will form
new Establishments in
Bengal and the Coromandel Coast, while
maintaining the China Trade.
As for the Islands of France and Bourbon
It encounters many Difficulties
concerning the Cession that this Company wished

at first accepted, but the Matter [déplorée]
put to Examination, does not appear to suit
