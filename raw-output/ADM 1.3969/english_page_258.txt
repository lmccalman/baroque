                Paris the 10th October 1780.

People are still occupied with
the upcoming War against the two
Emperors, [d'outres et on fr gloire]
always to detach Sweden which
could form against the Grand
Seigneur. They write to Versailles that
one believes that there had just arrived
Dispatches from Berlin, and from Vienna,
which seemed to promise, that
the Emperor was stopped at the moment
he was going to cross the Rubicon;
the Empress of Russia, content
with the fine Campaign that She has made
without firing a single Cannon shot, was
not very eager to assist him,
thus it would turn out well that the year
would pass, without any hostilities, and
that the Emperor would bear all the
burden of this Campaign, without
any Profit, for having fatigued
