M. de Chimée is appointed with the
nomination to the Command of the
Hirondelle, which must be fitted out
in 5 or 6 Days.

Another Letter from Brest
of December 20, 1779.

All the [Perennes] of the
Nomination which had been made of the
Provision on Board the Vessels, & Union,
are embarked this Morning; a Part
of the R. [Bataillol] in [Dombaslégent] [demeurm]
on this Vessel; and the Rest on the
[Sérié], and 90 men outside a
transport Vessel. M. de
Coningh who commands the Union,
has received Instructions, one does not
know too much where he goes, when one will manage
to know it.

The Regiments of [Aux Bourguesa]
[Sons] near [Cours], and one reviews that in
its turn the Dauphin, the Rhône, and
the Belle Poule.

The Hirondelle
