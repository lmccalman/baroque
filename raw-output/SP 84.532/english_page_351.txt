N° 23.

Paris the 22nd March 1773.

No News from our
Peers nor Through those of Finances, and from our Vacations.
Our Letters from the King were read by the 1st that of Messrs
from the Province, concerning their 1st Letters, the Experience:
But the Parliament has neither responded, nor Given
The Registration of these of Declarations.

Nothing New for our tribunals, That
gives us More in Council, they cannot
Find that we will not have the King.

The King has [rethéré] at Metz Messieurs Messrs 1st le Bonnart
the President of Finances; a general Assembly
for the 25th March; but the Governor [d'est] in
Finances. As The Registration will be done towards
the Motive of this extraordinary Session.

The Chancellor after rendered an Decree The King the
last, The The [Bâtons] Parliaments of being a Letter
Patent from the King, on The assembly of the Tower, and on the
of Very in Included.

The Parliament would be at Its Limits by the
Body of Three, to find it a Judge The [Cleuril]
The Burgundian who has had done the Years of 22nd
March, is announced to us at this Session which must be
rendered to Henry IV. That will go back up these
Difficulties; when it is at the Court of Aids and
at The Chamber of Accounts to find themselves there,
as well as it is at [L'Longueville].

Since at [Sous] one has begun several
at Palace; where all the Lords of the king will be
whose Judgments have been appealable.

On.
