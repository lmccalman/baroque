Madrid the 21 June 1784.

The 2nd or 3rd current arrived
at Cartagena, coming from Malta, a
ship of the line, two frigates, four
galleys, and a xebec of the religion
of St. John, under the orders of the Bailiff of
Froulx. The court has also received
news of the arrival at Cartagena,
of the Neapolitan squadron, destined
against Algiers, as well as at any moment
one expects to receive the news of the
departure of our expedition for
this great enterprise.
