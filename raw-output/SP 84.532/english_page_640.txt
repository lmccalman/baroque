Letter from the Correspondent
of Sieur Re Conte.

Paris, May 01, 1775.

My Court Clerks
[la vîmes] have informed me, that we had
purchased a number of [Charghaurs], both in
[en brede], and elsewhere, and that one must
bring in about a hundred others
into the Porto Brasil; that work continues
always seriously, that there is much
effort, to put our Navy in
a respectable state, and that one has [dés] the
needs, in an improvised manner to
[bien du Moises].

I must repeat to you, that there [s'y poufte]
very certainly, all this impenetrable
[Candit], something of the
Serious and interesting between the
Courts of Versailles and of Madrid.

I have discovered, at present almost without
much doubt, that among the 3 or 4 pairs
of different Persons, of whom I
