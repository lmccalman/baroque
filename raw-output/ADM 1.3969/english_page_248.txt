Madrid the 22nd September 1782

Don Bernard de Galvez,
Lieutenant-General, arrived at
Cadiz with the last Convoy from
Havana; having been summoned to
Court, is expected there at any moment
with the Lieutenant-General of the
Navy Mr Solano: the last Convoy
has brought back to Europe on its Transports
the Remnants of Four Infantry Regiments,
namely, Prince, Spain,
Guadalaxara, and Navarre, with
1665 Officers, and nearly Two Thousand
Soldiers, who are approximately a Third
of their forces, the other Two Thirds
having perished in America, either from
Disease, or from Desertion, &c.

Our National Bank
grows fatter every Day, by the [sortes]
Entries, generally in metallic Species,
being brought there with Eagerness.
