309.
                                                    19 to Auguste

                        Paris July 27, 1770.

                        It is reported from Toulon
on the 19th that the King of Tunis had consented to the
Restitution of the Ships and Corsican Slaves, and of the
Vessels other than the four from Ragusa for which
he would return even the money he had received
for the Sale of said ships, but M. de Bordes was not
convinced because a Jew had assured foreigners
that this is so, that he had heard it said from the Mouth of the
King, to make him go, but the King proposes to refer
to what was discussed in his Divan which will assemble
Soon.

It is written from Brest, that there is question of arming
in this Port the Vessels the Zephir and the Pluton
for our Islands. They continue to fit out in this
Port the 29 Cannons, the 18s and the 12s, and to send
the Cannonballs to the Workers of the Ports and Colonies.
They are employing 200 Poor people to fill and to
level the Quarries which have become the object of
the Town, and of the eyes of the convicts, and of the women and
children. One stayed 10 months in Rome and about 4 years in Paris
with the Children, they are making in the Town a Petition
for the Parliament, at the Request of the King has been sent
to send the attested facts of six hundred
                                    men.

It is written to us on the 20th from of July of the 17th and 18th that
that the Vessel l'Africain, good Sailer, after
several Voyages, brought back Wheat from
the Island of Malta and the Island of Sardinia to the Island of
Sardinia, and that plantings of barley were made in the Island
of Madagascar, loaded with Cattle and Provisions:
It will make the Voyage to St. Domingue in November, there have been
dispatched from Bayonne for St. Domingue some lots,
the Ambulans in Paris of 400 Cattle, he sees Lots
of 400.
                                    Orig.
