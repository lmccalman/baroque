Madrid the 20th March 1782.
Ormonde from Cadix of the 28th.

It is [s'hers pathe], that the Communication
with Gibraltar, was entirely free
and open. That Mr d'Estaing had
received the order to have sent back
the Squadron, and the French Troops to
their respective Departments, some
to Toulon, and others to Brest, Rochefort,
&c: but that there would remain in the
Hospitals of Cadix, from 2 to 3 Thousand
Sick, both Soldiers and Sailors
French.

It is assured, that the King has had dispatched
his Orders to Cadix, to have depart
without Delay, Eleven Vessels of his
Squadron, to go to the Aid of the
Poor Inhabitants of Calabria &c,
&c, with Assistance of all
Kinds.
