Paris the 7th April 1782.

The Squadron of Toulon has been
obliged to anchor at Malaga because
of Contrary Winds. That of Brest having departed
from Cadiz on the 16th of last month, was signaled on the 31st
id at Brest. The [Quaderne] had already arrived there.
[L'itineraire l'avéau] in spite of M. Cornil
whose [ombré appareroit de l'elite] has
departed at present with an English vessel
to Newfoundland, to establish the Boundaries, and
receive the Restitution of the Islands St. Pierre
and Miquelon.

It appears decided, that the
Port of Cherbourg will be put in a state to receive
Ships of the Line, but before beginning
this great work one could form
an Idea of the Beach, and the shelter that will be given by
a [Grille], [il déjà un] amassed the Materials
by taking the measurements etc. which must serve
to form the piles and to raise the forts.
The administration wishing that nothing
can be lacking for the works, allocates to them
36 Thousand m of Livres, which cannot
be diverted to any other use, and which
will be able to suffice to finish this great Work,
