My Lord                                Rotterdam 3 May 1710.

As the Spanish Company do not here
drawn upon me for their Butter which was due
the 3d of this Instant, and as the Honours to-
day tenderd to your Lordship, I am to desire
me and my Commissioners to let a g. Walker
shall by the most Publick, the Liberty, the
now upon Holliman Davis Esqr of the Treasury
for Bd. 14.18 Sterling (being the half of the
above said Sum) by my Bill payable 14 Days
after Date to the Order of his Excellency William
Davidson and Company.

The quantity of Butter by me mentioned
in my last Letter, is arrived at Helvoet, and
is taking proper Measures to revise, and
to extend the said Correspondence, and I hope
some time hence to be able to give your Lordship
some Account of the Progress that he has
made.

I have the Honour to be most Respectfully
                    My Lord

                              Your Lordship's
                              most Humble and
                              most Obedient Servant

                              R. Welters
