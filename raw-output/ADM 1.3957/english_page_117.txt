R 31 of said 1776.

                          Paris the 20th March 1776.

                          Instructions are sent to the
Archbishops or Bishops, Presidents or Intendants of the
Provinces, and to the parties not to go to Toulon.
     M. Berger de Leguille commands in the
[Marresson] and the Port. The King has [sy craint] placed
Maurville commanding the land Marine troops
by commission.
     Nothing is written of the interior of Aix nor of the other
ports.
     It has happened that the Ministers have obtained
that nothing would be diminished from the Sums paid
for their Departments, and that they have shown
to Surpley that they make of them, and the Necessity of these
same Sums.
     The [Remanentiation] is always sent to the
twelve Ministers, the Interior ones both in absence,
and it is to those who have [bleuté] to treat the
human and administrative affairs, although they have the form
administrative, and the object of the [gécouverte] with the
[Emoncé], and to profit from them the Action, the Emergencies
[fourneillent] entirely, and hold the Projects
proper to dispose the Spirits in the [Savareur].
     The Count of M. Marentin has resigned in favor
of his Son, the one who was [Hussau], and upon
charge of Chancellor of The [Cerdre] of the [Mis] of [Reignit],
but to [Regonnement] of the King, but has had [juster]
this charge upon the [Secreires Chances lancien] to give them
the Right to carry the [Cerdre Roteau].
