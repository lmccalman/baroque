Paris the 23 February 1775.

The Letters of Jussion
of the 11th announced nothing New;
they were there in Expectation of the Orders
from the Court for the work which would
precede them.

The great News of the Day is the
Disgrace that the Chancellor is sent back to
the Palace at noon. The Two of the King
of the Parliament were [Immanulés] the [Sallettes]
at Versailles; the King himself announces to them
an Edict of the Day; to have the Court
read this Edict, two Councillors
to make the Registration of it.

The Chancellor sent back to Paris
immediately; left from his house in grand
Ceremony with the Ministers, to
return to the Palace to M. Hermes; the Work
[à Cheveut] at 2½ from 20 to 3 hours, they
[renvoycent] the Palace and the Streets;
this sent everywhere an infinite World;
no other person was found at the Palace,
everyone followed them only, to
see the Courier that the Councillors were going to read
at the Grand St Article, in the Chamber very
