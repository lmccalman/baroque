Madrid, December 2, 1775.

I am written from Cadiz
that they had just enlisted two more
new [Remisfrens] of Vessels
for the fleets of the Indies, & that there were
already 13 named, without counting
the 2 Vessels of the King, destined to
convoy them.

The Spanish War [Présutés], the
St. Dorothea was to depart for the Canaries
with 200 Thousand piastres in cash, that the
Council of France was putting on Board for
Account of its Court; & it is said that a
French [Voudieu] came there to bring this
Money to transport it to the Island
of Bourbon.

I am informed from Ferrol, that they
had News from Buenos Aires there,
that the War Vessel the Beleario
had arrived at Callao at Lima in rather
bad State, they had [deviffe] greatly
from the heavy weather.
