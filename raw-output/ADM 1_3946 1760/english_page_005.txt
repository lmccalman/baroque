84.

P.S. I reopen my letter to tell you that they
come to the Post Office, that the Courier charged with the
Letters from the Posts, that the Courier charged with the
Letters from Ypres of the 6th, heard while passing through Furnes,
the Drums of Bernard and the Cannon from the Isle of Cassel, the
that the Tocsin was being rung in several Churches and that they
[lié à passé]. It was recalled that this could be
an Attack by English Vessels on the Battery
of Portillon, or on the Vessels & of la Villaine.

at Furnes the 11th February 1760.

The [Cantoniere] of Lille is
still at the Head of Dunkerque occupied by two
Battalions. There are other [detaillemens] by which the said Regiment
the 3rd Battalion remains here.

I learn at this instant that the King's Household will remain
in Flanders.

As I was told that 7 or 8 French Troops
must arrive at Ostende and at Bruges, I keep myself perfectly there.

at Brussels the 14th February 1760.

A Battalion of the Regiment
of the King, newly recruited, passed yesterday, going
to the Army of the Lower Rhine.
