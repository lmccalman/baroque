                                                4th 19th Bundle. No 2.

                            Paris the 11th March 1773.

                                    They have sent to
Rochefort the notice for the experience, for
the children of Africa, and sent to St
Domingue.

     That of Kerlau of the 3rd March says
that on the 1st of February a Dutch frigate
of 36 cannons, anchored in the River,
coming from St Eustatia, and brought word that the
Algerians proposed to receive well
the Danish squadron.

     The King has ordered a general call
to arms, and it is believed that these arma-
ments have as their object the Polish in
two or three countries of Sweden.

     The Complement of our squadron [apfêt]
counted of [Vellots] Bretons or [Parlementés],
they have not been, to go to the [chandes],
which to validate, but it is believed that at
the same time are [remus] in Pomme;
that moreover the Parliament would entrust them
perhaps, and that it was necessary to wait
until there be a well established Parliament,
and [bât] with the King and the [nettoies] within.

     They speak of an upcoming general
election and an examination to depose,
