R. 22 Oct. No 4.

Brest the 30th October 1770.

The Corvette la Perle
of 16 Cannons, armed as [Breeze], commanded
by Mr. de Port Lieutenant of Vessel,
set sail yesterday for the Sea of the
Antilles; she goes first to Guadeloupe,
then to Martinique, St. Lucia, and to
St. Domingue: Her Orders are Sealed.
[Nos abservent être armeriés] only on the high
Seas; she has taken three Months of Water,
and six Months of Provisions.

[Mgr. de Boismont s'offre Lars], and
100 men of Crew: she has left
from the [dégfer] of the English.

They have put 18 Officers and 6 Brigades in
a State to be armed at the first Moment.

They are currently working to put
to Sea the 2 Vessels which are on the
Shipyard, there are two in addition [d'armement]
2 Frigates and 2 Corvettes, which they believe [à
trois de] to send by an order from Paris
Workers of all Kinds from this Port
to the [voisante] Island, and this [cngent] has not been lacking
for Ten Days.

Nantes.
