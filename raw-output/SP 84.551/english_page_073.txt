So that each person may know the Laws
of the State & submit to them with greater
docility, there shall be made as soon as
possible a printed general Code
which will contain all the Edicts & Regu-
lations.

In 1768, the Legislator, viewing
the execution of this Law as very near,
made it desired even more ardently
than ever, by this express declaration:
that, as soon as the general Code
of Edicts & Regulations is made, only those
uses & customs that are not contrary to the Edicts
shall be recognized.

The Citizens & Bourgeois were awaiting
this Code, when the MAGNIFICENT SMALL
COUNCIL brought, to the deliberation of the MAG-

vision of our political Edicts, approved
