225.
                                        14.7 december 1782

                    Paris November 30, 1780

                            The Receiver of farms
at Chaury for up to the year, the last
Is preparing, it is said, to leave His House to the
King of Spain to engage in the prelature
in dues of beneficiations, awaiting
His response. We are always in
the preparations for war, they are renewing the
war ovens, they have ordered the
Chests for the [bigmentels], at Brest,
Rochefort, and at Lorient.

They have already made depart from That of Brest
24 Vessels for our Colonies of
America, they will make depart 600 more.

The Vessels from Rochefort from Lorient
will have to pass to Brest all Gathered together
their Company will be at Easter [australe].

They call Back a Declaration of the
King, that the Duke of Orleans has had
Registered at the Chamber of Accounts
by the Arrangement of the borders;
this Piece is not, Wise and worthy affection;
too much for a long foundation to join it here.

They call finally an Edict of the
King, which Compensates in the [Reynousse]
the [Primagnes] of Europe, and [Compagnes]
the Company of General Receivers of
                                        finances.
