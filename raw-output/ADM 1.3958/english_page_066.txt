                                      the 10th of February

                    Paris the 3rd February 1771.

                         The Peers of the Blood
assembled 3 days ago at the home of the
Count of Clermont in the Parish of [la
voir], because he had the goodness, he was designated
by M. d'Orleans to be present
at [Trone]. The latter was named, in the capacity
of [Évelets] of the Parliament, and of the
necessity to reestablish it for the general
Good and the support of the Laws.
    This Memorial was sent to His Majesty
by the Duke of Orleans, the King received it
very badly, and disapproved [étoit comte
Promes]. The Memorial was not
the same: This one returned in the
[Champs] to Versailles, he saw the King.
There is no Doubt that they explained themselves
differently, for they proved
[écrire] very content with one another.
    The News of the Peace has spread
a universal Joy everywhere, but
especially in all our Ports, except
the Naval Officers, who envision
[voler] the War for the [promouvoir]
of Advancement.
