                                                17 May 1703.

From Cap François St Domingue
    the 8th June 1703.

On the 3rd of this Month at 7½ o'clock in the evening
we felt an earthquake,
which lasted two Minutes at least;
some people said it had
lasted nearly four Minutes. It made so strong
Most of those who were astonished who have it.
Many Houses in Town have suffered,
some have had considerable
Damage, but not one fell down.
In this Plain there were some
Losses of Livestock, which was knocked down;
going up into the Parterre of D and M,
the Effects were quite considerable.
    There was likewise in several
Places in the West, le Boucasier, Leogane,
Jacmel, there were several Buildings,
entire Plantations overturned,
and all the Petites was overturned.
    At Port Margot the Ravines, Mills
and Drying house were largely overturned,
namely at Mr Surié's, at Mr
Nemon's, and at Mr Duclos's.
