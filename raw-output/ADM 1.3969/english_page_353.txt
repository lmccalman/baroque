Paris the 21. May 1784.

There appears nothing very
interesting at this moment, neither at
Court, nor in the City.

One sees in the Public a Notice
on the Port of Bayonne which is quite
interesting for Commerce, bearing
since this Port has obtained the franchises
which ought to restore to it its former
splendor.

Nature seems to have taken pleasure
in uniting the two rivers the Adour and
the Nive, to form one of the most beautiful
ports in the world; this union occurs
under the walls of Bayonne, the
roadstead of Boucanlt inside the bar
has all possible advantages; but
the bar offers difficulties, it does not
have permanent stability, and
varies sometimes from forty to
fifty fathoms from north to south, these
changes are not daily,
