N° 13.                                                      Paris February 8, 1775.

                                                            The Princes of the Blood
[le Gentilshomblés], and at 5 to 6 o'clock; at the
Count of Clermont's, all under the Pretext of
seeing him, because he has the Gout; they [fit
Signé pour n.] to enter. [Eux] a Memoir
to the King in the form of Remonstrances,
in favor of the Exiles of the Parliament,
and of the Recapitulation to restore for
the general Good, and the Support of
the Laws.
     The Memoir was sent to his Majesty
by the Duke of Orleans, the King received it
very badly, and disapproved of this
Conduct, by a Response which he
sent to this Prince: The latter
withdrew immediately to Versailles.
He saw the King, and without Doubt he
[Respliqua] differently; for
they appeared both very well satisfied
with one another.
