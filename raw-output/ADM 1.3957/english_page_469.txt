Paris November 9, 1770.

It is reported from Rochefort
that the Dauphin Régné, the Bizarre, and two
Flutes which had left this Port, have Orders
to proceed to Brest, where they would be
employed for important Objects.

It is added that since the 15th of October
last [affirmé et de la faillé], the Saumière,
the Bidé and the [Plumes assourdentes] with
two Frigates the Rhémes, and they claim
to destine these Vessels.

One would find in all the Ports established
Magazines to prevent Needs.

The Rumor has spread here and elsewhere, that the
last [Catastrophe] of Bourgogne
can have arrived at Court in 20 hours:
it is not said whether by land or by Sea,
one does not know what it reported, but they
speak of Peace between the [Juges] and the Russians.

Our English at Brest, [manque
l'Association renouveau] of Rousseau.

The Commerce of Marseilles on what
is said about several of its Vessels
in the Archipelago, requests the Protection
of some Vessels of the King for them
[agant à actuellement] that the Chadelle
and two Chabeks.

Dupuis
