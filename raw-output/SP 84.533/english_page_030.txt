Private Letter

                                             Paris July 5, 1790

                                                                The Parliament has
registered on June 3rd, the Edict of 1789 September carrying the
Suppression of all the Offices of Sworn
Appraisers, Furniture Sellers, & Auctions
only those of the Civil Tournelle
of Paris, with Reimbursement
of the Finance of said Offices, and Extinction
at the Instant of the same Offices on all the
most respectable.

To understand this edict, one must Know
that these Offices had been at a fixed
period, and that they had been suppressed. They have been by
the continuation stronger, than the Payment of said
annuities should take place, only supposing
a very distant liquidation, and that one
could be freed from it [oblongo] the new
Purchasers: thus there was in some sort
to stop the envisions to the effect, by the Office
to take for cash, what would
come back to them in a Word, they are regarded
with Reason as the property of the State.
