137.                    The 12th their N°2.

                                                Paris March 4, 1778.

                The Letters from London
confirm the acceptance of the Armament of
the Batteries of the Ports and of the [Regle], and the
Recall of the extraordinary Workers, whom
had been employed in the Department of War
employed in Paris.

Our Letters from Brest of February 28 say,
that the munitions [nous le continuent à dans]
the Port, but nothing is being loaded: the attention
which [profette] the Bourgogne.

There are still 65 foreign Vessels, of which
nineteen Dutch [prochain elles comptent],
without Brest, in the Number of 18 to 20. Thousand
Men.

The exportation of Grain has been Continued
until next July 1st, in China.

[Le prevoit] an imposing [chamore], levied on
[gels], by the [Ratabissent] of the India
Company: it is certain that the Ministry
is charged with the Means of [la retobles].

[Le prevoit constent], that [l'Hotel Derrig]
has had some Conferences with the
Banker on the [Munition], they name Bayer
M. de Bourgogne to replace him;
and as the latter is absolutely in
finances, from charges profound to their [Rides].

Our [Ralement] declared to have seized yesterday
a [Bâti], which belongs to a Port of Commerce
