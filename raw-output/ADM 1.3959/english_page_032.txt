H. 27. Juin N°1

                    Genoa the 29th February 1772.

                         [SEAL/STAMP IMAGE]

                                          I the Undersigned whose.
I inform you, that they have sent to
Cadrac, the named Mr. Brancone; By
Construction has been directed for the
Captains Castellans, that these Are
Number of 5, as much Greeks, as the Sixth
is from the [Massiles] Catholic;
and that it is destined, for the [Battre]
granted to Commerce for the Indies.

The Sheikh contributed for the Remainder
of the necessary; the Government has
declaring it that this Vessel is
nothing less than for the King: The
Remainder, to dispense the the [longter]
[portér], having on Board Sixteen Hundred
the true Destination.

These last Sums they have rented to
One of the [co gouges Batiques];
built by before of our
Merchants, carrying at around
8000 pounds, and proposed for
an Armament of 12 Pieces of
                                          Cannons
