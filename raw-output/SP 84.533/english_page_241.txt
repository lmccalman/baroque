He has, so strongly, in question of the Marshals
of Brissac, in their capacity as Marshals &
[remplisseur] in the Government of
Paris, which is worth 180 Thousand livres in Revenue.

The Marquis de la Coste, Lieutenant-
General, has obtained from the King the Promise
of the first Grand Red Cordon which
shall become vacant.

The Prince de Montbazon, Squadron Commander,
is no longer going as Commander to Martinique;
he wrote to the King to thank him.
He sent him his Letters of Service;
this Prince married recently, does
not without doubt wish to leave his Wife.
It is believed that Mr de Vaugirars will
replace him.

Letters have been received from
Martinique of the 1st of September; this Colony
has been tranquil, but several
ships [de Comblemont de terres]
which remain there, still keep
the inhabitants in a state of
alarm.

Our Letters from Rome of the 28th report,
