To make with our son M. the Marquis
de Vaulx the Continuation of the Community of acquisitions [j'advietre]
together, I Consent to it Provided that he Acknowledge that I
recognize Myself to have Received the Capital of Ten Thousand livres
which Compose My Claims delivered to the Amount of which
I have made My Remittances to the Community in such manner that I
have nothing else to Withdraw from it and that of all the Goods
which shall be acquired henceforth in this Community until the day
of my death Death this [Monture] the third Must Return to Me
which I pray M. The heirs to Settle with Me at said time
in My Accounts according to the Benevolence that I have for
Them for all the Agents we have Agreed signed in the
Best Community that we have been able This stipulation.

Made this 8 March 1784 For all those to give form
to all the Movables affairs Concerning the Movement I Consent
to refer Myself to the Distribution that M. the Marquis My son
and M. the Count My son-in-law shall judge proper to give Consequently
here it is signed without appeal

B.
