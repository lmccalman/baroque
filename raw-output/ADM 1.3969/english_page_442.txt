                        Paris the 2nd November 1794.

        We still have no
News of the Ships of the Line.
Our Cabinet is impenetrable.
But I work on it with the greatest
Secrecy, but one cannot doubt that
the Emperor is certain of not being
troubled by france in
his Projects, or she knows for the moment
that He will encounter no Opposition
on our part, because He has left
Namur, Luxembourg, &c. ungarrisoned,
to the Point, that these Places could
be taken at this Moment by
Six Companies of Grenadiers.
Whatever the case, It is certain that
this Affair greatly occupies our
Cabinet. Several Couriers,
who came last Week from
Holland from Brussels, and from Berlin,
