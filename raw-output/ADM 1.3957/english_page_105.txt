                                                    B 21 deuxts.

                    Paris the 22. March 1770.

         I am written to from Toulon
on the 4th that they are going to order both [Venus] on the
Shipyards of the Esperance of Perpignan of 56,
in accordance with the Court's order of February 16 last.
         It is assured that they will employ for these Vessels
the Wood from the two condemned ones which are still
good.
         It is reported from L'Orient on the 9th that the
Vessels Mars, the Robuste, and the Hector
cast off, are under Yards, in readiness to
set Sail.
         The Vessels the Brillant, and the Northon
will depart from Brest afterwards; the latter will transfer
to the Roads of the Isle of Aix, to take on there the
Recruits of Regiments that are destined
for the Leeward Islands. They have for the Battalion
on the 5th embarked about 220 men of Recruits
for this Regiment.
         M. le Changey Intendant of the Marine
of Brest, is in Paris, he must depart to
go take in the Name of the King possession of the
Port of L'orient, and of all that depends on it.
[Lorslemen] of the Department of Mery who is also
[aceru] who has Commission as Ordonnateur:
it is M. Changey.
