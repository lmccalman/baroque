Sir-

you will easily see by the Publication
joined last week all the Hague, and by that published
all Utrecht that there is a great disturbance amongst
the Regency, and I am sorry to see that the difference of
Religion is the occasion of it. I hope that the
Politicians will have a good affection towards the
anti, which is only yet amongst the lower sort of
People, will not spread further.

All is in confusion at Zealand since the fall
of Borgensprom, and several of the best inhabitants
are sending away their best effects. The generality
of the Nation is to have a strict inquiry made into
the causes of the disgrace of Borgensprom.
