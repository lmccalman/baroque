Madrid the 29th July 1778.

        They are employing 6 Battalions,
as has been said, but these are those of
Infantry Regiments, which must
embark at Cadix, for Buenos-Ayres,
and 600 Dragoons, likewise who will
take at St. Telliers, under the Orders of
Mr. Yriola, Colonel of this Corps, 485
Officers, who will depart today, and
will go to Cadix, with several
charged Artisans.
        My Letters from Barcelona of the 20th
Current state, that they were working there
night and Day on War Preparations
of all kinds, and that they
would put in the respective Vessels
for the transport of Munitions
and troops to Cadix, and [de en estal]
likewise at Alicante, Carthagene,
and Meulugo, from where they will draw the
                    Garrisons
