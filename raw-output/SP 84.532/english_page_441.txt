Letters from Correspondents
of Reum Le Compte.

Paris the 8th April 1778.

Although one still have
the Reserve, and nothing appears to me
very important at the present Moment,
there being before you to communicate:
Here you let pass without my
addressing to you my dispatch of weekly News of the Parliament of Paris in exile; the
members must very urgently to
the Chancellor, where, it is said, the King himself
is sometimes found, and that he forms there
the Plan to form the most
prompt and quickest Royals; so that
these councils and Royal Authority with the
Claims of the said Parliament.
Some Persons who approach
closely the Character of the Minister,
they even accustomed to Publish some
rumors, believe that this [hurd] will happen
well, and even soon; because the
King has need of tranquility within,
and that it is of all Necessity
that he put the finances back on a good
