Monday the 10th of this Month at Ten
thirty in the Morning the Powder
Magazine of St. Madam, which is Three
Leagues from Bordeaux, blew up;
it contained then more than 15 Thousand
of Powder. The Mill was carried away;
there remained no Vestige of the
magazine, of Six Men who found
themselves taking refuge there during the storm,
only one was found whole.
All the neighboring houses were
collapsed, or strongly shaken
by the shock.

Madrid the 17th February 1783.
We still await the
Ratification of the Preliminaries of Peace.
[On litres] all the Inhabitants who
had taken part on one side and the other, although Without
disarming until now.
Officers of note from the Camp of St.
Roch die here each
Day.
