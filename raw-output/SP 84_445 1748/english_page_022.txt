My Lord Duke

I am sorry to see myself obliged to tell your grace that the fears of my Amsterdam Correspondents, mentioned in my Postscript of the 10th instant N.S. were too well grounded, as the affair of the Regulation the City Council of was has given a good deal harm to every thing that has been hitherto done in that great city. To be more particular and exact I shall relate by way of journal the most material circumstances of that affair.

On the 9th in the afternoon Gomeleich, one of the Burger Deputies that was one of the chief people has brought about the change of the Magistracy, and who had been in great credit with the Royal family in the Backs, a formerly reported that he had received a favourable answer from the States of Holland, or Bedford.
