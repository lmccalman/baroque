Toulon, July 12, 1788.

The Ship la Couronne
of the Squadron of M. de Vaudreuil
returned this 12th of the current month it
brought to Toulon Three Hundred Men
from the Regiment of Soissonnois who
were put ashore by the Champs
and quartered.

We are still waiting for the Ships
le Triomphant, and le Souverain also
from the Squadron of M. de Vaudreuil.

The new Ships le Mercure, and
le Souverain, had visibly,
the sails finished equipped and as
one leaves the Shipyards, it is
immediately replaced by another
and can be armed at the first
Order: which makes one believe that
the intention of the Crown, to keep
its forces ready to act, all the more
so that the [equipe Plein d'ouvr], one making
the Sails in a Corps of Troops
which [vit. presenties] in the Surroundings of this City.
