Paris the 28th January 1773.

The latest Letters
from Toulon announce nothing New to us
there they were awaiting days in anxiety the arrival
From the Court, and the Fate of the Ecclesiastics who had
refused, as they [secouperont] there more than of
the instruction of the Bishopric two.

The King who went Wednesday to Marby, must
return this evening in the Evening to Versailles.

The [insurrection] awaiting from London
assures us that there have passed some affairs quite
considerable; Notably there are some for the
Continental of the Peace, nothing is known of it, to
express the Conditions, but the Affair is
Sure, the Ambassador of Spain, and that
of England all in free voyage by their
to their Courts.

This News which comes from Holland, and the all
indeed so much, in the present Circumstance of
affairs within. A Courier has been sent
to Madrid the same Day.

The [Journeau] Parliament goes within times
That at the Palace, if there is formed a World
infinite, they call Cases, and perhaps
Tomorrow, they will occupy themselves with events more
important, they do not [touaille] from it
come. The new Judges have been [d'été] well
several times by the People, and all him by
by the Clerks of the Attorneys.
