                                    Toulon the 11 february 1770.

                                    The King has just granted
1500 Livres of Pension to M de Bompart for
the Housing of the intendant at his home, the
Offices, 20 Thousand Livres of Gratification
to the Count de Rade for his Voyage to
Constantinople, and 1000 to M de Viales.
    M Viales is appointed to go with
M the Baron de Tott to get cannon iron
and Powder to make Cannons.
    The [fait] for the Count is not yet appointed, they
are going to dismiss him, the whole Court being rotten;
they are sending a Berton to see him with them and
the forces.
    They are also going to demolish the Workshop that
is in the same Court, they are going to make use of the
Pieces that are good from other Places
to put on the Shipyard, and the one that
will bear the same Name.

                                    Paris the 29 february 1770.

                                    They report from Brest
on the 24th, that they are having a new Experiment done there
to recognize the advantages that one can
draw from it for a Vessel of 64 Cannons to
make the Voyage to Martinique, and receive
M Bournier, Malecier, and M Steelein;
Demonstrator at Pelegfeld, Ramberguier,
Roux and Vaufrin to conduct and Offer and his father
                                                        Report.
