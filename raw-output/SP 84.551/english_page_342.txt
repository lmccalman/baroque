No. 83.

Toulon the 3rd October 1776.

The small Squadron
of Mr. Dubon and Turgoin in the Roads,
Sir and the Orders of the Court
which favor him have doubled the two the
[fret], it is a payable [Rassonnement].
There are 13 Vessels refitted and masted
all ready to be armed, and 6 others
which will be ready in a Month.
The [livriers] have Biscuit in oven
the [Veruljeur], and 6 frigates, and bake
continually.

They have seen since the 5th of January last
until now, for Millions 806 Thousand
of Construction Wood, but it is
not dry enough.

They are working to finish the Jetty, and
the Basin, which they have been doing since the year 1680
on the shipyards, they are bringing
foreign workers from Genoa,
Nice, etc. They cannot have there the
Count of [l'Estoling], which was said, and I
bring a Counter of piece not
                                    Armaments.
