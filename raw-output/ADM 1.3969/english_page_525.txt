By Johnson


Paris, January 7, 1785.

One must judge by
the calm that reigns here, one no longer
thinks at all that it is necessary
to arm; however here are the
officers who arrive, and nothing
is yet ordered for their
equipages. Several among them
bring horses, the minister
finding that they have hurried too
much, there is reason to believe that their
sustenance will be at their expense.

The concern that the King has just
shown, could not appear
at a more favorable moment
and one expects here that there will come
to our countryside many
