Paris the 7th March 1776.

We have received from our
Ports. Parliamentary Commander of Toulon,
that the Butter still flies its course;
but that Russian aid would like it,
by the Difficulty of putting much
armor on it; but when we have finished
the [sharg√©e], we will put as much on it as

A Letter from Nantes of the 5th March says:
One cannot conceive what is happening here,
independently of the deep Rumors
of War, for several days we have been
embarking Carpenters, Caulkers
and Sailors, in such a way that our
Armaments suffer greatly from it,
we are forced to tin Arks we are
obliged to pay the workers more dearly.
