N°. 148.

                                Paris June 18, 1770.

                                        A Letter [Reçue] from
Rome says, that the Pentecost festivities having
given occasion to some thousands of Foreign
Persons who had gathered there
from Rome in the Churches; more than Three, This crowd
gave much work to the Troops, as well
as to the Police of the city. They have added, in the city
the Jesuits, but in his new Consistory
it is certain, that what has been Published by
the Jesuits in Rome, no longer does anything
Similar there. But at present, they have
in a Prelate of France, a foreigner who has
relative to the Clergy of Goods, Among
the Pope, to Suspend the Censures, which
had been issued against the Parliament, by
which the M.P. named Cardinal Ducal
On the Duchy of Parma. That which regards
The King on this Side, makes [Scanance] to the Heart of
Naples to the Papacy in the [fées]
Temporal, If it was proper if ever
to have these [avaluans] by the laws, but
Great of Holy Spain, or at least by what
Reason to the Court of Vienna, they Proposed
to the [tension] of this [vides].
                A Letter from Rome Mann
says, that the Rumor ran there, that the [Rofits]
had Recalled the Privileges
