EDICT OF THE KING,

CARRYING suppression, reimbursement & creation
of Offices in the Châtelet of Paris.

Given at Versailles in the month of May 1771.

Registered in Parliament the 28th of May 1771.

LOUIS, by the grace of God, King of France &
of Navarre: To all present & to come; GREETINGS.
By the account which has been rendered to Us of the state of the
Offices which currently compose the Châtelet
of our good City of Paris, We have re-
cognized that those of our Officers who have been successively
received in this Jurisdiction, had been multiplied to the point that
the privileges that We have granted them by our Letters
Patent in the form of an Edict, of the month of August 1768, would become
prejudicial to our Subjects, if We did not reduce them to the number
that We had judged necessary for the administration of Justice.
FOR THESE CAUSES, & others moving Us thereto, with the advice of
our Council, & of our certain knowledge, full power
& Royal authority, We have, by our present perpetual
& irrevocable Edict, said, established & ordained, say, establish &
ordain, will & it pleases Us as follows:
