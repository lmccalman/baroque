N.° 80.

Paris the 5th April 1776.

News from Brest
of the 3rd, that already six Vessels of the Squadron
of Evolution are in the Roads, the others
are going successively.
They have begun in this Port the
ordered effects, by the [brûgules],
the first was the Sincere, they have
ordered a Careening, and to double
the Planking, the others will follow
likewise.
The Commander of the Squadron
has declared, that the Rallying Point
for the function of the Divisions would
be to the South West of Cape St. Vincent,
within Sight of Land; the Signals are
given accordingly.
In Toulon, they speak only of the
fleet, and of the approaching Arrival
of the Duke [d'Ille la Douchette] of Chartres
in this Port.
