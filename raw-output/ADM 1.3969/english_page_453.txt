14 29 Nov. N°4

Paris the 15th November 1784.

It is not possible to be able
to hide any longer the Dispositions
that must take place by our [Battures].
It is said primarily today, that
two Army Corps will be assembled;
one in Flanders, and the other in
Africa, the First of 80 Thousand Men,
the Second of 40 Thousand. The Generals
are not yet known; but
there is agreement in naming the Prince de
Condé, and the Marshal de Broglio.
The Orders for the Provisions
have been given for Three Days.
The Chevalier de Ternay, commanding
the Artillery in Flanders, is here.

The Dutch Ambassadors
will have Tomorrow the Ministerial Response.
The one that the King requested from
the Emperor, is not expected until the 23rd
of this Month.

On,
