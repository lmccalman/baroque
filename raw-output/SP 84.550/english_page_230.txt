Brest 8 May 1776.

The Transport ships the
[emponnées], commanded by Mr
Verdeur, and the 13 Hours, by
Mr de La Villebon, set sail
yesterday from Brest Martin, with a Wind from
southwest-Northwest, but the Wind
having changed they returned,
they entered the next day.

The [Camponnée] was dismasted of
its small Topmast at the Base of
the Trestle-tree, it was noticed since,
that it was rotten.

The Renommée was raised
up, and has re-entered the Port;
it sustained [emportes],
the Damage otherwise is not
considerable. Mr Duthé,
who was to preside at the Council
of War for this [Brigante],
