Geneva the 30th April 1778.

The Couriers made
since our [trompés] in Provence,
and toward the frontiers of Dauphiné,
as well as toward those of C. Heure,
in Switzerland, Tyrol, and the spa
towns, in traversing the hills of
[l'Embe] of people In Thousand Men
There is also seen the great Manor
of Blois.

The Letters from Paris of the 27th March
inform me, that one was coming into the
Ports, several Vessels and Frigates
which have been transported into the Basin and
of Monsieur with a view to making use of them and
which must take it: to Spanish
Launches, and that the greatest
Part of these Ships [troule] in
the Archipelago, and in the Mediterranean.

That the Courts of Versailles, of
London, and of Madrid, live
today in a perfect
Harmony, and that they work
to enter into their views. These
