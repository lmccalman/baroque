Our Letters from Rome of the 8th say, that there is
much laughter there about the story of Father Norbert, that these are not
without Bourbons of whom he errs, and that the Pope
ought to declare them in the Consistory, which he
was to hold the following Monday.

The Count of Benevento will have rendered to him
[ora Re] that already the Neapolitan Bourgeois
are spreading out to reside in their houses, and that it
not be rendered at the same Time, one even says of
[Condettiens]; but that still merits its
Confirmation.

It is claimed that the Pope is quite a Man and
handsome and good Politician, but otherwise he upsets
that will harm him in the Mind of People of the World.

P.S. At the departure from the Gate we receive News
from Brussels which reports that at Court they harass
the Bourgeois, and levy 12000 Florins per month on
the Silver, but there had been Orders to depart
and seeing, and a peremptory Order from the Queen,
but the Parliament had suspended the Suspension
of the Sending of this Money until further Order.

From two Sides the Merchants have been even taken
to the number of 500, some as hostages in this
measure, and the other as others to the King, in which
they show that [reste] the rest of the King of
Commerce, and they declare, that if [elle surs] of
permitted, they [voyages] will go all their
Expeditions.
