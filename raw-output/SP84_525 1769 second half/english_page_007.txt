Rome, July 26, 1769.

The Jesuits are regarded here as lost, & one no longer sees the good union expected at this moment between the King of Naples & the Clergy of Rome [eu] the Minister of Portugal has reclaimed with the Pope the [appellés Bourgnenois & provideo à]
The Grand Duke seeing Tuscany vacant, thought that the King did not [opus] had the same as to other Sovereigns, [les] of the Circulation, the Holy See has [coûté] to France, to beg it to arrange this Dispute.
The King of Spain in his Letter to the Pope says that he has had the apprehension of being opposed in the affair, that he does not touch the virtues [tolerence] the troubles of the Court of Rome, to humiliate them with [ordinaires] in leaving will have not the authority of the Pope.
A great Rumor has arisen here on the Subject of a Papal Bull, addressed to the Jesuits, for a Religion in their House.
