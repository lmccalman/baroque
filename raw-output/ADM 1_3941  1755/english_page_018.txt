at Nantes the 28 April 1733.

    The fleet of Brest has
not yet departed; it is said that besides
the contrary Winds, a definitive Order
from the Court detains them, however
this cannot last long without
great Inconveniences.

at Paris the 28 April 1733.


    The Courier who must
bring the News of the Departure of
the fleet did not arrive yesterday Evening.
    The letters from Rochefort and
Toulon speak of no other armament
than two Galleys, two frigates,
four Xebecs, and two Galliots with
Oars, to watch over the Safety of
the Levant Commerce against the
Corsairs of Barbary.
