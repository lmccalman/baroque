Paris, July 16, 1784

The Letters from Madrid announce to us,
yet again the Departure of the Squadron from Cartagena,
when the Count d'Aranda learned through the
Spanish Consul residing in Marseille, that a
Vessel arrived the day before, had reported having
encountered this Squadron at sea on June 28,
at the latitude of Cartagena: The first Courier
from Spain, will surely confirm this news to us.

The King of Sweden took leave of our Court
these last days, and after having delayed several
times his departure, he has fixed it irrevocably for
next Sunday. There has been no particular Treaty
between this Prince and France.

The affair of the projected exchange, of which one has spoken,
has failed, as much due to the difficulty of finding
in the Antilles a suitable Island for Sweden,
the Spanish not wanting to permit that a
foreign Power form Establishments at
