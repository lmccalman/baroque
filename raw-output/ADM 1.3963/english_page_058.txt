                    Paris the 9th February 1776.

                                        It was believed known
today the Marshal, that had
determined the Count of Maillebois
to compromise the friendship with Spain
and not [mys] ally on what is said
that he ought to have the day of the:
the death of the Marshal du Muy;
& the nomination of the Count of St
Germain to the Position of Secretary
of State for War, having made
Things change face for
Him. He hoped to return to
favor, And up to the present He has
not been frustrated in his Hope,
it is certain today, that the
Count of St. Germain holds him in the
highest Esteem, that he has [confisté]
him much in the [Rufsique], and
that he will make him Marshal of
France. [J'est] from there in the Ministry
