19 Febr. N° 2.

Breslaw, January 1790.

The Count of Roquefeuille,
Guard Commander, is in London with Meunier having
returned from Versailles, without having been able to see the King.
Besides the Frigate Germany, taken at [Hue
nes Cités], several other Vessels have
had the same fate.
Mr. de Duguet [dont sommande] Calais
to the satisfaction of the [Martinig et j.]
[son de Doctement commenceur] the
States for the City of [Guinte], and for the
Account of the Vessels.
Our Letters from Cap Français of November 9
[describptendy] are tranquil.
