Brussels the 21 May 1793.

I have just been able to [pouvoir-er] an Ordinance by which an [importante] of one florin is imposed on each head of revenue in the cities, agglomerations and in the entire ensemble; and the quantity of these Animals there that are maintained there.

Friday a State Ordinance published and sent to all the Counties both of [Eternelles] and of [noirs Aimans] called in relief and to no longer receive Religious men or Religious women for the [Coflot]; but for the two [Païnes] of Miss Etienne d'Amanda, of which the [Mettée] always kept by the Convent which will have received, [à t'autre Morbid] by the Parents who will have requested the hands of thing, and that the Person to be received must have reached the Age of 24 Years, under the same Fine to be paid in the same Manner as in the first Article point.
