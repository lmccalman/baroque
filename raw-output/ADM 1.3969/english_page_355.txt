     One can conclude that this Port is very
practicable for ships drawing two
or three feet of water; very troublesome for
those that draw more, as
there is much sea over the bar
one must count on a warping
of about two feet.
     One has been working for several years
at great expense to retain the bed of the
river near the bar, to increase
the draught, and make the bar hold
in proportion to the greater rapidity of
the water.
     Commerce does not languish at all in
Bayonne; one outfits there for all
