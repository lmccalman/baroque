Conditions, which the Directors of the East
India Company are pleased to grant to such
Farmers, their Wifes and families, as shall be
willing to go to the East Indies.

     1. The farmers, that are willing to go, either
alone, or with their families, to the East Indies,
may address themselves to any one of the Compa-
ny's members at Amsterdam, Zelandrough,
Delft, Rotterdam, Hoorn or Enkhuisen,
as shall be most convenient to them.

     2. They shall have their passage to
Batavia free, provided they work for their
Diet.

     3. To procure them the means of subsisting
honestly and well there, they shall receive for the
first five years a summ of Money annually
even as one hundred Rixdollars, not only to buy
the necessary Cattle for the Plough and Dairy,
but to subsist them, till their Labour can
maintain them.

     4. After these five years, they shall for
three subsequent years be free of all taxes.

     5. After this term, each farm will be
charged with an annual tax of fifty
Rixdollars.
