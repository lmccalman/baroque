Furnes the 24th April 1773.

Its [echparts] from
Dunkirk 31 Sloops and 5 Hourquiets
for the Cod Fisheries; and these Vessels
are equipped with 1 to 9 others for the
same Fisheries.

It is assured that it is still said, that
there are no Vessels notably in
Vessel, for the Guards of the Places;
others however wish to assure
that these things proceed perfectly.

Yesterday that it [ent-ici], no news has been heard
from London, from The Hague, or from elsewhere;
nor from Embden, to hold their mission there.
[Mionholt] living to go to Dunkirk;
there are still only 4 Boatmen.

Brussels the 28th April 1773.

The Army is the departure
from London, [dans], and from this city, the
continue always.

For the [Menier] of [Mag] we await
here all the Corps and the Artillery, with a
number of Pieces of Cannon; which in
ordinary at Brussels, which the others in
Corps, [particularis].
