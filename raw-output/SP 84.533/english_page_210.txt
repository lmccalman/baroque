No 79

Paris September 21, 1775.

The Letters from our Ports
announce nothing very interesting.
The events are not interesting
either in the ordinary course. The King who
went to St. Hubert Sunday, stayed there two
days, His Majesty went yesterday to Choisy
for two days Only.

The affair of the Parlements still makes
the fine News of the day. The Duke
of Harouart, and the Marquis of Lisbon
His Son, departed last Saturday to
go to Rouen, to execute the orders of the
King, jointly with Mr. de Crosne,
Master of Requests and Intendant of the
Province. The implementation has already begun
the day before yesterday with the Suppression of the
Chamber of Inquiries, and [Eaux des Siéges]
of Normandy; and yesterday with that of the
Parlement. We shall have News of it
today.

It is reported here that Mr. de Moncieu,
First President of Rouen, His Son,
First President of Dijon, and one of the
Mr. de Lanvigny, who will withdraw.
