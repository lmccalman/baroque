The Minister of the Council left Paris,
with the 10th of January, the First President
assembled the Chambers, the King's letters
were read, it was deliberated not to
register them and to keep them between, and it was
proposed to [délester] from Paris the Body of
people of the Parlement, the Prince
Duke of Orleans and five of His Peers were named and [du sol écrivi]
to men witnesses against the
famous Leconnas. In the evening the Prince
[d'Avernement] personally the Duke
of Aquillon, and other Members of the
Council who convoked the Commission
of St Malo; finally a Counselor was named
to conduct these exiles, and then sr
Robert Le Duc. Between, it was agreed
that they would suppress the Processes commenced
that [capis] was to bring to the Chancellor
with books to have them come to the King.

Here is where things stand. They have made
leave from Rennes Messrs du Cleu to go
to St Malo and [ceper] the famous Leconnas
to give their Resignation.

The 4 Counselors who were detained;
[Scruples saiées], presented Petition to [ne]
to the [sellement] to be judged there, and discharged
from the Accusation with which they had been Charged.
