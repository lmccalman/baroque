Paris the 3rd June 1715.

Our letters of the
twenty-second, twenty-third of May and the 28th announce
not the slightest News.
There were many Pensions given
to Military men, on the Extraordinary of
wars; these Pensions were better paid
than at the Royal treasury; they have just changed them,
they are discharging them from the Extraordinary of
wars to transfer them to the Royal
treasury, where they will be worse paid;
This does not much favor
economies, but the State is in arrears
for their carried debts.
This provost has given me Councils, for the
Revocation of Privileges of Exemption
from Duties in the Jurisdiction of the King;
which can only produce much
for the State; it announces the forthcoming Re-entry
of the Kings into the pledged Domains.
As for the Price of Offices, it
orders that they will pay the Hundredth
Penny, done when [l'onut], it is ignored,
and very long.
