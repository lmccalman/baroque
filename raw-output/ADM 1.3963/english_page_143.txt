The News from the Horse bearing,
that the Enemies the St Augustine,
and the St Lawrence, and two Boston
brigades, ready to set Sail for
Quebec, with troops; and that
several other brigades were
on the point of receiving orders.

The Secretary of the Election of
Northo, having left from here in the
night from Saturday to last Sunday,
to go sent near the King, to St Sesprit, in a
bark of St George, the regiment
is [?] at Cayenne, whose cap-
tain commands a [tromilière],
[à reine fertile à une Livre du]
when he perceived, that the Caffre of the
Réveins was stronger there than him,
obliged him to go back return him,
to go make Investigations,
and give Report by an express
to the Envoy; but until now he
foresees, that all the Investigations
have been useless.
