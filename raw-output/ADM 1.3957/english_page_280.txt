                                        4. 20 July. NÂ° 9.

                        Brussels the 20th July 1770.

                                The Ordinance
mentioned in my last, has had its
effect, as I had indicated to you.
They sent Two Hundred Men, all
good without selection, which are all
effective for the Sea, without the Three
Hundred from the Mariner Classes, which the King has
already chosen, which are said a few days from being
on route.

They write to me from Berlin, that the Reg. A.B. the
Prince of Prussia, and the Diebus Gadereinster
Are preparing to make Camp, in the Country.

There departed from Leiden Uniforms and [Requilemens]
of which to make Uniforms and to furnish them
Austrian Generals, King John
[Rejouissant] General of France is here in
Rome, the original has well imagined it
here to carry it.

This Journey will take place from the 14th to the 15th, or 16th and the
entry the three named, the Princes of
Brunswick and of Anhalt, and the Generals
Lostalles and de Lydonne the suite of Journeys.
On the 18th April one will be at Compiess, the 19th at Versailles,
the 20th at Schuvestritz, the 3rd [au] at Belgarberg.
                                                                    The 29th.
