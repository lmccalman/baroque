That the Magistrates are; by the position they
hold, of the Monarch & of the Law, the or-
gans of the Nation toward the Sovereign, &
of the Sovereign toward the Nation: that it is in
the exercise of this double ministry, which one
cannot restrict arbitrarily without destroy-
ing it, that consist the essential relations which
constitute every good law, whose end is ne-
cessarily the convenience & the advantages
of society.

That after imputations so damaging,
they can no longer announce to the People the
wishes of the Sovereign, by imprinting on them the
august character of the Law, of which they themselves
should experience the rigor, nor present
the wish of the Nation to the Sovereign, whose
confidence they have had the misfortune to lose.
