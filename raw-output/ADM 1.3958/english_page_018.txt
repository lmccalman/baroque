                    Paris the 25 January 1775.

                                        Word comes from
Martinique of the 16th November, that everything
is in the air in this Colony for
war, they are putting strongly into Order the
Defenses, they are working to fortify the
the Fort St Pierre, they are mounting Cannon there.
M. M. d'Ennery returns to his
favor with the Court, he is
strong he is good and brave.
        The troops which are there, of which strong
those which by Deaths and Illnesses
the Regiment of Vexin is the same
complete, because it has received Recruits,
the Regiment of Poitou which has
arrived, at 500 men, has no more than
689, of which a quarter sick, Bouillon
is reduced to 350. Martinique has left the
half out of service. One does not know
what in this Colony but Thousands
Men in Condition to serve, and about
70 Men of Artillery. There are
60 Men detached at Ste Lucia.
        The Workshops are quite numerous,
but one can scarcely count on them;
they are only good for a Corps
