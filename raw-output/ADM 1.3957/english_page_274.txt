The Representations of the Parlement stated
that the Courts, having appeared with Scales in hand
in July and 5 August 1787, This Parlement was
suppressed on the 15th, as being printed
without Permission. They appealed to the King,
but it was in vain: one supposed, and one had
suppressed them by an Edict, which supposed
that they existed. These Representations
charged heavily the Duke of Aiguillon,
and even the King, also the Court ordered
his arrest remarked, that never had one
seen the King bring forth Just details.

The King wished Tomorrow to hold His
Lit de Justice at the Palace, and erased by the
King's hand the appeals. [Deux]
d'Aiguillon [Jeu].

The Countess of Grammont, Lady of
[chainés] the Dauphine, has just been Dismissed,
She had only 2 [Siècles] to leave the Court,
One speaks of a Successor of 15 to 1
employees, the Thing is not well
The Parlement still increased in Paris;
the [Dédaiction] is in all the Provinces,
the Farmers General complain that there is
almost no more Consumption of Salt and of
other Rights that they farm; The Bishops
do everything through Memoirs in His favor,
are only the Report: all going into departments;
but it appears they no longer have [que trouva].
They dare [loucy] while in Paris, but the [Playe]
has received [Perçe], which disobeys.
