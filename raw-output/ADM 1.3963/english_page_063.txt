Conglans, has finally taken place at their
Dispatch from last Saturday;
but a Number of Claimants are
cruelly disappointed there, for
there have been Favors only for
those who were present, and
distinguished themselves, in the Expedition
of Algiers, but there is no
Captain General. It is assured
however, that His Majesty is
of the Intention, to make yet
another general Promotion
for the entire Army, thus
that we will soon have as many
Generals as Soldiers:
their Number would suffice for an
Army of One Hundred Thousand Men,
at a time when one would have much
Difficulty, in assembling
20 Thousand Veterans.
