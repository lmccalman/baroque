No. 26.

Paris the 28th March 1743.

This is thus an
Abstract of the Memoirs that are opposed by Parliaments
extracts. His Friend, the Abbé de Gévise,
advocate at the Parliament of Paris, is in
Paris lastly, and he will retire
to the Chartreuse de Bossiers.

The Deputy is from the Parliament the
render to hear at Notre-Dame, to
attend the Procession of the Reduction
of La Rochelle, which is at the same time
the first, and preliminary of [Cantamie]
the first Fathers.

The Chamber of Inquiries, and the
Court of Aids arrived a Moment
after, seeing the Parliament in
the high Stalls, returned
on their Steps.

The Court of Aids also [vos] a
Letter, to which Paris the Edict this vis
has name, she to prepare it, and has rendered
a Decree, which carries; 1° that she will
never recognize the People of the
Council as Parliament, until
all the Officers who have the Honor to
serve are other than the Ancient.
