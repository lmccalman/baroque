Private Letter

Paris, January 30, 1773.

The Court is still
agitated by new Intrigues regarding the
Three Ministers that the Duke of
Aiguillon, has not brought Peace.
I am informed that the Chancellor
and the Duke of Aguillon hate each other
mortally, and mutually seek
to supplant one another. They are said even
to have had a very heated Quarrel, in
which the Duke is said to have reproached
the Chancellor for Duplicity and
bad faith, to which the other
replied, [ne put-il point en tenir
Et Parvenez vous qui étes levers
moi] since I Signed your
pardon and [aurai vouleut]. It is
added, that in spite of this Misery
it could well be a matter for
Parliament to take up again the Affair
of the Duke.
