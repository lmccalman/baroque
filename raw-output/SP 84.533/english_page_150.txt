Brest the 22nd April 1778.

The Marquis d'Anouville
Lieutenant General, Director of Engineers
& Inspector General, arrived here
Monday at Noon, with 8 or 9 Officers
who accompany him on the tour,
Madame d'Anouville and his Daughter
arrived in the afternoon, all arrived
lodged at the Intendancy: He began
there the next day His [France] of the troops.
We have in Garrison here the Regiment
of Ragueneau 2 Battalions, d'Imbrosie
one Battalion, Royal Corse the other one
Battalion, and Royal Artillery two
Battalions; [Surister] the Port, our Coasts
and our Batteries of Land and Sea.
they are [fleubi] days at least here
after which he will continue His tour
in the Province.

For the rest nothing New, except
the Arming of the Frigates, of which
we have spoken.
