Copy of a Private Letter from Paris
dated December 1, 1740.

"You ask me, My Friend, for news & pleasant Tales. You know better than I that in [prose balete] there is not always facility. I have informed you up to my last that several different Ministers had secretly different Powers to take together an Idea of Alliance, to treat of Operations that regarded the North their Conferences [develles] would continue it; and where strong the [Seminaires], but there is nothing yet decided."

"I will know more fully what I leave you as the Catholics up to the century of the Stuarts & the good times, I could hazard Conjectures, but my Correspondence warns me principally of the table, tomorrow the dispatch of M. the Duke of Choiseul which I send you presently announced, as well as the realities of the Subject in question, which I follow up to the [loges], to maintain in everything, as in particular, & finally the other stable fact that I long failed to send you, to convince you [d'eleve] still of previous advance."

"The Change of Ministers changes nothing at the foundation of the Ministry, one works always with the same activity both in our Bureaus & in our Ports"

"Be assured that War is decided or that it [s'entre]
