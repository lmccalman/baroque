1710 June N°1

                                    Second.

                           Madrid the 20 May 1776.

                           Letters from Cadiz
of the 10th of this month, make one envision
War as very near with
our Neighbors, however it appears much
more probable, that there will be nothing
this Year, the Season being too
advanced, and that the whole will be resolved either
amicably, through the intermediary of
France, or militarily, but
not War, and even less with
Portugal, if it is true as it is
said, that there is a Marriage after the
death, between the Sister of the King of
Spain, and the Heir of Portugal;
and if frequent Couriers come,
both for our Court, as for
that of Versailles, and although nothing
transpires, however it is claimed,
that there is question of a Congress in
Paris, and that the Marquis of Pombal,
