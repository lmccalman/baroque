The Paris additional Correspondence
when I had upon tryal by Lord Rochford's
Orders, and who was procured by Comte
of Amsterdam, having by me been
discharged upon the Grounds sett down
in the Letter which I wrote to Lord Sandwich
on that first month, has been very
clamorous, and complaining to all he
(for he does not know that I am in this office)
to complain to His Majesty & Secretary of
State. If he should, I ask well a debt for
answer his Charge whatsoever it be;
his Name is R. Pierre. He is an imposing
ignorant Man, who wanted to give his
Notions for Intelligences.

The Accounts of the State of the waters
in the Lower Lands are favourable, and the
hopes of preventing an Inundations
excessive. by the Authority of God &c
that comes down the River this day,
I should judge that the Ice Dam. that
was found in the Course of the River Lek,
