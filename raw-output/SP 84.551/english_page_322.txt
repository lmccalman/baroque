                    Paris the 30th September 1776.

                         The Count d'Estaing
he left to go to Toulon, has
assumed the Command of a
squadron that must be armed there;
he is said to be charged with a
particular Expedition, for which he
will take his Thousand Men.

     Our Letters from Toulon of the 22nd
announce [Son remaniement] not yet
any Armament ordered
but word has already been received of
the coming Arrival of Mr d'Estaing
& [Reunion] of this Port, who left
the Evolution Squadron, arrived by
the Coasts of Barbary, and must
return to the Roads of Toulon
the 1st October.

     The Count de Bougainville Lieutenant
General of the Armies, who was here, has had
Orders to leave for Brest.
