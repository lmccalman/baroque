Whitehall 29th Decr 1726

Sir,

I received yours of the 11th Decr last
enclosing that by messieurs, and laid it
immediately before the King, who was
extremely pleased with the papers &
Considerations on the present Conjuncture,
which was delivered to you at the Conference,
are some enclosed in your dispatch of the
4th Decr, And that you may see how much
His Majty approved of the Remarks & Plan
laid down in that Paper, I send you by his
Orders Copy of the Letter he made me (the
Duke of Newcastle being then out of Town)
dispatch by a Messenger immediately to
Mr Walpole. You will observe from it the
terms of this Letter with which I wrote
to France upon the Contents of this Dispatch
or Piece of the states General, And
you will therefore make these things
Compliments upon this occasion, in the best
manner you can to my good friend Monsr
