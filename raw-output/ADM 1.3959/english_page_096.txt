293                                    R 504, No 2

                    Paris the 28 September 1792

[SEAL]                  The letters from Vauban
say this can only be the opinion of
Châteaudun, which are brought to us;
he worried entirely, that this kind
ought to be settled at their hearth report
to the Commune, so affected to Caëlin.
     All are admitted the most
numerous that one said; these
towns were assembling Councils. The
Inhabitants are in town of lodge
     The treaty of Partition of Poland
has made them to the Council. One does not know
what Russia will arrive for the Porte;
but one thinks, that the [tromée] will enter
to the Crown, and that for the towns
being able to make the happiness of men,
one would obey Constantinople to the new
exploit.
     One knows that there are many
Malcontents in Russia. The Grandes
are jealous of the authority and the
arrogance of the House Orloff,
who were making propositions.
