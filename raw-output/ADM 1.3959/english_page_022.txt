148.

The King of England incessantly sends
orders for their ordinance; it
records for the 8th of March an extraordinary
Assembly of the Clergy of France,
establishes that it is for two reasons;
the first to take a definition
of what was then still demanded in 1748.
This extraordinary demand is not
disguised, for it is known that almost
everywhere the Revenues of Church Properties
have doubled in recent Years,
and are consequently beyond the
Charges fixed in 1748, and consequently in a state
to [y] the 30 Millions are
uniquely destined to reimburse
all the Charges of the Parliaments,
and others suppressed.

Brussels the 30th March 1778.

I have sent the States
of Brabant, with the Consent of the
Nine Trade Guilds, have authorized the Recruitment
of 600 men, namely 300 Walloons,
and 300 [Vientoises] who will be a brigade of
Free Companies, but until now
one cannot know where they will be employed.
