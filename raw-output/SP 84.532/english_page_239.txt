Toulon the 24th February 1773.

                    We will continue to
work on finishing putting our
ships in condition, to repair a Good State,
but especially The Patriote and The Guerrier, and
[Ortra] to have the Armaments
of the Land batteries completed.

                    Brest the 25th February 1773.

                    We still lack
here some of your supplies; and The Small
Good can no longer come.
    Monsieur has arrived since B. hours
90. Barks, a Vessel loaded with
provisions, Munitions, and supplies.
I [partant] you that everything is moving for
and you count that Sunday one
and Monday, if The weather proves favorable
we will have many matters; and
[sel] Workers.
    Today we will put The Roland
to Sea, and [La Siemiede Monde] we
will put The Alcionaire, both
of 54. Cannons, They will then lengthen
On The shipyards.

                                        L.
