Vienna, August 24, 1776.

By the Movement
of troops in Galicia one had regarded
the Rumor, that the Empire had the Intention
of new Frontiers in Poland;
but this Movement has no other Purpose,
than to approach Bukovina, to
support it with Russia, the [Convex-
ation] with the Diet, that the Parties
of Opposition would perhaps trouble
deprived of Precautions, to render it
fruitless. Finally one assures us, that
the Chiefs of troops in Austrian
Poland, have Orders to watch over
the [Moix] and Intentions that they
receive from Baron Revitzski, our
Ambassador at Warsaw.

It is known, that the Salt mines of
[Crhemia] and of Wieliczka, make one
of the principal and most useful
Acquisitions of Austrian Poland;
