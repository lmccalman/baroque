The court has just communicated
to the public the news from Oran, dated
the 1st of the current month, this news reports
that on the 26th past the general
commander Don Pierre Gudin, having received
notice that a body of Moors was ravaging
all the gardens of the place, immediately
had the general alarm beaten, and having
placed himself at the head of all the companies
of grenadiers, marched straight toward the
enemies, who withdrew in order to
seize an uneven terrain,
and entrenched themselves there to the number of
seven to eight thousand men, both Turks
and Moors, commanded by the Dey
of Algiers in person, who maintained good
countenance, and a very lively fire, but
