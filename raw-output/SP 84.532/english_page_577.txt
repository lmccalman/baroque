Paris the 27th May 1775.

Only in the
greatest tranquility in our Ports;
and nothing appears for the men.
The event of the Marriage, and the Affairs of the
Chiefs have caused the greater part
of the inhabitants to emigrate into the neighboring Provinces
Last Sunday at Fontainebleau.
The Count and Countess de la Marche & the
wife, as well as the Duke de Penthievre,
and the Countess de Lamballe. The Council
in rendering it to be able to remain from the Court
and to render itself the Parliament and all the Court
will return to Versailles. Madame the
Countess de Provence remained alone with her
House to fight at Choisy; this
Princess departed from there Saturday 13th. Yesterday, without
all pomp, and according to the Orders of his Sisters
Majesty which had been suspended because of the
Royal Banquet where the Sovereign and the
Princes and Princesses who had been at
Fontainebleau and the most the Dukes & Peers
de Chartres, and the Bourbons. There one sees the
Days for the Illuminations at Versailles
The fire which was made in the Park the day before
Yesterday, succeeded very well.
