H. St. Dom. No 2

Paris, January 19, 1784.

One learns from L'Orient,
that the anxiety there is at its height,
concerning the Vessels from India
which are not arriving, and also about
the Subject of the thunderbolt, endured by
M. de Suffren at the end of June.

According to the Letters from Brittany, one
thinks seriously about making navigable
the Rivers of the Province, and the
communication from St Malo, will be
opened very soon. One must
also occupy oneself with the Province of
Cornisie, which fell into the River
of Brest. It is equally a Question
of making a Port at Isle à Bas, near
Paimpœuf, Bishopric of Treguier, and
of St Brieux. The Port is entirely made,
but the approaches to it are difficult.
