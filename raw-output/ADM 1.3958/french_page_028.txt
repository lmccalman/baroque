Some further Particulars of the
French Navy 1770.

Brest. There was the 20th. of December thirteen different
       Ships in the different Docks, three for building, six
       had Ships for & two taking out new Planking, one taking
       Stuff enough from the Port for all the Ships.
       & three new Ships building - 
       The Ships from the Minedau Mountains
       are all here -
       They are besides Shipbuilding yard, Several Ships
       afloat, viz. Ships, two of the 3d. Roubards, of which
       many already condemned. The rest of the Service
       are very Serviceable. There are Gates for 2 Stone
       Bason or about 14 Ships. Number D. Ships of the Line
       laid by amounted to 90 Ships -
       ready to Sail, four of that, at the Port Bottles, viz.
       The Royal Louise, 110, La Bretagne 110, The Union 90,
       & the Brilliant 64, Palmix Brilliant, Drinkes, Devote, Holdin & Sea
       Brunswick, Content of 64. Northumberland &c.
