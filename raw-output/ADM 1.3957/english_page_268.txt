                                                    R 20 July -

                    Paris the 30th July 1770.

                    Your letters from Paris

speak to me only of Chancelleries and Agitation in
the other Departments.

   They report from Toulon, that the new Armament
is ready, and for for Rio Maio of Vessels and
                         Cadiz     [crossed out: and all is]
   Likewise State of December, [crossed out: I not of that it]
makes no Affairs.

   That d'Aiguillon does Marvels in
Bretagne, to restore the manner of the Nobles
for the King.

   The great Deputation of Parlement will go to
render Marie at Versailles, the King pricked by
Curiosity, has had brought the Representations, the
replies, having encountered the Supplications, [Jus]
Expositions, has made the following Response:

   "After the Decree that you have rendered the 2nd of this
Month, and which I have [enreg], I should not have against
You, my Resolution is taken, to receive none
who reports the least of the Officers them to the Execution
of my Letters Patent of the 27th June last.
I forbid you, under penalty of Disobedience,
to assemble yourselves, and I permit you to the
Duke d'Aiguillon, to make known to you my
Rights and Prerogatives attached to the Peerage."

   After this Response the King named the [2ds]
to the Deputies, all declaring to them the Day
that it will go to Versailles at 4 Hours tomorrow.
