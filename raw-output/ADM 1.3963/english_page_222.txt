                                        21 Janv 1777 No 9.

                    Paris the 20. December 1776.

                            The letters from Toulon
of the 18th. state, that all is tranquil there,
with the exception of the ordinary
work begun.
    They are arming two Ships at
Havre, of 18. Cannons each one,
and they have loaded them with Artillery,
with all sorts of Munitions
of War, with Wheat, Porches, &c,
for the Provisioning
of our Colonies. One of these
Ships has departed, by which
more than a Hundred Officers, were
embarked; the other will be
loaded, likewise; but they
say that they will not find themselves there
