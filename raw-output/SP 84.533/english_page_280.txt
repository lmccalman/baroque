N° 29

Paris the 5th November 1775.

There is talk of an imminent
great Movement in the Navy to
exercise both the [Emplanis] and the Sailors.
A Promotion in the Navy should appear
these days, several Officers of this
Department, who find themselves under the law of
[dictionnaire] opinion, going back to the time of
Duke de Paslin, will reenter and regain
their Rank.

The New Parliament has [élague] the
men and the interdicted, and men of the old
Parliament of the King has made the oath
of all of the King, and the oath of the Subjects,
and the tone is low and complete. Those Gentlemen
[cales] have signed the Protestation, which is
already printed, moreover it is sent to
all the Parliaments of France, where they are
so that it will not even delay in leaving,
and that by the Hand of Rousseaus.

The New Parliament of Rennes has
ordered to render Justice, without awaiting
St. Martin, but from this St. Martin
to see what will happen in the Parliament
of Paris, and accordingly, it will judge
what it can do.

That one will not be a Parliament, because
it will not hold by its institution. The
Most Ancient Sovereign Council of the
