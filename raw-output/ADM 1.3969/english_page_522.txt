Bethune

Paris, January 2, 1815.

The letters from Vienna contain nothing new, some brigades of priests, [Brissels], sometimes to [Tais], sometimes deaths, formed the substance. [Rejouroit] that the Emperor is not yet disposed to leave for the Low Countries, that with his campaign equipment and his [Euvre] would already have arrived.

Nothing comes out of the War Offices, which is [ennip] that one believes of a near conclusion, to the point that many persons expect that there will not even be an Army of Observation, and that the money destined for this object will be employed for the remounts of the Cavalry:
