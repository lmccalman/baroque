B. 20 Estamay.


Paris the 9th of February 1789.

At present here, people speak only
of Russia, and the Porte. The signed
Convention consists of Three Articles.
By the first the Porte recognizes
the occupation of Crimea, or Caban;
of the Isle of Taman, and consents to the
reunion in perpetuity of these Countries to
the Empire of Russia.
In the Second, the Two contracting
Parties confirm the Treaties
and anterior Conventions of 1774,
18, and 79, as well as the Treaty of
Commerce of 1783, with the exception
of Articles three and four of the
explanatory Convention of 10 March
1779, concerning the Tartars, which
are annulled, Russia having, and
guarantees the Porte the Possession,
