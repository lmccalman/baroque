19 11 March 1782

Paris the 27 February 1783.

        The last Letters from
Cadix say, that Mr de la Fayette and the
Prince of Nassau went to Gibraltar
where they will have been very well received.
The Crews of Mr d'Estaing of which
we Speak, will go to Toulon and will return
soon himself to Paris.

        It is believed that the definitive Peace
Treaties will not be concluded at the earliest
until the month of July next.
    The Dutch have distinguished themselves in
a Third Part of the Loan,
to have by means of our Conditions
through Mediation.

        A new Loan will be made here
of 20 to 30 Millions, by Way of
Lottery.

        A Council Decree is going to appear,
which suspends the Payment of Bills
of Exchange, drawn from our Colonies of
America, and of India, for the
