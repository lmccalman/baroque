Paris November 8, 1763.

Extract from a Letter from Messrs dated October 30, 1763.

A Decree has been rendered today on the Rules and which of the King's Men which forbids all Ecclesiastics of whatever Order and Conditions they may be, from spreading or reading in the Acts of the Assembly of the Clergy, as being contrary to the Service of the King, a Retraction to the said Letters and others from the King, enjoins moreover all Priests and Curates to conform in the Administration of the Sacraments to the Canons received in the Church, Forbids them from contravening directly or indirectly, the Declaration of 1754, which orders absolute Silence on the Subject of the Bull Unigenitus. The Parliament will be printed and posted.

By a particular Decree an Assembly of the Chambers has been indicated for November 26 next, to Rule on the substance of the said Acts of the Clergy.

The Letters from your three Ports teach us nothing new.

A vessel from Bayonne has established itself in Marseille with mediocre cargo of Cod.
