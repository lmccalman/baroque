Madrid the 17th March 1783.

The first Troops of the
Camp of St. Roch, have begun to
set themselves in march the 9th of the Current month,
and must continue until the entire
evacuation of the Camp, with Two Days
interval, so as not to interfere with
them in their March, and in the Lodgings.
And the Letters from Cadiz of the 9th of the Current month
report, that the French Troops,
had already embarked there, for their
Garrisons, to proceed to their respective
Destinations, leaving there a quantity of
Sick in the Hospitals.
Twelve French Ships, loaded
with Provisions and Munitions, which were
destined for the great Expedition under
Orders of the Count d'Estaing, continue
their Voyage to America for the
French Islands.
