266.

                                                R. 2. July

                        Toulon the 17th June 1770.

                                                The Count of command
by Mr de Ponvert, having received orders to put
[Courtier de bois] with his brigade of battalion and all the
workers of the Artillery out of condition to serve.

     The Chevalier de Vignole with the detachment of two
Battalions is currently awaiting fair weather, to
embark for Calvi; the Battalions of the March have been left
Grenoble, and the Strength of Each Battalion is on this
Day of Spain equal.

     The Marquis de Maillebois has passed here here while
going to [Bryencken] & Rochelle; the whole [baere]
commanded; by Mr de Gales, the other Commanders have not yet
been named; I do not find them [dient]
of Israel. Lieutenant Colonel Peret has come here
new Regulations is destined for the war
against the [turques mattes].

     They have sent for 25 [canelles] the Mr of
[fromes] orders to load them with provisions for
the army.

     The part of the Regiment [d'ouvenant] that they want to make
at the Camp of Rouen.

     The King, it is in this tower Grand Master
of Malta, and I would like the [Baille] de la Rochegyre
by putting him in the place of General of the Cavalry
that same place. Awaiting all the News.


                        Paris the 25th June 1770.

                                                The Chevalier of the
[Carions] of the Swiss Guard of the Indies, who should serve
currently as Colonel, has already completed the time, at which
that he of them holds, his change is probable.
Others are expected to see very often.

                                                        Lery
