Garden from the 14th of March 1750 until the following 24th of April.
The 14th. the embellishment of Princes of the mountains making very
pretty tours, the weeping willows form shade cabinets
very pleasant, the dryness of the weather and the continual wind
leaves much to be desired.
The 15th. I had 4 fruit trees planted in the quarter garden
as well as along the courtyard wall, and had a great number of plants planted in
the flower beds, and placed 6 benches around the basins.

By the King of Marutte de Kervert

The weather continues dry and cold, I have watering done in the morning and
Evening all the young trees to preserve and strengthen them,
I am busy having a second bed constructed
beside the first to extend my fruits.

On a Thursday, I have a hundred young Obelisk planted
having feared the frost I had them worked yesterday.
I also make two holes for the onions, for the beans are well
advanced, and the peas have emerged. One could follow this account
This week we finished planting 70 [gales] lettuces emerged from the
hotbeds and the beds are beginning to grow very well.
One is very tired with this weather, and everyone suffers in the garden.
