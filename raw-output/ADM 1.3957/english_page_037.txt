32.
                                        M. du Parny

                    Paris the 22 January 1770.

                    The Position of Major

General of the Marine at Brest, vacant by the
retirement of M. de Maillard, has been given to the
command of the Islands of France and of
Bourbon, is given to M. d'Hector Captain
of Ship, who commanded the Sphinx
most recently. This is always News
that must be told to you.

    A Letter from l'Orient of the 14th says that
the Ships which must leave from there presently,
for Bengal, and which will depart within three
Weeks, are the Brisson, the Mars, and the Harpaye,
the others are not for the Month of March.

    The Syndics & Directors of the Company
of the Indies, have in consequence of the Assembly
at which I attended, and there is a General
Assembly of the Shareholders convened for
Tomorrow Monday. One feels how much this
Assembly must be interesting for
the Shareholders, of which that which has passed
until now on this Object, announces
that there will be Woods which will perhaps make
an Epoch in the Fate of this Company.

    We have seen M. Bellecombe; who is
General here; more in a State than anyone
to appraise at their just Value The
