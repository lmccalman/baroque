Considering that if in times of troubles and confusion, or by a momentary abuse of authority one has endeavored to accredit some of the maxims that said Edict contains, they have been abandoned subsequently by the Sovereigns, or followed by so many disorders, that assembled in a body of laws, they can only hide some foreign project, to multiply the charges of the State, spread discouragement in the Magistrature, attack the safety of the Citizens, extinguish the love of the Fatherland, and cast consternation in all minds.

That it is in circumstances of this nature that the Nation, whose rights the Edict attacks, has assembled several times under the authority of the Sovereign to bring its complaint to the foot of the Throne, and to make heard there its just grievances.
