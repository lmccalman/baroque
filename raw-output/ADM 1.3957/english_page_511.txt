610.

One could still conclude from this
that one would see Reus or Gibraltar [Dujunas]
They left Barcelona, that 4 Battalions
Walloon and Spanish Guards are on the
march to quarter in the Environs
of this City, in Place of the 2 Battalions,
which have taken the Route to Port St. Maurice;
and that all forces are being advanced, and another
convoy of all nature for its use
for Magazine.
I am informed from Cadiz, that General
Don Martin continues to garrison the City
and its Environs with the new
Batteries, and that the Embarkations
for America are frequent.
Until now they have called the Convoy of the
English with the Retaking of this Squadron
which appears so protected by a
Squadron of English but under certain
Conditions which are still unknown;
one would believe however that they are
not at all advantageous to the Court of London.
Several Camps have been marked out, to
encamp troops in the
Environs; it is the most fertile of the
landings; these Camps will be fortified
with Trenches and Batteries.
