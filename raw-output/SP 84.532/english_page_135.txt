Private Letter

Paris, January 23, 1775

It appears that the
Count of Monteynar has the hope of making
many changes in the various
Infantry Regiments, both with regard to
[rendues] all the Ministry of the Duke of
Choiseul, there is no doubt above all, that he
will make many Reforms among the
Colonels, where there is such a great number of
[veuformes], and especially a Regiment of immense
The Economic Spirit and the new
[Recrutois] of State, and the Favorites, lead him
very suitable to enter into the Views
of Arrangements and Reductions,
of the Regiments of his Department.
It is already said that he has stopped the Regiment
of retired Officers, to whom the Duke of
Choiseul had granted in place of
Pensions to be taken from the Royal Treasury,
the 32 Infantry Officers in active
duty continued on the ordinary pay
of two rx. By this Means these
were paid regularly, while
the Pensions left to the others,
long in arrears of nearly three years
would be to Account in the future & be rendered
Account of the Economies made from such a
Reform;
