6.) As it may be thought proper to encourage
the peopling His Majesty's new Acquisitions
in North America, I judge it of my Duty
to represent that many Individuals, which if
not speedily well managed will give a Proof of my
Zeal and of no Wise Will.

7.) The People that are most versed in these Affairs
with whom I have made it my Business to
converse, give me the Strongest Assurances,
that if the Inclinations of the Government be
publickly known as I have said, and that the
Germans (like the Swedes and Dutch, and Suisse, like
People even) will come without the Government
being at the whole Expence of transporting them.

8.) The thing being transacted entirely by the Merchants
is subject to great Abuses, & scarce has prompted
several of them to cheat these poor People in
the Choice of the Shipping, in the Provisions on
the Voyage, afterwards not properly honoured on
Board for so long a Voyage, the Bad Effects of
which have cost the Lives of many of those poor
People, and I have seen numerous Instances
of Deceits of this Kind.
