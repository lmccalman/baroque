To Versemy N°4

Paris, January 4, 1773.

Our letters from Brest
and from Rochefort speak only of the
sensation caused there by the disgrace of the
two Ministers.

Word comes from L'Orient of the 29th that the
[l'aifence] of Bizarre and the Duchaffaut are the
only ships ready to be armed, and that the
repairs are advancing very slowly at Brest.

A letter from Le Havre of January 5 reports
that Sieur Bateillan de Limourin has
departed, that Sieur de Rogiual [l'aîgeureux]
was embarked on two large merchant
ships, and that they were to set
sail the next day.

Different maritime notices are beginning
to appear in the Gazettes, one sees
among others that at [le Hautburg du Cap frédini],
and the others successively, which
will inform them of their true
destination.

What occupies all Paris today
is [Si Kiliani] or where the new
Ministers are, the response has not yet
appeared.
