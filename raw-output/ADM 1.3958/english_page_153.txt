201.                                    48th year

                                    Paris, April 3, 1773.

The Letters from Toulon
of the 23rd say, that the Arming of the 3 frigates
of which one has already spoken, is the only Movement
of the Port, but there will soon be others,
if one takes it into one's head to quarrel with us.
One reports from Brest of the 27th, that one has sold
at public auction for the profit of the shipowners
& of the State on the 24th, a vessel taken by a [Bon Veant].
to go to the Cape, to carry to our Colonies the
certificate of the Peace. This is the Only
News from this Port, one scarcely touches among the
crews of the same everything is absolute-
ly dead, and one sees there only ships
in port & tranquil, & one knows
Only the course of Commerce of
Individuals.
There comes out absolutely nothing, neither large, nor of
movement, nor of any Sort; neither at Rochefort
nor at Lorient. We have no news
not of their movements.
It appears from here, that the King has [déciminé]
yesterday a Position of Physician in recompense
the Devotion, that Mr de Montagnard
Physician in chief, of the navy who resides here
although he had been sent to [leortes & Etat],
that for Regency of the University, & one says
finally for having done what he could that.
