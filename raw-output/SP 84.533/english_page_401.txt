N°. 104.

Private Letter

Paris, December 22, 1778.

There are some
Infallibly those who wish to maintain their [affin]
in the New Company of the Indies;
they have decided, that given the current
Difficulties of the men of the Indies; the
new Establishment could only take place
in a time when these Difficulties
would be less great.

Indeed this Establishment is regarded
as a [Meuble], well suited to
bring value to the Shares of the
Voyages Fund, the Public occupations of this
Important object, and interests all
those who have acquired some Annuities and
in this Part, instead where experience
can take the place of great Forces.

[Mr de la Lande] has been appointed to
visit all the Voyages of the Comp.y;
than the men, the [batifs] & this object.;
but despite everything, they do not wish to
acknowledge Mr de Boynes for us
to find by this Affairs of the Indies
necessary for the Reestablishment of this
a Public Establishment of [Vittet], and that
is not [neçente] for complete.
