19 di Marly No 12

Brest the 18th April 1783

They are occupied here with disarming
the Vessels returned from Cadix, which are
the Royal Louis the Majestueux, the Terrible,
the Invincible, the Bretagne, the Robuste, the
Bien-aimé, the Sceptre, the Diadême, and the
Frigates the Engageante, the Gentille, the
Richmond and the Crescent.

There are three Barges of the King which have been
chartered to transport Military personnel and provisions are
commanded by Messrs de Brugneau, de
Rosily, and de Kerhues. When the Flutes
have returned, there will be for a long time four
[Embargues] by these Barges, which will be
commanded by Lieutenants of Vessel.

The 9 Thousand two Hundred Men of the
Troops of the Royal Corps of the Marine, have
gone to Landerneau to take
possession of the Convent of the Cordeliers which
served as hospital during the War.
The King having made great Repairs, and
this Convent being moreover very vast, and
very well built, it will serve the Corps as Barracks
and a Quarter, one cannot place better
and more conveniently for the Troops of
