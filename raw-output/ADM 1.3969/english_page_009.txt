the Corsair approached, & fired a second cannonball at the stern of
the Ship. Being then within voice range, the Dutch Captain
remonstrated to him that he was neutral; & coming from Grenada
with a cargo of sugar; but far from this response having induced the
Corsair to withdraw, he sent immediately a boat
filled with armed men, & a prize Captain,
to seize the vessel. The Captain & the Crew
were made Prisoners; the Pirates seized all
the papers they found, & mine were included
among them; I was left on board with my Negro & the Dutch
Pilot; all the rest of the Crew was conducted on
board the Corsair, which continued its cruise.

The Associates of sieur Gibb in the arming of this Cor-
sair, are Smugglers from Jersey & Guernsey,
who, having quit their trade, have raised the saber since
England declared war on the Dutch. The Crew
was composed of Fishermen from Mevagissey; small
Village of Cornwall, where there is done each year a
great fishing of Sardines. The prize Captain was a
Norman married at Guernsey; it had already been twenty-four
hours that he treated me as a Prisoner, when he was willing
to inform me that the King of England was irritated
against the Dutch, & that, to correct them, he had
declared war on them for about a week; that
in truth he had excepted from hostilities the Ships coming
from Grenada with cargoes; but that the Cor-
sairs did not trouble themselves with the proclamation, because
