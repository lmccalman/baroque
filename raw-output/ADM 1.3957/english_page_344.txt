The two Counselors of Vannes have been
sent back from the [Chaleine] of Commende to
that of Pierre Saince.

The Parliament of Brittany is Suspended
since the 26th, after having sent to Paris
a Deputy and a Justificatory Letter, in the form
of Remonstrances, and Has ordered the
Chambers of Questions to adjourn to all
the Officers, and instructed not to solicit the
Return of the two Magistrates.

The Marshal of Richelieu has calmed the
spirits in Bordeaux, and has done what he
could do, the bodies in order, those
two of Rouen, and of Yvennes, have been
more two worldly Churches than one
had hoped. His Travels, his Commissions,
they have put Water in their Wine.

That of Rouen, Has contented itself with
Reading Remonstrances on the occasion
of the Parliament of Brittany, and of Yvennes.

The Parliament of Metz has burned a decree
in April, by which it persists in its Appeal
against Mr de Calonne.
