Yesterday we received Letters from
Constantinople of the 30th of April, they
communicate News of the most
Considerable in that Capital,
one expects Peace, without exposing oneself too much.
According to the Letters from Rome of the 2nd
of September, affairs are always
in the same [Parell]; this is the current [Barleme]
of that Court.

Our Letters from Bautzen of the 3rd of this Month,
say, that all is generally enough
tranquil in that City; but there are
many Sick among our troops,
and the Inhabitants suffer greatly, especially
because the Heat has been more
considerable, but fortunately,
these Illnesses are not
fatal.

L.B
