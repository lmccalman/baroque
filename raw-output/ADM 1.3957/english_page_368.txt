113.
4 21 Sept N° 1

Paris the 27th September 1770.

It is reported from Toulon
on the 8th, that the Regiments of Penthièvre and la
Marck were embarked; that the greater Part
of the Companies of Cerfs, of the Regiments of
Forêts, Quercy, Costelles, and Belzunce will be
transported there within a few days; and the Same Visits
that we have made known.

Nothing is heard from our Province [devenir]
Havrie, nor from Corsica since the 8th August. Nor
is anything heard from the other Ports.

The Parliament of Rennes has arrived
with that of Brittany.

The 18 Chambers summoned with the Officers
of the Parliament of Guyenne, will assemble
on the 28th at 9 o'clock in the morning of the King and present to the
Gentlemen that Mr. Le Duc has convened the
Province for this purpose.

It is alleged the 18 Chambers of the Parliament
of Toulouse are announced very soon to the Copies
and Registers for the same Ceremonies. These
Registers are being made at this time. That of the
18 Chambers of Dijon has not been made, and
yet it has done no less than the others.

The Parliament of Rouen with the annulment
against d'Aiguillon on the subject of the Attentat on
the 10th August; the Duc and Peer at the time having been
deposed by Mr. la Duc. The assemblies, and
the Instruction of the Minister against him.
