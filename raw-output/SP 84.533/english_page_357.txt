Breslau 9 November 1758.

They are working to form
Battalions for the Navy, whose
foundation will be drawn from 40 Infantry
Regiments, so much per Company.

Messrs. de Boynes come here in the Spring
they hope that the Town will be more useful
to the Corps of the Marine, than that of the
Duke of Broslin.

The frigates the Zephyr, and the Vigilant from
St. Malo [alliers] to France, they were made
to return, to haul like the 4 other
frigates, at Libourne, they are inspected by the
Commissioners named for the Course.

La Belle Poule is ready to depart
to transport Messrs. de Lannuy to
the Isle of Bourbon.

They will distinguish from the Foreigner, they
learn that he put in at Cherbourg.

Mr. de la Borde has the rank of Squadron
Chief, with three Thousand Livres of
Pension, and the Command of
Bougonnais.

There are several Regiments assembled
