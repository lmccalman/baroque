Paris the 25th March 1775.

The King who had
gone last Sunday to [Meutes],
returned Tuesday to Versailles,
to receive there the King of Sweden, who
has finally taken [Cyprus], his Principal
absolutely today.

The King has done him the honors of Paris
Everyone is vying to show him the
most beautiful [rebellions] that one supposes
to find, a complete Service in
Porcelain from the Manufacture of
Sèvres, and a magnificent [Portsmouth]
in tapestries.

The Count of Maillebois had
requested a private Audience
with the King, which was refused him; he has
left to retire to his lands near
Chalons, where he will work, it is said, on a
Memoir against the Marshal of
Broglie.
