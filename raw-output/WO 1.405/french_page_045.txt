sessions of something we we made use of a Arabesque

on board. The information they gave us was more

all agreed to every point of the same intelligence.
I had beside traité Colours and left but one or
two hands on deck making my language a mixture
of Tarté and Portuguese. The substance of what I
collected from them is as follows.

"There have been within these last three or
four weeks, eleven sail of which three is the English
Lord viz: 2 very large Ships of 100 Guns 2 of 98,
and 6 of from 70 to 60 - and 3 Frigates. 44 that I
have there were also near two there without
Clothes, and the ports adjacent. The troops were
all embarked on board the fleet which sailed for
Cadiz, as supposed of 8. 9. 9. 9. & and I really and
he got under way. The latter a Frigate which was
full of Troops. At this time there remain in
Terrol but two Ships of war - a 74 Gun Ship and
a Frigate - and but very few Military - all of
which that could be collected having been
embarked on board the force for Cadiz."
