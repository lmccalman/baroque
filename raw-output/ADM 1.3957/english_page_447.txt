525                    R 2 Nov. 1770

                        Private Letter

                                        Paris the 6th of October 1770.

                                                    There are in Marseilles
Letters from Constantinople, and from
the Dardanelles via Ras, the first
state that the German had arrived
in the Capital, that the Porte was making
great Preparations there, and that they feared
a Revolt there.
        Those from the Dardanelles indicate
that Mr. de Tott had completed his
new Batteries, and had
established his Garrison there, but
that they would not hold against the Russians,
under the Command of 2 Prussian Officers
who had been seen Passing there, among whom
was named the Director of Crevres;
and that they always Plan
the Strait [Rhinel]
        The Russian Chief has [desespere] 30 Thousand Men
on the Coasts of Europe and Asia.
        They had taken from this Vessel which
was bringing provisions to the Lazaretto.
