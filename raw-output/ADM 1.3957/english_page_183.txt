294.

A Council Decree was published the day before yesterday which orders every Pastor to Recognize [de la dernière Rochegarde] of the Cevennes, called the Sieur de Brueis, currently in Paris; that when they could make a Voyage, he could [détriplement] only Recognize him at 50 per Cent.

A Council Decree appears which extends until the 1st of next October the Liquidation of the Treasury Notes of the Colony of Louisiana, and [en affaire] a renewal [le demèle] which must be made of [un heur hariginal], converted to make the Payment thereof.

Another concerns the Capitation and the Reinforcement of Munitions in the Bishopric of Lyon, and a Declaration which annuls Debts to prevent to different Treasuries and Payers, and to [d'hereditaires] of [Termes quittancables].

The King's [Réfuté] is delayed until the end of this Month, while waiting the Duke of Aiguillon will provide a Memorial to justify him, [le Jaurat] having worked on it, I have not seen all the Judges. Nothing is known.
