Letter from a new Correspondent
from Brest of the 19th April 1735.

We only had to wait for the
Wind to depart: It is said that if we
leave within 3 or 4 Days, we have
nothing to fear in the Crossing;
but that if we delay further, the
English will be ready, and that there will be
Blows if we encounter each other.

The Iterative Orders that are received
are to depart, and to prepare here for
the Defense of the Coasts: There is talk of fortifying
the Round and Long Islands in our
Roadstead: By Means of Batteries on
these two small Islands, we will be able to
prevent the anchorage of Enemy
Vessels in the Roadstead even if
they cross the Narrows; They have
already appointed Officers who must
command the old Batteries,
