16.5 Novembre N°2

                        Paris the 27th October 1770.

     The Letters from Toulon
of the 23rd say, that there are no Orders
of departure, but only preparations
for War. The Vessels which have departed
up to the Pierre St Louis are disarmed;
M. de Bouvac is expected who will command
the Provence, the Joyeuse, and Ste Alexandrine.

     The Mégonnie, commanded by Mr
d'Aimonis must go to the Dardanelles with
the Count de Choiseul on board. Six Galleys
from Naples, arrived there in 8 Days:
Its Receiver of customs at Toulon.

     The Frigate armed at Bordeaux, commanded
for the Count d'Amblimont, under the Title
of Entraineur, by order of the Duke de Praslins
is in Order to arm at Rochefort The
Dauphin Royal of 70; the Couronne of 80;
the Brest the Antre of 36; and the Fortune of 30;
it is thought that it is to transport
into our Colonies Troops that will be
taken from the Isle of Ré.

     The Isle of Corsica is provided for The
Next Campaign for The Account of
Vines; has returned to Rochefort.

     The Intendant of The Navy puts all
his Care to restore and improve the Port
