                    Brest the 3rd January 1773.

                    The Charter that our
two Ministers has made and still makes
the greatest Sensation.
    The Orders given in this Port by
the Duke of Praslin, have been confirmed
by the Duke of la Vrillière on behalf of the
King, for War, the Colonies and the
Exchanges, and have always worked in
this Port as usual.
    We are very impatient to learn
who we will have for Minister, and
Mr.Md de Clugny was former Sr. Bour
in War.
    They have just made a tableau of all
the Officers of this Department, and the
Men, that [tu on] want to arm in
our Vessels and Frigates, both [aux]
as in the future, without excepting any,
in all the State and in the Bureaux
all in a State to go to Sea, there are
many [si] who have been given founded
Retirements, which [en néan] rendered.
    Our Chief is at Court, but he has little
Credit, which is very bad for
the Subordinates.
