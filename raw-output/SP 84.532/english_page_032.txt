Private Letter

Paris, December 22, 1770.

Everything is up in the air
regarding the fall of the Dukes of Choiseul and d'Aiguillon
and one still does not understand how one could have
wanted this without having suitable Subjects in view
to replace them. The [Etiquettes] do not
end there, but all the news
reduces to saying that M. d'Aiguillon and that is to say
the most faithful Friend that the Triad has had and
[à tout l'envie d'opposition à renonce Son
principes de Port en dernier]. It is
nothing less than impossible that the amiable,
the playful Cardinal de Bernis return
to the head of Foreign Affairs;
at least the Flow of Nomination makes one
believe that it must be someone like him, or the
Count of Broglie. The one of La Vrillière
does not speak of ever being in the
King's favors.

Three Princes of the Blood had the Courage
to make the strongest Representations
to the King about the Uprightness of the
Intentions of Parliament, and about the
Disorders that can occasion and the
Discontent that breaks out in several
