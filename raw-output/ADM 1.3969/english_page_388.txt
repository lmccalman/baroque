It is written from Caen that there have been
rather lively murmurs on the occasion
of the wheat. The neighboring [Prêts] are filled
with this Commodity, thus it will be easy
to provision the City well.

A Memoir has been received at Versailles or
rather a Manifesto from the Emperor,
containing the exposition of his just claims
upon a part of the principality of
Liège, and even some charges to
our account. Which joined to his demands
[petits] to the Dutch, is nonetheless
considerable. The Emperor says [les
lèvent ses politiques], to maintain three
hundred thousand men, without them
bringing him any profit.
