Baniowski, besides the considerable
Loss of Men that we had

Expense of which it was for the
State of France, the King made it
understood, that one could never
sufficiently conquer it, to put it
safe from the Infidelities of the
English at the first War; that these
would not fail to seize it,
and that by the Event one would
have worked only for Them.
Moreover, the State of the
finance of this Colony, that is
to say of the Isle de France,
