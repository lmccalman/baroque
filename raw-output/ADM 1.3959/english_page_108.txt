The Prince of Condé will return from
Chantilly, the King will march, and the
troops to the Bridge of St. Cloud and
his Palaces, while waiting for this
passage finally to be completed.

His Princes who are always the same,
and it appears that they do not retreat
from their pretensions, they are
meanwhile always excluded from the
Court, without that they would [se grossisse
abandonnerois] from their Courtiers.

The Count of la Marche [tant bridée] at
the Court, has there the greatest
Ascendancy, and makes them do there what he
wants.

The Parliaments of Rouen, Pau,
Grenoble and of Besançon; revolted.
The Parliaments [rebellerions], proceed
as if there had been so finished that they
were Established.

The Chancellor always enjoyed the
highest Consideration at the Court:
The Ministers are two unusually
secretly united.
