Brest, January 10, 1778.

On the 10th there arrived here
47. a Courier from Versailles for this Department,
from the Chancellery, the Count of Maurepas, and
Mr. the Count of Sartine; the same Courier brought
upon departing other
Graces, Namely, Brevets in the order
of St. Louis for 9 Officers, and nine
Brevets made for the Competition, according to the
Wish of the Royal conventions Beginning,
there were obtained 18 Crosses of St. Louis.

[On Cheute] the Silence, The matter has for
great object that of the Crews of [Raderest];
and transporting them to the Indies.

They still speak of a very
near day, where could be the Complement
of the Council for the Closure, and for
other measures.

We know by the Count of [Chawon];
who was lent to us by the King;
2 vessels from France have arrived here.

The Crew has reported; that there
was lacking from the Isle of France no
News
