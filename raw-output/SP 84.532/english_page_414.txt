A magistracy acquired for a price
of money, could govern despotically the State under the name of the
Sovereign; against the will of the
Sovereign, the clergy, the nobility, the
people would fall, without perceiving it, into the most
humiliating & the most harsh servitude.

This government would need,
to maintain itself, means as violent as that of the Turks, witness the
state inquisitors*, & the box where any
informer can in the shadow throw
his note with his accusation.

The citizen, without distinction of
rank nor of quality, would find
himself ceaselessly under the eyes of his masters, or of their ministers, whose
caprices would have to be respected, &
whose wills would have to be feared: their gazes
would penetrate even into the
interior of houses; the slightest murmur would become a crime of State;
the groans of oppressed innocence would be punished as
