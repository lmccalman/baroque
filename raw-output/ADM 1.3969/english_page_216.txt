Sp. Ch. Sept.

Paris the 5th September 1782.

The 8th Current between noon
and one O'clock, the Plenipotentiaries [Plant]
assembled at Mr. de Vergennes's the definitive
Treaty between Spain and England
was Signed at Versailles, and following that the one
between France and England.
In the Morning of the same Day the
Treaties of the United States had been
Signed in Paris by the respective
Commissioners. Peace was same Day
of the Plenipotentiaries of Peace between
Great Britain and Holland,
had been Signed likewise, by
the Plenipotentiary Ministers of the
Two Powers, [aux] of the States General
having received full Powers to
conclude everything, would have very much wished
to Sign also their definitive Treaty at
the same Time, but the Duke of Manchester
excused himself on the Lack of Instructions & from Court
on this Subject.
