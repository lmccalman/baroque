the 22 October No 3

Private Letter

                                        Paris the 22nd October 1770.

                                        In combining all
that I hear said for and
against the Enemies I find myself
deciding on one side or the other.
        We are fortunately making
Dispositions and Equipments
in our Ports; we are sending
Relief to our Colonies, but it
is claimed that all this tends only to be
Precautionary Measures.
        The Spanish Navy is being armed
quite slowly, which will end up
mounting to approximately 60 Ships
of the Line, 8 or 9 [prisons], and more
than 20 Million in the Coffers.
For Us, we have 60 of them, but
they are not all in a State to Serve,
and for Millions, none.
We do not lack Sailors at all,
Spain even less so, but
