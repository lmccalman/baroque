4 January

Paris the 5th January 1770.

The letters from Brest

inform us that there came to the Islands of the Cape and that

of Martinique and Guadeloupe only the army here.

It is reported from Toulon that there were leaving [quarton

d'armemants] at this moment here, that this offered him

the facility, to go [appointement] the officer

to cruise on the Coasts of Corsica.

They hold ready in this Port 7 M of the Infantry

and 12 icy troops to be armed in an instant

and lead them to Barcelona.

A Courier from Paris arrived at Toulon,

coming from Mahon; reported that a Russian

Vessel of 74 Cannons, Equipped with their Crews

has entered Mahon to await there the Rest

of the Squadron. All Mahon is the Point of

Reunions.

It appears according to the reports being received, that the

Spanish Governments, are sending off the

Bordeaux for the [franceschales], which must

have not been to the Orient, but that they have

loaded at Royan the Sr J.Battalion of the Royal

[Contois] Regiments, forming 650 men. The first

Battalions is already in the Indies.

There has already departed from the Squadron of Count Rhe

of Spain for the East Indies, Namely the

number of 650 men of the Dragoon Regiments
