April 15, 1781.

                        Toulon March 20, 1783.

                                            The Regiment of
Comte de Provence will arrive here from Corsica in
this Port, it will [emportera] the day after tomorrow for
Marseille and will continue its route [apperant aux]
new buildings and the Recruits belonging to
Madame the Countess of Provence.
     The Regiment of Quercy, which is here
attached to the Navy and will transport it;
and that of Languedoc which is here, will perform
the service of the Port.
     The [Régente] the Atalante went to
fixed post this Morning, [Allegre] passing there and
the Regiment will come next Monday.
     The Vessel of the King of Denmark
departed yesterday morning and [arlene] the Flag
of the King of Sweden, when the Atalante
was in the Roads, the Officers who
command it, rendered to them Corps for Corps.
     Messrs de Bougainville [nous commandent tant],
[reçoitdes] the latest Instructions,
to go on Cruise.
     The [armeta Canis] will be [elle], from
Marseille tomorrow. [Du la Lannique]
[nous iles] of our Port.
