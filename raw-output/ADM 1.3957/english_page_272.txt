18.24 July

Toulon the 9th of July 1770.

Barbary Corsairs
armed for in our Seas, having insulted that
[Réglé] in the Port of Antibes, two Neapolitan
Ships loaded with Wood for this Port, we have
received Order to arm one immediately the two
Frigates, [tandisque quatre pas] these Corsairs,
that we think to depart the wood of Penserres.
These 2 Chebecs are commanded by Messieurs
de Grefsmans, and de St André, and 2 [bay oste]
St. Segreux who the 2 Frigates, having among them
some [hausse], but it is not yet
ordered. [Crédi], until now 36 Vessels
armed in the Port, at the point of descent
to Offers.
The Chebecs of M. de Siles have gone to
Marseille, to take a Convoy of Merchant
Vessels, to escort them into the Levant,
the 2 [briguels] wait in the Roadstead of Hieres
for this Convoy to be able also to go.
The Songs continually and these Germans
to go, there are 22 leagues that Sr Fillet is charged
to learn something of the Operations
of M. de Bornes [desemblesoir], and the Consequences
of his Expedition.


Paris,
