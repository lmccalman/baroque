EDICT OF THE KING,

Establishing the creation of Superior Councils.

Given at Versailles in the month of February 1771.

Registered in Parliament.

LOUIS, BY THE GRACE OF GOD, KING OF FRANCE
AND OF NAVARRE: To all present & to come; GREETINGS.
It is only with the most profound regret that we have seen
the Officers of our Parliament of Paris give themselves over to a
disobedience equally condemned by the Laws, by their
oaths, by the public interest; erect as a principle the arbitrary
suspension of their functions; & finally openly attribute to themselves
the right to prevent the execution of our will: To color
their pretensions with a specious pretext, they have attempted to alarm
our subjects concerning their state, concerning their honor, concerning their properties,
concerning the very fate of the Laws which establish the succession to the
Crown, as if a regulation of discipline could have
