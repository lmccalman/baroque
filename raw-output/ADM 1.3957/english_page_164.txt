174.

The Governor Mister Miles Only
no right before the Village Chief, who
questioned them about their Nation, & why
they found themselves among them, they replied
that they were English, & that the Brigadier
had been Shipwrecked; they were locked up
in the Cabin. The Negroes made
visits & [envierent] that no
Service was done, the women, but, in
proposing to them, they advised them,
to kill the two Guards and to
Escape to the Portuguese, by
instructing them of their Route.
Mr. de Only sent one of these
Guards to go get Water,
as soon as he had left he secured the other
& when the one returned from the Mountain
he killed him too, and they left at once,
crossing the [Cherazze]
it was at [Mauves], it's the time when
We were sleeping, they saw no one
& by dint of walking, they encountered
a troop of Portuguese who brought them
to their [conduites] and to safety.
The Portuguese sent to these Negroes
such terrors of turquoise and
[Singlets], assuring that they ate all the
Negroes
