18 27 April N°1

Toulon the 30th March 1782.

The Ship the Lion of 64.

Convoys forming Part of the Squadron which
returns from Cadix, entered on the 28th of this Month
in our Roadstead. The Captain Mr Gilbert
declared that he left the Squadron at the Height
of Malaga. This Ship had on board Sick
Sick Sailors, and about 30. Men
of the Land Troops.

The Suffront of 74 Cannons, commanded
by Mr de Galabell, and forming Part of the
same Squadron, entered our Roadstead
on the 29th in the Evening, a Gust of Wind having separated
it from the Squadron (with Two Transport
Vessels, loaded with Troops) The Force
as well as the Two Vessels anchored
at the Hyères Islands.

The Lion and the Frigate the Flore, are preparing
to enter the Port, to work
on their disarmament. The Flore anchored
in our Roadstead on the 28th, she returns from

Marseille
