18.14.Aug.1821

Paris the 6th April 1770.

It is reported from Brest
on the 3rd April, that the Clérets de Durade, and the
Carriers were in the Roadstead, and were about to depart
for Bayonne, this is the only News from this
Port, until present.

Nothing New from the 29th we are
still not informed of anything about the Expedition to Tunis. Nothing
has been received from Corsica yesterday.

The Vessel the Zephire of 64 under the orders of
M. de la Clocte, has arrived at l'Orient.

It is learned from Pondicherry, that since the
Month that Monsieur MacLeane had arrived there,
he had not yet received a Letter from the English,
inhabitants in the North of the English Possessions
threatening them, which engages them in many
journeys to undertake and to choose the Best
[Malléons], who had no good welcome, and who can
return at any Moment.

The Sieur l'Espinaisse of the Company
of the Iron Crown, and the Countess of Barujières
at Bombay, attacked by the Malabars have just
been [élus les Portés] to the Coast and [est h'y ont rien].

You would have received so extensively. M.
Everigne, These Royal [Tournées] will finish
in a good [lendel].

M.P.
