It is reported from Boulogne, that the
Workers are occupied with demolishing
the Church of the Professed House of the
Jesuits.

The Marshal de Richelieu arrived
at Touze at the Departure of the Jesuits from
Nimes had soon what he did there.
He returned the following Month to Paris.

It is written from Nancy that the Parliament
has received Letters of Suspension to
register the Declaration which
authorizes the Seizure of the Goods of the
Jesuits. [Entourquence] the Ministers
seized by armed [Mairie] the
Houses of the Jesuits of Lorraine.
He took away the Religious as
to Prison at their homes.

The Duke d'Aiguillon is almost
[redevable] of his Credit for good, and
departs soon for Brittany.
To facilitate for Mr. de Villeneuve
the Acquisition of the Office of
Civil Lieutenant, the King [presse]
500 Thousand Livres on the Brevets of
[Roleurs], which is 600 Thousand; there will
remain
