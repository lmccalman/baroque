Toulon January 2, 1775.

I am ordered My dear Sir to [quelque 17] Vessels of the frigate the Protector, which are armed there, in very good condition, in first order, the action has passed into line [agance] of [Remontrage], four which are at Brest and Rochefort also Vessels Brilliant [aux deux] Vessel, of the Rhine, and of the Corvette which are in very poor condition.
     All the two Divisions Vessels would complete that they will be this Principal Minister if absolutely confirmed, the three others need Refittings which [demeurant] for little of all [l'abord]; the great that one cannot [longer] time to make use of them. These three Vessels are the [Contents], the Tonnant, and the Robuste.
     The two Frigates the [Plenniere] and the Legere, which are in the Roads, with the [Roque] ready since some Days, [m'attend m'offre] more than two Intentions to make oneself in War than the Instructions to [lister] that in the Moment the right to have them at Brest, they would be fortunately out to service in the Moment, where the Barbaras are always ready to [inquire] all the Vessels.
     It would be agreed by Minister to be [reconnements] to cause to fail Orders of [Sabonique] a Battery, commanded for the Captain Mething, because the Port of this Vessel has been very much against requested for this Rhine.
     All our Batteries are ready for some Days furnished with Cannons, and there is no more [eu au] of the [Colon], which one can call to carry in 24 hours.
                                                      Yours
