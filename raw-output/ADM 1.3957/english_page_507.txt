                                                        J. De Neuvil: NÂ°2

                                        Paris the 22 November 1770.

                    I know nothing certain or
interesting about the Estates of Brittany.
Mr The Duke of Rouxembert is still in
Paris, the Parliament of the Crown, at Court,
where he has been well received, she hardly
leaves the Duke of Choisenil anymore, the
arrangement is regarded as concluded, the Marches
of Lower Brittany and Poitou nevertheless
want to compel him to Pass.
        The Public has not yet arrived, and
the Duke is indisposed with the Flu.
        The Prince of Beauveau leaves around the
15 November to be of the French Academy
in the Place of Mrs de Moncris.
        It is said that the Estates of Savoy is in
Quarrel with the estates of Beauvais over the
Weight of Posts and Prunes, or
[ottez] forming something [lauts] one another.
The Letters from Rome of the 8 November announce
nothing interesting.
        One of our Ships which took refuge in
a Dutch Colony, carried away from it
20 Thousand Muscats to plant, which it has
deposited in Good Part at the Isle de France,
plantations have been made of them, they have
