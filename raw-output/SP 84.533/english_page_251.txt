No 04.

Paris the 14th October 1775.

                                    One the march also
Extremely interesting at Fontainebleau.
Mr de Boynes recommends [uyand] obtained
from the Kings, does not join with it. Of explanation
of the Parlement of a Province, where he is going
to be Intendant, His Majesty has charged
with this Task Mr Bertier Councilor
of State, who is going to leave soon & with 6 of the
Duke of Nivernais for this operation.
     One reports from Arras, that all the
Productions of this Province are in good State,
& that all is going for the best there. Mr le Doyen
Minister of Requests, who was kept in
the Disposition with Mr de Rochechouart,
has returned to Paris, he has been very well
received at Court; one is satisfied with
his operation.
     One reports from Rennes, that one expects
the Suppression of the Parlement; the
People will see it without Pain; the
Hauteur and pride of the Members
of this Senate, make them detested; all is
quite changed in Brittany since the time
of the Duke of Aiguillon; one finds him
