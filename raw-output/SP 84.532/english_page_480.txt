EDICT OF THE KING,

Ordering the suppression of the Grand Council.

Given at Versailles in the month of April 1771.

Registered in Parliament.

LOUIS, BY THE GRACE OF GOD, KING OF FRANCE AND NAVARRE: To all present & to come, greetings. The wishes of the people & the attention to affairs with which our Court of Parliament of Paris was overburdened, determined our predecessors, Kings Charles VIII & Louis XII, to create a Tribunal of Members of his private Council, & subsequently a Tribunal of Councillors of the King with the right to judge all matters within the jurisdiction of our Court of Paris, on appeals from inferior & particular Judges, the functions of the Council were circumscribed, & as simple depositary of the laws & of the authority of the Legislator. The superior Councils of some of our Provinces have been vested with several rights of the Parliament of Paris, & the men whom we have prescribed to the right of Committimus, have decided us; & we would be inclined to recall near us the Members of our Council, who still compose even less confidence that they have always until now by their zeal, by their enlightenment & by their assiduity merited by their zeal, but what occupies us essentially in our Parliament of Paris: In this view, we have the object of the general wish that has been made to render to this Court, the object of the general oath that they have always given us & of the oath by which has ended to render to this Court, the particular offices that we had consequently suppressed the office of our Grand Council, & the offices that had been attached to it.
