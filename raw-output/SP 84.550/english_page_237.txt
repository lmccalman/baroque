Second.

Madrid the 2. May 1776:

I have just received
news from Cartagena dated
the 27th of last Month, that on the 22nd ditto,
entered into this Port the Two
frigates of the King, the Sta Julie, and the
Naye of the Canarias, commanded
by Mr. de Quereda, with a
small French frigate, which
served the Smugglers,
destined for Mayen, with a
cargo of Contraband,
consisting of Masts, Construction
Wood, Hames for
Bridles, Cordage, &c., and having
on board an Ambassador
who is said to be the General of the
Marine of said France,
and Twelve [Mauvres], who are
currently there
