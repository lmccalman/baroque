136.

The King having first taken the Defense
of the Count of Mailleboris, said, that he had
undergone the Sentence of the Tribunal, and that the
[Confiance] that he sold him, justified him
fully; the Merchants of Rome
sent the money on the 17th... with
a Letter to the Marshal de Couvroy for the Count
de Reiny, and the War; His Majesty
has dismissed to the Queen the Count of Remy
who had received from Este Minister
of war with Miss de Montesquieu.

One does not know if the Count of Mailleboris
will leave the Kingdom to seek
an Asylum elsewhere.

The Count of Reiny will depart for the
[Journées], he has joined then the [quartennée] of
the Press, Piacenza, the Parmesan,
S. Ariens, and the Maritime States of these
Provinces.

The Marquis of Torigny will leave the
Regiment that he has of the Cavalry Guard
He has [demeuré] the grand Equerry Bourget,
and this Regiment will be given to the Count
of Roigny.
