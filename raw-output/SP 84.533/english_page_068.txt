Rotterdam this 20th July 1771.

Melor.

Because the Correspondent whom
we had in Shay, and who at present is in
Antwerp, has delivered to us a certificate, that
he has dealt with a new Sailor, a very
[bequagne] and finds him so, and that one foresees
[tourelune] in the Ports of Holland, in
[Sagementup], in order not to find myself badly, to
send him as soon as possible, he will arrive only
on the 2nd, 4th, and 5th August in the [privince] of
[Fage] of the Announcement Sheet of Antwerp, with
three Meland, that all is exactly
[achondement].

Supposing that a Correspondent from
[Vieldes] could perhaps be of some
utility, from your [epuisance], I have taken
the Steps to endeavor whether he could
answer us with his Response
that one awaits in what good it does us not,
and I have inquired in all [Relances]
of Holland through [Rentrise] of Rotterdam,
how the merchants understand
that they will [verserout] very little to good, given the
