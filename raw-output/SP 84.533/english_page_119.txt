Private Letter

Paris, April 16, 1773.

One regards here
the Support that one [gave?] of the Parliament of Besançon
as very important. No one from Messrs
de Boynes, nor from here Parliament has
wanted to make [him] First Resident, at the
time when he was Intendant of this
Province, because he had been Procurator
General of the Royal Chamber: The [frois]
is the Cause of His Greatness, for in order
[to leave] him with Honor, he has been made
Counselor of State, and introduced to the Council.

Private Letters from Poland
announce Successes in some
places of the Confederates: the [pouvois] made
determined to Sarmatia will be [recensés]
to an exact Discipline, and to a
followed tactic, of which the beginnings
to attempt the Advantages and the legitimate Presage
one claims in Consequence, that they [regardé-]
asked the Ministry of France for
officers to command them, and
Confirm them in these Dispositions.
