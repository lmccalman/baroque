The Empress could well write in
this Manner to a Minister whom She
might suspect could furnish her
Projects, but She knows too well that our
interests are intimately linked with those
of the Turk, to attempt to separate them from them.
There is no reason to believe either that
the entreaties and steps of Count
Du Luc of Sweden have had greater
success with the Protestant leagues
of Germany, and they await all the display
that the King of Sweden will make in Poland,
which proves according to Them, that this Prince
will remain in perfect Neutrality.
If this is so, as well as the accession of the
Republic of Venice to the Alliance
of the Two Imperial Courts, which have
promised it a small Portion in the
Spoils of the Turk, it must be admitted that
Russia, which we knew well established,
was quite well negotiated. As for
our Cabinet, it does not yet appear
