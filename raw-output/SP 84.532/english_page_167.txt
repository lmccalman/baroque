Private letter
                                        Paris the 3rd February 1771.

                                        The Chancellor Mault
went to Dinner at Madame de la Vrillere's about him
Dignification of the Parliaments, Madame Adelaïde and
put him on this Matter, told him that he
undertook a Work of the History of
France, that he Discovers nothing difficult
for the happiness of the Majesty and the Maintenance
of the Royal Authority; this Princess having
replied, that she and the ten others
P. the Opposition of the Parliaments in the Moments
in which they counted, made him understand
that she found very disadvantageous for the
Children of a Part of the Company,
And that there would be reason to apprehend
the same thing for the future;
that he relied here in this way.
We the Marshal de Boufflers having
to Support the [Griffonnés] of the Chief of
the Magistrature, and to regard him as
the man drawn whom he needed
and the time; which engages him to ask
that you were there, Monsieur the Marshal
de Vrillere of the Bastilles where you found yourself
there, replied the Chancellor, and I hope
to extricate myself from it as well.
