305.
                                    4 31 July

                    Toulon the 25th July 1770.

                         La Touyère

Fifty commanded commanded by M.r de Vaudreuil,
twenty by M.r de Marigny, at la Courbe by M.r
de Callaye, le Bluissant by M.r Bougette,
and le Requin by M.r de Guichen, set sail
the day before yesterday Morning for the Levant, and since then
three Swedish Merchant Vessels and two
Danish Brigantines, one of Them has three masts, and two Spanish
Tartans from Malaga, and le Serpent by M.r
de Cambrey Lieutenant of Vessel, armed
to lend by the board of the Barres to these against
the Barbary pirates, departed yesterday.

The Squadron of M.r de Borda parcels from the 1st to the
3rd June, will return in a few days.
Work is currently underway on [arque non] 24 which
determined the Commander to go bombard
Biserte, the Squadron is occupied with this Bombardment,
while some Vessels, [Chabeès], french,
the Galleys and [Galliettes], [Malhetaires] returned
from Cagliari and other Places.

Advice has been received that the Danish Squadron was bombarding
Algiers, and that the [Hollets], [Gauffes] and [Ollemmaniens]
formed the squadron, the Advice is doubtful.

The [Hollets] of the Barres made the appearance
of a [holet] on the 19th, a [Gauffre] the appearance, and
[flamanes] from England and Provence.

The Corvette la Blonde arrived yesterday from
Basta, the expedition successful.

                                        Pervis.
