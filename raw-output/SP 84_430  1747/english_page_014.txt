At La Rochelle the 2nd of October 1744
I arrive at this moment from La Rochelle Paris
after having endured there a Storm that would have been
far and which only caused one Merchant vessel to run aground
[bord d'été] at the Coast, fortunately it ran aground
on the mud, there will not be great Harm.

Here is the Name of the King's vessels destined
to escort the assembled fleet which is at Bordeaux:
The Tonnant of 90 cannon [l'haprès]
the Sorbe [fe Les] of 74, The Monarque 74, The
Neptune 70, The Trident 60, The Sangueux 50
The Sirène 54, The Castor frigate 62, The Mé-
gère 60, The Contans of the Company of 60.
But it only has this first [l'Saberté] of 60
cannon of 12 [Liste].

A Ship loaded with Cod has arrived
come from Newfoundland. There also arrived on the 28th
September another Ship loaded with Dried Cod
which made its catch at the Bank, it
reports that it encountered off The [Travers] of the Is-
land of [Olésal] 80 English War Vessels
[MMe de l'esclam] set sail on the 20th Sept.
all alone to go to St Domingue.
