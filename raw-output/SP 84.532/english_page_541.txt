                                                                     Paris the 9. May 1725

                                                                     The Absence of our
Former Parliament, and the Absence of the other
Provincial Parliaments, throws a furious
Void in the News; our New
Parliament furnishes us nothing, and the
great tranquilities which reign in
our Ports now, completes the
Political silence.
     There is an old King at Versailles, an
extraordinary Council, which lasted
a long time.
     Mr. de Repas who is a great Worker,
has made great Memoirs, [trouveilles]
on the great Arrangements in
the Corps of the Navy, he intended to
put them back into Force, to perfectly reestablish
everything, by increasing the Number of
Vessels and Officers, and by
diminishing useless Expenses.
     The Chatelet has not yet recognized
our New Parliament; but
one believes that it will do so. There were, in
case of Refusal, [Luttes] all ready
to replace it.
                                                                     On.
