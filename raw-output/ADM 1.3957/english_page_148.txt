The Letters from Korea of the 5th of April, and those
from Paris of the 28th of March, announce to us
nothing of interest.

It is believed at Court, that one has succeeded at
Constantinople in recovering from the Alarm
that had caused the News of the approaching
Coming of a Russian Fleet in the Archipelago;
it is assured that the Ottoman Marines are
putting themselves on a good footing, and the Maritime Islands
well garrisoned.

I have just reviewed three Hundred
[Bourgaux], of which you will see the Detail in
your Gazettes.

They are leaving [l'Ismeilles] which carry
the nearby Islands.
