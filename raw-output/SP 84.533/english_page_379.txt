Extract

from a Letter of Count Roumanzow
from the Camp of Valency
Dated the 27th October 1783. O.S.

I announce that I had advanced before
entering into Winter Quarters, to attack
generally all the Corps of the Enemy which
were positioned on this side and beyond the Danube, I received
the News, that Monsieur Inglés was advancing
towards the Corps of General Igelstrom in Wallachia:
I was therefore gone, I carried not being able to give
my orders in time enough to have them executed
immediately my orders to General
d'Igelstrom, to cross the Danube, to attack
Fotzay, [A.Sile], saw several days.
I had the order march to the Lieutenant
General of the Corps at Babadaag, where until
he [s'étrangloit] my Attended. I had
given on the 18th from the Camp to the Turkish Corps, posted
at Toulzay, taken 40 pieces of Cannon, 3 [pierriers]
& 2 mortars, 12 Flags, & carried off a
part of the Town of Babadaag, from the Camp
of the Vizir, from his very tent, & of eight
the Park of Artillery which he had with him.
He remained there on the 21st to have evacuated
the Cannons which he could not transport,
to have the Castle burned, which was there
fortified.
