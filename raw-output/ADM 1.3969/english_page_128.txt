N. 1 July. No. 2.

Paris the 26th June 1782.

It no longer appears doubtful
that the Prussians have entered
Bohemia; and we expect
to learn in a few days that the
two armies have entirely
Mastered this Province; because
it would have experienced no
resistance.

A Letter written from Prague by
a General Officer, seems to announce
that the Emperor will not remain the ample
Spectator of this War; and that the
Russians profiting already ready, against the
account of the King of Prussia, who on the
point of opening it would attack them first
outside of Vienna; will instruct us
fully on these Objects.

by the Treaty concluded between Spain
and the Porte, the Common Power has
committed itself to furnish ten Warships
