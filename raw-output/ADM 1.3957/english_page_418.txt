The Provincial Letters speak only of a Council Decree of last September 24th, which was posted in the Province, and which creates the happiest sensations there; it orders that ten Counselors abroad will be named. It orders that they will be named a First Governor.

The King having had it declared to the States of Brittany, that he did not want anything that would have the agent of Officers of the Duke of Aiguillon, that he would nevertheless agree to speak of it, the States to conform to his intentions, charged their Committee to examine the establishment of Parliament, Commissioners were named to carry out the examination of this matter, after rendering Account of it.

There arose a universal [Demeure] of a [Oye], general at La Ré. At the decree dated [des d'écnire] having deposed. [D'Ecosquis] in the [Rers].

The opinion of the [Rers] was to send the keeper in [Tenens], [Perediès] in Copper, to solicit the Return of this man who holds the Province. He would have to [les Devoir], the Nobility wanted to [Surveiller] a Deputation in Body, but M. Dupleix told them that upon their Return the Bishop of Nantes refused his cares. He said, if they insisted for the Clergy, he would write a very strong Letter to the Comptrollers
