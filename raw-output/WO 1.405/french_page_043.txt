Plymouth Gen'l of 7 May 180

County/Eure/Son
& Last/Mont/Seur
of Any, 4 May 98

I remains having arrived in the neighbourhood of the
small at this part of an English prize from Belleisle which
except thirty & seized here this morning informed
in parvis, & as far as I have yet the suspect, that the
man Society on his consuls having gone to Denmark
& which part his signal belongs, I have not been able
all men to see him - Nos transmitted to Eure inclosed
the account which I have obtained from him &

John Bouchier matter of the Brigge
Fidelity of Dartmouth, arrived in this port on the
2d instant from Copenhagen, which place he left on the
19th inst: part of his intend cargo behind
He intends to have sailed on the 20th but on the
morning of the 21st he was desired by His Danish
Madame, Mr Cons. to when he was consigned, to
leave the place immediately, or his signal would
be stopped and detained accordingly.

Two other English boats, on the Plymouth
& the Diligence, both of Dartmouth, of Bristford
sailed two or three hours after having left there on
the same terms - he says that at Belleisle was
a very lot proof of Teamen - he does that on
Embarque had a week before been laid a English
Profile as Ostia, and he says the same was
repented at Belleisle, but not on the Profile of others
nations -

This Profile did not take on board any than
one third of her cargo, the rest being engaged for a
plan of draught.
