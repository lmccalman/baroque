Madrid the 5th July 1784.

The King has received news
of the departure of our expedition against
Algiers, which set sail from Cartagena
the eve of St Peter's Day. This Armament
consists of nine ships of the line,
eleven frigates, twelve xebecs, four
brigantines, four fireships, four
salandres, four galleys 36 gunboats,
36 bomb vessels, two
galiots: six vessels destined for
boarding, and six transport
vessels. In this number are
included the assistance sent by
the Courts of Naples, of Lisbon, and
the Religion of Malta.

It is written from Cartagena that
Don Frederico Gouvira, captain
of the King's ship, has brought in
a French ship loaded
With artillery, and war munitions
destined for Algiers, and coming from Samos.
