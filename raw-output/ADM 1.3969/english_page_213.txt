Mr de Breuvet learned that Peace
was made, upon touching at the Cape of
Good Hope, where after a rather
long Stay, he brought back, of which one had
not yet officially received [news].
We shall have in a few Days
private Letters that the
Corvette brought from India; and
with them, greater Details
about all that has passed, since
last December
until the 12th of April.

It is said that the Duke of Mandelier
has made new Memorials for the
the strongest Instances for
Peace, at Petersburg, causing the greatest
Prejudice to the Commerce
of his Nation, and to the Interests
of His King. These Reasons were heeded,
and he received a
positive [response], that it would be Signed on the 6th
of September.

Mr Henderson, Secretary
of Embassy, died publicly.
