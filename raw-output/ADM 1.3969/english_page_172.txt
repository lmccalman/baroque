Madrid, July 17, 1740.

We were waiting at any
moment to learn that the Squadron
of Cartagena had set sail,
Still written to see Prado; Mr Courant,
consisting of four Line Regiments,
Namely the Terrible, the St Lorenzo, the St
Pascal, & St Jean Baptiste; Four
Brigantines, Ten Xebecs, Two Tartans
Two Bilanders, Twenty-one Gun
Sloops, Twenty-one Bomb vessels
Three Feluccas, and Three Fireships.

There are found more than 4 Ships of the
Line, Frigates, and even
Xebecs, than Mr Barralo had
requested; this comes from the
fact that the Number of gun sloops
and Bomb vessels being considerable,
and their Service requiring Men each;
(although in their [Taverne] they
only need five or six Sailors) the Ships
and the frigates will carry all the men who must be
