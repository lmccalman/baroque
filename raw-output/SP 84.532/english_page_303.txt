For lack of interesting News, I attach
here a Piece, which shows well enough the interior state
of this Kingdom, and if it does not instruct,
it could perhaps serve to amuse
for a Moment.

                Extract
        from the Gazette of Copenhagen
           from the Month of January 1778.

His Majesty the Emperor in the Month of December
last, [l'on] Ambassador for the Court of
France, with a Present of 30 Elite Horses
in Recompense for the welcome he received
from His Maj. the King of France.

    His Majesty the Emperor stopped in Paris two
[avons] only, and asked to speak
to the First [Suegen], to give him the
horses that he had in his care. he was
told that he was dead, and that the Position
was vacant.
    He inquired next for a Minister
of Foreign Affairs: he was told
that he was exiled to [les terres], and was not
replaced.
    He wanted Next to speak to the Minister of
War, he was told that this Position was
in the same Case.
    He would have been in Correspondence with the one of the
Navy, he learned that he was exiled, and not
replaced.
