this operation, the Judges & those subject to justice, we shall facilitate
access to the Tribunals, we shall render them inaccessible & the
dearest to our peoples, by simplifying the forms & by reducing
the costs of procedures. Finally we shall ensure the repose of our
subjects, the maintenance of public order & the punishment of crimes,
by making the high-justice Seigneurs find their particular advantage
in the prosecution of the guilty, & by furnishing them with
the means to discharge themselves of the costs entailed by criminal
procedures. If to fulfill these views, we have felt the necessity of
restricting the contentious jurisdiction of our Parlement of Paris,
we have made it our duty to preserve for it moreover
all its rights & all its prerogatives. Depository of the laws,
charged with promulgating them, with having them executed; with making
known to us their inconveniences, & with conveying to
us the needs of our peoples. Judge finally of all
questions which concern our Crown, & the rights of Peers
& Peerages, it will enjoy still, this confidence, more
precious than that given by truth, enlightenment, zeal & 
disinterestedness. For these causes & others moving us thereto,
by the advice of our Council, & of our certain knowledge, full
power & royal authority, We have by the present perpetual
& irrevocable Edict, said, established & ordained, say, establish
& ordain, will & it pleases us as follows:
