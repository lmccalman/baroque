Constantinople May 3, 1770.

The Affairs of the Turks
are taking a better turn than one
would have dared to hope, the [indefinissible] Peace
presented by the Court of Petersburg and accepted
with Eagerness, is signed, which has made some Attentions
The principle that one professes since such a time, of which
they have charged the Russians, it is all by the [ens]
their glory, in the intention of rendering them
the means, but also, for the [Arides].

It appears that the Porte will bring all its
Maritime forces against Crimea, it is all
a matter of time to bring what will precede everything,
it is the only time, and it is of which the Russians
can. [Haut leur].

Count Orloff has delivered all the [triones]
to us, as if he wished to attempt some
Expedition, but he is not believed to be in a state
to make an Enterprise.

The Court has asked me for Two Thousand auxiliary
men, for, as soon as we she will be able in
good faith the essence of She demands Troops
from this Power, which in you will place
The independence of these Peoples, would not be
the oblique, or rather the refugees [mois],
and one can the field after a Spirit on
Grand Seigneur to inform him of it
