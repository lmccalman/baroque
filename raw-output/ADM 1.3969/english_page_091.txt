Cadiz, April 28, 1782.

By the arrival of the Corvette
the Vestale, departed from Havana on the 16th
of last March, we have the following
Details.

That M. de Grasse with 9. warships
✓ of war, thousands of [maries] in convoy
of Troops, had departed from Havana
for St. Domingue.

M. de Solano with Five warships
of war, and the Convoy destined for
Europe, was preparing to set Sail,
but by order that one had that the English
squadron would have equally left
Jamaica, he decided to [faire l'danger]
and know what he had on board his
Vessels.

They were still awaiting at Havana
✓ the Vessel the St. Charles, and the Frigate
the Eglé from Terra Cruz, with Five Million
Piastres, &c.
