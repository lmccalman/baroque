B 22 August

Paris the 11th August 1783

It is reported from Marseille
on the 31st July, that a merchant Vessel
had just entered there, coming from Port-au-
Prince, whose Captain reports having
seen on the 4th June, abeam, a
Ship from St. Domingue, absolutely
abandoned, and floating at the mercy of the Wind;
it was believed to be Dutch.

The King being in his Council
[Inmer] last, has resolved to make
the Acquisition of the Port of L'Orient
belonging to the House of Behar,
as an Object very advantageous
for his Service, and His Majesty has
authorized his Comptroller General
to treat accordingly with the
House of Behar. This Acquisition
will cost 10 Millions to the King, [sca] Thousand
Livres each Year, for
Twenty Years.
