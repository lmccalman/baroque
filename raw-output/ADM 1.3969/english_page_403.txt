19 7bre Arrived

Paris the 2nd August 1785.

                    There is nothing new
at our Ports.
                    Nothing interesting is
happening at Court either.
                    The Parliament which will hear
tomorrow the report of the King's Men,
in the affair of Mr le Nce, dismissed
on this occasion the Dukes and Peers.

                    Rumors are still spreading on
the subject of news of the abdication of the
King of Poland.

                    It is reported from Vienna that
Dom Barcelo wrote to the minister
dated the 13th of July in the evening to
give him notice that he had anchored
before Algiers the morning of the same day
driven by the winds until the moment
when he began his attack upon his
entry into the Bay. He began it
