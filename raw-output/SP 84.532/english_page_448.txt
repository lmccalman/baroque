Brest the 5th August 1725.

[Relâche] Garrison

that he has renewed it.
M. de Cours has finally received orders;
& one expects to depart, only the new
armament of the Dutch to pass there.
M. de Courcy has not yet received his
Own there. His destination is posts at the
great [longe]; Master Nozieux in the capacity
of Governor.
One had united hope since the advancement
to that of Martinique; but O. Holles
Cercy procured it for His Nephew, former
Captain of the [Baletandres] Regiment.
One sends 2 Vessels from Lorient for
the King; and 2 Flutes in our Port to
St Pierre de Montreal, and [des Rogueliers]
commanding the two Vessels;
the two Flutes will be by Messieurs
Lombar & [Maunahée] who have
[precisés] them from Mademoiselle Rolland. The 4 Vessels
go to the Isle de France, and carry
Workers, a Number of 600 Men.
