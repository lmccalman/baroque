N° 92.                                Paris the 7th October 1725.

                                      It is reported from Brest
that the [Place] departed for The Observations
the 28th to the 26th; and the other armed Vessels
will depart successively within 8 days.
An order has been sent to the Chevalier de Martocé
to go there, and to several Staff Officers,
and she will do quarantine. The S.r Arguelen
armed were about to depart. Work has been done this
time [la doucicité] and the Zeal of the Police,
the [le] of Orleans will be in a state to arm.

It is expected here that the Squadron
which is with [amarrée est appris], but
that it had departed from Paris in three
Divisions, the 1st for Cannes, the 2nd for
Melchior, and the 3rd for Marseilles.
It is said that there are at Toulon Letters from Tel-
[Secres], their Princes of the Armament the summer, but
there have been no numerous Enemies.

My Patriots who have used this near the Profes-
[sore] have been well received, they so affirm,
has been on Board with Counter Order, which is to him the
affairs to give him several Catalans,
[deux] many small Vessels suitable for
Coasting; and they disarm them of Cannon and
Munitions [respectés] Vessels that all can.

The King and the Royal family, with the exception
of the two young Princesses, will go
Next Monday directly from Versailles to
Fontainebleau.
