Versailles the 26th September 1776.

I would presume enough
that the King, Sir Mr. le Maurepas
would want to applaud the Views of Mr.
de Vergÿ, all applied to those,
that one has had until now, Views so small,
but so expedient for the Kingdom
that he sends [j'on l'eliffee] that he
can do nothing. We do not believe that there are
today such famous [Boules].
the Comptroller having the [acon?es],
similar, an [Anglot] quite singular
to have much money:
It is to have redeemed ancient
deposits of the Center to [Danien], in
touching the Hand to those who would
[foucieraient] there, not of the [Touristée],
by the [Messais], to reimburse
some charges by the old fund.
the [Rojel] holds the [meroit] of the
officers, by the arrear, but they
would raise by all the place.
