                             Paris the 18th March 1776.

                             My letters from Toulon
of the 10th report, that the Ships without
Armament were to that same Day
enter into Careening.
     Those from Brest say, that the
Ships the Brillant, the Justicier to
the Indien, to carry the [Chef de France]
the new Governor, is in the Roadstead,
and will not delay in setting Sail.
They are working with Diligence, on
the Armament of the Evolution Squadron.
     The Parliament for the return of
Letters on the 12th, assembled on the 13th,
to make a Protestation on
the Lit and the form of the Lit de Justice,
and the decision on the Remonstrances
on the substance, the Commissioners
appointed to place the Objects thereof,
are to assemble this morning at the
Palace, to work on it.
