                                    Brest the 11th December, 1770.

                        We have been for
some days in the firm Persuasions
of Peace; this may and must be; in the
Quantity of troops that are being brought
into Brittany, and of which here is the Statement sent
by the Duke of Duras.

Namely the Charuis 2. Battalions, from Brest and Port-Louis
 2. Battalions, from Rennes 2. Battalions, and the heaviest
 Artillery 2400. Men, from Brest,
 Centre Berge 2 Bat.s and M. Paul fa. By the 2. Bernier
 Angers Deluniel 2. Bat.s and Artillery of 8. and Bernier
 Gruny Delaugil 2. Bat.s and That Delaunay 2. Bat.s and
 Companies Miners 60. Men, from Brest the 9th January
 Regim.t of Bourbon and Ponthiere and of Bretagne the 10th January
 Each 2. Battalions, and the Regim.t of the 10th January
 Each 2. Bat.s and Artillery of 4. and of the 12th January
 P.elle.tte Berge 2. Bat.s and the Gendarmerie of the 12th January
 Groiny de Conduit 2400. Men, and Lunetier of 7th January
 Companies Horses 60. Men, and Rame of the 18 Horses.
 Companies Sappe.rs and Grenadiers of 30. Horses
 Warnne 2. Bat.s and Dreux 2. Bat.s.

 It Gruny makes 2. Bat.s and Chartrussel and Castell 2. Bat.s and
 d' Hauteforte 2. Bat.s and Their Carot and Grenadiere
 and Carnes 4. Bat.s and Barmy and 67. January, others 18. February
 of Bretagne of 3000. Men, at Vannes.
