165.
                                                    17.10 Oct. N°2

                    Paris the 5th October 1790.

                                        Word is sent from
Toulon on the 27th that the frigate l'Alcide, whose
Committee has received the orders, left this part of the
[Médité] of Paris, the 19th the 16th September. The
[bâmes] remain in commerce before Tunis
until the Ratification of the Court has
confirmed the Peace between the Grand Seigneur and the Court
of this treaty, which is moreover all the more useful
the Preliminaries are always lengthy to be
transcribed, and you will have them in your Gazettes.

Nothing interesting has been learned from
Trials, neither from Paris, nor from the Provinces, nor
from Paris nor Marseille, that the Corsairs
of Tunis were beginning to arrive there, and that
those from the [Echelons] were awaited with anxiety
to have News.

M. de Rainny gave the letter of
M. de Castries. It will be signed at two
Deputies of the Coasts from Bayonne to
Holland for the [Mêteo] of Navigation.

The Danish Ships which were before
[Mazo] having all left the Harbor of Marseille
to go to Mahon where it was thought that they
would delay receiving the order to make their
Reason at Marseille as Usual.

Letters from [Cranes] of the 14th July said,
that they are [bucks] and [Coppeys] the attachment returns.

M.
