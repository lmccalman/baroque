N°69.

                                    Paris the 22nd April 1771.

                    Reports from Brest
of the 18th that M. the Marquis de la Rosiere, who was
disembarked at the Cape to serve the King on
land, distinguished himself there in the Corps of the
Marine.

A frigate is to be armed for St Domingue,
which the Count de Beurnonville will board.

Messieurs de Roquefeuil, father and La Motte Piquet
with the titles have a Corvette for Martinique
and M. de Ligondès Ship Lieutenants have the Commission
of Fireship Captains, and the Count Dubourg.

Messieurs Lombard and de Comarieu have had
for the Command of the gun boats at the [Chequeur] of
Brest for the East Indies.

M. de Roquefeuille, Captain, has been launched
and remained Commander of a Port also Captain.
The King has appointed His Excellency The Ambassador
of Parma for the Service and the Port of being
[agrée] effective; it is by honor that one destines him.

In the Port of Rochefort, the [Majordé]
has made me only one Lieutenant General, it is M. the
Marquis, Inspector of the three Departments,
and [Maréchal] de Contes. The Promotions for
the Ship Captains of 1. is postponed
to the Month of September next.

The Marquis de Montegnard has worked
on the plans with the King, he has been disposed
in the transfer of the Schools following the
