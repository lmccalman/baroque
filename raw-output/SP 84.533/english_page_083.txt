Toulon the 26th July 1795.

                                    Our two Frigates are
in quarantine until the 20th August.
    The Correspondence [alias] for Mr Durand de
Sauvage, was delivered to Sieur Reyffer;
I will inform you of all the Corps.
    I encountered in the Inn of Vaufrienne
Reyffer, a Frigate, J. G. Butte, without the [St] of Cannons;
likewise Mr Durand [parent] in delivering him
the Reyffer and the 2 packets, in present those
of the two Cargoes to make a Corps of Cannons
at Bouc and in the Country, which all accepts with
his Flag and the Pennant; the Reyffer that this
had not made a [hond] Corps at Bouc and;
Mr Durand put his Consent, during
which time he and played on Board the 30 Corps
of Cannon, of which one part the large made the other
crossed the [St] Board, and in a Theater of.
His [Condt] to exit then from the Reyffer, who for
this Response will accept a Theater of;
then to the [Condt], Mr Durand and [Levauge] the
Corps of Cannon, and made them Put their pieces;
On the Plank in [rendis] the Officer detained;
in complaining of a [St] sent to him; Mr
Durand put him the Admiral to carry him
the Complaints; the Reyffe refused to carry
this letter. There were Words come from both
sides; our Frigate proposed to the
