at Paris the 1st February 1760,
by Bouvelles

My Man has returned from
Bordeaux; he brings me a packet from Holland containing
a Case of this line on his Ministry of Government
for Canada; In Michel as particular Frigates
are loaded Provisions and Munitions; but the Court has not
yet made explicit, Whether it will send Troops.
They had offered the Command of this Squadron
to grand Louvo, which he refused: This Refusal is attributed
to the fact that he has neither the Cross of St. Louis nor
with Vessel of the King; the Minister refused it
with du Rouvrai; which is very
bad Politics in the present circumstance.
They designated, more specifically Captain
Cornit, or Joffret [bras homme]; and in truth one has
great need of it, for an expedition so precarious.
But the [ou] proposed to the Directors of the interest fr &
the Armament] that it be chartered for the Mutinies.
