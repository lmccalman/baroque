Toulon the 27th of February 1793.

Mister de Bourgeat.

Commander of the Regiment of
la Sarre commands Artillery Regiment of the
Isles [lui demeu] for three months new
street, the Destination is subordinate
to transport to Tunis & the Equipment
of the King, which one expects from Paris in
18 hours, and to render it to the place of the
guard of the Bey who must make it
reach the spouse and the Queen.
One has well that these same arms and the
parts and [vivile ay. tuniville] have the
[moulele ay.] to Malta, and then
will enter to Tunis.

There are eleven English armed two
frigates, Tunisville, & Ray [apparel] those
who depart from the King & the Queen and
who carries from Good Battery v.

Commanders that all that still
named.

go in correspond [se] the Regiment
