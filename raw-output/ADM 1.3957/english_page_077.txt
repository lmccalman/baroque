Bouglay, No 3.

                    Paris the 16th February 1770:

                         A Letter from [Prédérit]
authorized arms two Corvettes from Gueusd, and the
Bougayné, the first for the Martinique, and
the Second for St Domingo. That is all there
is in this Country.

    That from the Cape will [Appointeront] us. the two
Officers arrived.

    Nothing is done at Toulon.

    Funds are being made in funds at the Isle of [l'Aße]
for Embarkations for our Colonies of
America, but as it is the greater part
Children of Paris Libertines, there always dies
always two thirds of them in these Islands.

    The King has truly summoned Prince Frederick
of Prussia, and will remain [Règlement] at Magdeburg
but will [Condemnera] the Prince President
to give him the Regency. It carries in
Substance, that His Highness the Finances not having
appeared to return to his Means, He could not
change anything in them at present, but that he
will occupy himself with seeking the forms to make
affairs as prosperous as the State to
relieve His Peoples.

    The Prince President renders the Army his
this Regency [Etande] to the Chambers assembled,
after many Debates and different Opinions. It
[fistoroit] that it would be made to His Majesty
humble Remonstrances.
