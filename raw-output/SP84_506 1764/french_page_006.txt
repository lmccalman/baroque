6.) As it may be thought proper to encourage
the peopling His Majesty's new Acquisitions
in North America, be it as it of my Duty
to represent to Your Excellency, which if
not noticed, will be the Ruin of a great many
Zeal and good wise.

7.) The People that are most versed in these Affairs
with whom I have made it my Business to
converse, give me the strongest Assurances,
that if the Inclinations of the Government to
encourage new Settlers were known, and that the
Germans like the terms they did before, that a
People enough will come without the Government
being at the whole Expence of transporting them.

8.) The Thing being transacted intirely by the Merchants
is subject to great Abuses. A great has prompted
several of them to steal these poor People in
the Choice of the Shipping, in which Instances no
Regard is often had to their proper Room or
Board for so long a Voyage, the sad Effects of
which have cost the Lives of many of these poor
People, and I have seen numerous Instances
of Deceits of this Kind.

I am directed to send the Letters to such
