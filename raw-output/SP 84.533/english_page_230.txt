Madrid the 22nd September 1775.

The last letters received from Corrall, inform me that they have appointed as War Vicar the P. and American of 60 Cavalry Pieces, Amon the Vicar of St. Joseph of America, as they had indicated to me [n'habitement], who [elevoit le tendre à l'exilice], and who [avoit avoir à jour] the Order for the said Post.

But I received Notice from [l'exilice], that the Vicar had arrived in this Poyo.

I am likewise informed, that the Vicar of [Terre le Princepan], was again at the head of this Post, to make another Crusade.

I had News from [l'Hirégène], that the widow Lafayette, mentioned in my previous ones, was to [taire une Noile] for [l'exilice], in order to transport to [l'athégène] of the Islands, the [Vice-Roch] of the [Vie].
