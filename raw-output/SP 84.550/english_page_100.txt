Munitions, and that more than 300
Artillerymen have already arrived there,
and are occupied in provisioning the supplies
from [bourges] that they are going to send there.
They are delivering here all the Vagabonds
and People Without Avowal, for the Service,
and it is presumed, that they will do
the same throughout the Kingdom.
My Friends from our Maritime Parts
report nothing interesting.
The Moroccans are very tranquil,
and yet until now one does not
know what to make of it, regarding
the alleged Treaty of accommodation
and of commerce with them, of which
so much has been spoken. It is Supposed that
His Majesty wants to make them
accept it, and see, if by their
conduct they render themselves
susceptible to it.
