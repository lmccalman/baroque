                        Toulon the 11 December 1770

                                    I was beginning here to
be a question of war, Mr. Duquesne
in the absence of Mr. Herson our
Intendant, received yesterday Orders from the
Minister to have provisions prepared
and all that is necessary, to
arm 15 Ships and 8 Frigates
at the first Order that will be given;
consequently we are already working on it, but
afterwards feebly.    In the 2nd
instant of the Russians

                        Toulon the 19 December 1770

                                    The Armament of which one
Regards here the basis in manner; we
judge it only in Distinction
We have currently engaged;
in Double provisions, we prepare all
the Ships and Frigates.    Mr. the
Colonel Chief of armaments to arrive;
one does not know which Official [ne N.ce]
will serve long.
                                                    Brest.
