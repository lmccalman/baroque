Geneva the 23rd January 1770.

                A Bernese at Brie
right has sent me to write to you
for more than two years.
    The Letters come from Genoa, from Italy at
[tournez], that the Court of Nantes determines
to favor putting the Navy On a
good Footing. That in the Course of
December there left from Genoa some
large Tartans, loaded with Wood of
Construction, for Cartagena.
    These Letters add, that one
fears for the Peace of Italy,
that something is brewing there,
one even claims to know, that
France has requested from the King of
Sardinia the free Passage through
his States for Italy, of a Corps
of troops, that One sends that
His Sardinian Majesty will not be able
to refuse.
    One has learned here Prague, that the
English Papers have announced an
