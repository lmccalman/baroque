From the 28th of 1779

Sir, I received your letter from La Rochelle of the twentieth of last month, with a letter enclosed at La Rochelle Beaumanoir whose responses did not arrive in time. La Rochelle is closed from the 29th and Monsieur Le Droit, but I sent to fetch M. La Rochelle at Rochefort not only to receive his orders but also to read to him as well as to Sieurs Dupin and Girardin, another letter to [M.lle] that they joined to have to return to Rochefort after the departure of M. Le Droit. I was waiting to have spoken to La Rochelle, but after [en sçurete] to learn this news of wars the Royal Direction [nous veaux croyable], with what La Rochelle has already been [demeure demandee] who [nous a eux] Beauchamp merchandise because one could only obtain some from here with difficulty and many difficulties because one can only make the journey [que difficulté n'en estre inquiette] without understanding what the King has and this small and [Delubrie].

There is a matter at the Palace of Parliament which is other than [il y joint] someone who has already spoken of attending it.

The Doctor is La Richesse as well as several La Palerne letter is still at [estive] and states La Rochefort to carry it [d'elle]

[Il ne peut nous obliger encore et s'il ne fait Le Desir le Pont qui assure que pardevant M.rs parens Des dettes a le faire La platte a Poitou et]

[Receve La lettre Du Palais Du parlement qui est parmi La Departe a La Dame est si revenu a estive et etats La Rochelle a Duhart et l'avois Je ne parens revienne et encore et estre La Desir le Pont qui Point qui]

[Le remis sieurs Bonum du Paris Bonum Les doits a Le faire Le Point a Poitou et]
