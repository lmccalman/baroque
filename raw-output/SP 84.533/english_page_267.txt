N° 87.

                                                             Paris the 22nd October 1775.

                                                             Madame the Countess
of Provence is doing very well from Smallpox.
The King has appointed monsieur de Rosbecq master
in Supernumerary position, there is no Sign of Danger,
and She has experienced no Change at
Court; the Voyage will perhaps be prolonged
for 3 days, this cause, and the King plans
to return to Versailles.
     The King has newly disposed of the Governorships
of Paris, in favor of the Marshal de Biron.
     The general Government of S. Fran.
from which La Rochelle has been detached, is given
to the Duke de Laval, who preserves in addition the
Governorship of Brittany and
The King has called my brother-in-law that two of the
first Works of the Marquis de Monteynad
with the King.
     The Cabinet-physician J. general of Orleans,
and de la Beaujong, is given to the Baron de
Montmorency, Lieutenant general.
     The Lieutenant J. general of Lorraine is
given to the Duke that Marquis de Pierieux, is
granted to the Marquis de Briefity.
     The Secretary of State-J. officer of the Chamber
of Accounts of Dôle, is sold to the Counselor
at his Work to his reunion to the Parliament
of Franche Comté.
                                                                                           Bon.
