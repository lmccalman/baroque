At about noon, when the First President
was delivering to the King alone an Expedition from
the Parliament of Normandy which bore the
Response of the King, the Bourgeois maintained themselves
ennobled.

The First President returned as soon as possible
to Versailles, the King had left for
Havre. The lieutenant came from there, the Response
was sent by a Courier to Havre where
it came to return there the next day, and the
King doubtless to have the Response.

The Parliament adjourned to yesterday Monday
at 9 o'clock in the Morning, where it read
the Response of the King, which is as follows.

I approve the zeal of the Reception of the
courier, Gentlemen, Peasants, &
Parliament, thus I wish to be obeyed.

The Parliament decreed upon this
Response, that the First President
would return to Thank him at Versailles.
In giving Notice to the [Regeaite] of
the happy Reception of his Letters Patent,
all of it, and that she was begged to wish it
to attain the Response of his Majesty.
