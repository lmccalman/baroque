Metz

Memorandum on artillery and the casting of [?]

Monsieur the Duke of Choiseul, Minister and Secretary of State having the Department of War, having had the enclosed Memorandum that I had the honor of presenting to him examined by Mr. the Marshal de Belle Isle, and which has for its object the construction of cast pieces, and having been informed by the said Lord Marshal that my project merited being put into execution; the said Lord Duke wrote to me on the 6th of September last to let me know that he had resolved to adopt the propositions contained in my said Memorandum, and that consequently he had given orders so that I be furnished all the necessary assistance to conduct the trials and experiments in question; that these experiments would be done under my eyes and under my direction, and that in the meantime he was having a gratuity of two thousand livres given to me to help me defray the costs of the journeys that I would have to make.


October 1762
