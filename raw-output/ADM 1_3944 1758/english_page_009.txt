at Rochefort the 21st June 1788.

We are awaiting
to see the English appear, and all is well
prepared to receive them well.
The Convoy is preparing at the Isle of Aix, and will
depart at the first day for St Domingue and
Martinique.

at Paris the 20 June 1788.

They write from St Malo of the 22nd
that the English fleet was still in the Bay
of Cancale that Day, that the Day before they had found
on the seashore eight Sailors who had been thrown
from the fleet, that one counts that it was only waiting for a good
Wind to leave from the Coast and go elsewhere.

One is very anxious everywhere to Know where it
will go, one is well on Guard, but one cannot
be it everywhere; One has principally applied oneself to
provide for the Safety of Brest, Rochefort, l'Orient
Belle-Isle, Isle and Oleron, le Havre is well guarded also.

Here is the Statement sent from St Malo of Ships burned
by the English, for which the King has had the Goodness to
promise full Compensation.

20 Privateers from 4. up to 30. Cannons, burned.
5 [va] 26 Cannons in the Case that one will have Difficulty raising.
43. Merchant Vessels from 100. up to 350.
