N° 90.

                    Paris the 4th November 1775.

                         His Highnesses the Fortunes;
the Good Vendrke, the Count of, and the Countess to
the Merchant the Citangen, [s'ettiés] for the King,
all four departed from Ryslafort the 3rd
November last, and arrived at My Lord the Prince
of Cleves d'Enners, the 3rd of this Month, [Revenves],
having encountered on the Route the worst
weather, these four Vessels putting in
at the Bay of Kowens, have much
Sickness on their Decks, especially the
Fortune which has suffered the most, although
it is one of the good Ships there is.
The Courante, Maillozger, put in
to this Bay the 23rd December, The [Baas] Vauthier
the 3rd January, and the Baltique the 3rd June.
These Vessels should some time after
continue their Route for Iceland.

     The Letters that have been received, are all
for the [Daye] of Madeleine.

     One reports from Bayonne of the 3rd of this Month,
that the New armed Vessel of
[Inennes], without awaiting the Departure of
the Ships of [Basterd] deposited at Dubitar
Riennes, to Establish good Order.
