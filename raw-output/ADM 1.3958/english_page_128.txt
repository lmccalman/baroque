                                        R 25 Ivrend. N°1.

                    Toulon the 30th March 1773.

                                        They are going to examine if,
as has been imagined, a whole Squadron, which should be
commanded by M. d'Orchen, to support
the Envoy of Tunis, should enter the Grand
they will oppose it in such a time, as it is already too late
in this Country.
   M. the Chamber of Commerce of Marseille, learned
of the seizure of a French Vessel, loaded for
Algeria, captured by a Russian Frigate of 50, under
the command of M. de la Condamine, who was commanding
a Patrol in the [seruice], the Captain of the Cross could
give Account of his Conduct: M. de Barbands
was sailing from Conset with this Vessel - the Waters
they encountered the Russian Captain could only
[bien d'une l'autre m'accompagnant] to prevent [l'ivce]
could [le] M. the War [bon Recourcy]; he added
[negatiement], the Russian sent on Board
[Chabusk] to request to inspect the Vessel the
Merchant, [ne conti] of the [Chabusk] at this Vessel the
promise the [Brestis]; he indeed took it
but the Russian [s'atteignant], went away.
   We also learn that a Russian Squadron
[à 2 Battimonneymens] to the Commerce of Marseille,
the number being reported at sixty or thereabout
for M. de Barbands, he hastens, demonstrates,
that the Vessels brought, [ce me joint],
compromise the Flag.

                                        Brest.
