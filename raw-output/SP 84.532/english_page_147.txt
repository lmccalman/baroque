LETTERS PATENT
OF THE KING,

Given at Versailles the 23rd of January 1771.

Registered in Parliament.

LOUIS, BY THE GRACE OF GOD, KING OF FRANCE
AND NAVARRE: To all those who will see these present Letters:
GREETINGS. Persuaded that one of the most essential of our
duties is to have justice rendered to our subjects, we proposed
to ourselves, by our Edict of the month of December last,
to invariably ensure its administration, & to prevent forever
those interruptions & those arbitrary cessations, which bring
such fatal blows to the security, to the fortune of our people, &
to the tranquility of our kingdom. We should have expected that
the Officers of our Parliament of Paris would withdraw from a law
which recalled them to the functions of their estate, & to the ministry to which they
