February 17

                    Paris, February 26, 1770.

                    The Winter Fleet which
was transporting to Le Havre the Swiss Guards who
[Constituent] Port-au-Prince, has set sail from
Rochefort.
    They are arming at Brest the Fleet [l'Eliées], commanded
by Mr. de Gruyquet, for the transport of
Artillery Equipment to Martinique, and to
have them escorted to St. Pierre, to embark at
Guadeloupe against the Dutch. And the
Portuguese must go to the Cape.
    Our Letters from Toulon announce nothing
new.
    According to the News we have from [M[alta]on],
the Vice Prefect holds only to his state of making
Company; [elle s'afolie repandent être une
Querellées] in the Convent of Mercy.
    It is not yet known if the Canon is
Minister of State; If it is [autant libre] to the
Council.
    There is fermentation among the Ministers,
the Duke of Choiseul seeing that he cannot direct
That, has sought to gain from another, but perhaps
he will not always find the Count of S. Florentin
to [concourir] the Count of the State to the Council
[d'Ehelski]; and the Duke of Aiguillon.
