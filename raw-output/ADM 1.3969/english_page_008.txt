AFTER the hurricane which ravaged all the Antilles,
I embarked on the faith of an Act of Parliament
of London, for the free exportation of the productions of
Grenada, aboard neutral Ships, going to neutral
Ports. This Act made me hope that my voyage
would not be troubled.

The Ship on which I had arranged my voyage, is called the
Holland, that is to say, the Dutchman, belonging to the
firm Jan Jursan & Son, Gressille & Company, of Amsterdam, commanded by Jan PiterssenKrays, & chartered by
Messrs. Tournelin & Portales, Proprietors, absent, of a
