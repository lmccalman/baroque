Paris the 10th June 1786

It is reported from Provence
and neighboring Kingdoms that one is occupied
there only with stirring up the Protestants of Auvergne
the Religious, and the Missionaries.
The Commanders of the three last
Provinces are included in the orders
Concerning the newly appointed
with the Exception of the Prince of Beauveau
for Provence, the Count of Millas
for Roussillon, the Marquis des
Mines for Guyenne, and the
Marquis of Langeron for Brittany.
The Lieutenant Generals had taken
Seven Thousand livres in Salary
and provisionally it will be only once
to the Town, or Region. The
Field Marshals taken by each
Division will provide all the Swiss,
that is to say, three Months each year,
They each have two Thousand Livres
in payment per Month.
