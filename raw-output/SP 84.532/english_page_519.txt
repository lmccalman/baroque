Private Letter

Paris, April 26, 1725.

The new
Parliament of Paris has rendered a Decree,
by virtue of which that in the Parliament
of Rouen of the 18th of this Month, has been quashed
by the Execution of High Justice,
as [l'abesse], [attestant] them
to the Royal Authority, and to the Law
of the Kingdoms, and referred the matter
superior in it, the [Confrements] of the
Emigrants, to render the Fidelity
of the Temple, and to turn them away from
the Obedience due to the Sovereign,
and [s'imme] to falsely attribute
to the said Parliament the Royals.

As there were no Advocates
General Advocates, the young
Advocates Bazaque, Mr Bale, [des]
Henry, having been named for the Conclusions
of the King, Mr Amet, to support four other
[Pleus], from the College of Navarre and [Evres] of
the [Demonstrans] of the University of
Paris of February 18.
