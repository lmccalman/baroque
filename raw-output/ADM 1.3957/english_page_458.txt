543.

                                                                    Paris the 2nd November 5716.

                                    The letters from Toulon
                        of the 26th report, that embarkations continue
                        in this Port, & that the Preparations being
                        made there daily increase.
                            Protestants from England come daily
                        to go to the Environs of Toulon, to the
                        [Battiment de l'Eté] of Grasse, but they have
                        yet received nothing nor anyone there.
                            They continue in Paris to make there the strong ban
                        of Nobles for January, the funds being placed.
                        We shall know through Mon. the Attorney General
                        Martin in Chiefs, without including therein
                        O'Lesdier the Regiments of the Irish.
                            A young O'Brien is drowned at the [tarmie]
                        of Mars, the third one to be a young girl
                        or Boarder, & who entered at the [Ecu] at the
                        Palace of [Martinets].
                            The [Chde] of Tortenelle has departed from Rhys
                        to go carry 230 men of [Repareas]
                        for the Regiments which are at the Islands and
                        will give Notice of the Bourbons.
                            [Monnyer] the 3rd of Grasse has received letters
                        from his wife from the [Tourelle] of Mans, who had
                        found it very Magnificent, and very [altérée]
                        by S. Arnauld.
                            The Count of Ganay has made many
                        important Discoveries, in the Treaty &
                        [s'en déigue] this makes him Feared.
