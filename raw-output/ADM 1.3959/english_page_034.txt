165                    N° 27 Bau N° 2

                                        Paris March 20, 1772.

                                                    It is announced from
Toulon on the 12th, that it is M. de Cuillens who
is named to command the Atalante,
and M. de Mandols for the Flèche.
     At Toulon, as at Brest, they are
not satisfied with the suppression of the Council
of Ordinance: each freely speaks
his mind about it; they find things there
impossible to reconcile, and difficulties
of execution, the Reporter has no longer looked
at everything, but it is easy to repair everything
by a declaration which corporately
restores things to their proper state.
     The New Regulation of the King informs
that several Members of the old
Parliament were Soliciting the favor, to
fill in the New one the Positions,
which would become vacant, it makes of it a
vexing Fate, it is said that none may
ever [recevoir jamais etre suieu] of a Position in
the old Parliament. This article has been
sent to the King, who has approved it.
     M. de Miquel, son of the First President
of the current Parliament of Toulouse, has Solicited,
and has obtained from his Father, to be admitted into
the New Parliament; he has met with a refusal.
