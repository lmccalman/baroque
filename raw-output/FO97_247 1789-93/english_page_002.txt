Rotterdam this 7th April 1779


Sir

I have just received the letter that you have addressed to
me under the supervision of Mylord Malmsbury, in this country
Monsieur Blake has found himself alone by himself according to the arrangements
for the hundred pounds sterling obtained per quarter to be
shared between him his son and Haman, for some time
by the orders of Mylord Malmsbury this money is collected
at Monsieur Crawford's here and Monsieur Blake was to receive
in advance the quarter due the 25th of last month, but
some days ago Monsieur Blake received a note from
Monsieur Crawford in which he informs him that he finds
himself faced with an order from Mylord to discontinue the
payment of this sum so that Monsieur Correy
was surprised that this quarter has not been paid, the brother of
Monsieur Crawford the one who ordinarily resides
in Rotterdam having left for England, the one who
is currently [here], believes that his brother has neglected the the
presentation of this order before his departure. Monsieur Blake
