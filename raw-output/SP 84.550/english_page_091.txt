                                                     Paris the 25. March 1776.

                                                     The great

Deputation of Parliament summoned
to Versailles Thursday Evening, has
returned at 10. O'clock, the King has
given it the Following Response:
   "I have examined with great
   "Attention the Remonstrances of
   "my Parliaments, they
   "contain nothing that was not
   "maturely reflected upon before
   "I determined to address to It
   "my Edicts and Declarations.
   "My Parliament must have seen, that
   "all these Laws have no other Object
   "than to increase Abundance in
   "my Good City of Paris, to
   "deliver Commerce from a Constraint
   "that was very prejudicial to it,
   "and to provide for the Relief
   "of those of my Subjects, who do not
