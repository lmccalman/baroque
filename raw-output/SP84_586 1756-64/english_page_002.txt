And how would it be difficult for us, since we have a Father who calls us there, a Brother who draws us there, a Spouse who awaits us there. But let us fear that some Easters have put into practice for us only its seven days of exterior devotion and that their heart & their spirit have not entered into it. Let us fear that the Christians who have confessed have not reconciled with their enemies, that the young people who have received communion have not separated themselves from their vices, that the penitents who have received absolution have not abandoned their proximate occasions of sin, and that the devout souls who have approached Communion have not put into practice the resolutions that they had made there. Let us fear above all that the parishioners who have had the satisfaction of seeing their Pastor on the Altar & in their confessional allow themselves to grow cold in their good dispositions & fall back into their former tepidity. Let us pray the Good Shepherd to keep them in the fervor that he has inspired in them.
