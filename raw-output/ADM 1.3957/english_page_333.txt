. 269.
                                                    14 September

                    Paris the 27th April 1770.

     It is reported from Toulon
that the Regiments of Bourbonnois and Dauphiné
have orders to be ready to go to Corsica, in
place of the Regiments of Bretagne and Bourbonnais
which must return from there.
     Poitiers has moreover nothing from our Ports which justifies
the Fears nothing justifies the [interprétant] neither at Court
nor in the City. The Young whalers who have returned from
Norway, at Dieppe. Others were going to Russia and
Norway for Fishing, among others the
Princess de Condé, the young Princes should go
Tomorrow to Versailles.
     It is claimed that Madame the Countess
of Provence should come three days to Court and that one expects indeed
all the marks of attention on the part of the Royal family.
     The Minister of Finance has requested so many
Millions from the assembly of the Clergy, to undertake
seriously the payment.
     The Declaration of the King, for the Recall of
banished Priests and exiles is not yet
registered; one thinks that it will be today
instead of Modifications.
     All the letters from Roussillon and Marseille
announce that the arrival of Madame the Dauphine
who had a Fall during her Ordinary Walk;
has happily returned to Court for the
pleasures and not only of her Pregnancy; but
also of the legal state of the Queen, has been confirmed.
