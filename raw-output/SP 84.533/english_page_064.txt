N° 61.

                                             Paris the 26 July 1778.

                                                        It was expected here
I had to announce to you Winds,
Storms and Hail, but none have yet
appeared; No Doubt, that the Committees finding themselves
scattered at the Isle de France, have all
obliged them to stay to await the [liturges] Results,
where there is more than enough to
Detain them, a few more days of time yet.

Great Events are expected,
both regarding our Finances and regarding the Projects of the
Chancellor. The Minister of Finances
is on bad terms with the Chief of Justice, the
fall of D.Miré Genemy is regarded as very
[pour heune].

Parliament has just made a movement
between Spain and France, the Peace of
families is also extended, there is no
need for others.

I am sending you more days that warehouses are being made
in our Ports. they continue
here to have the books that were there sold at [Perle];
meanwhile, it is only that all the
merchants of any. Announcement.

It is announced that one must send from
the Navy to regenerate the land
troops, and all announces to us the Last days
of Peace.                                    On.
