                        Madrid the 20th October 1780.

                                            While awaiting the
Couriers who must carry the last
Responses on the further Propositions
from this Court to that of London,
one is still occupied with moving
troops to all the Coasts where one
can fear an Invasion.

It is also assured in several
Parts all the [Indelants profertes]
to the minister of france, that the treaty
of Subsidies which would bring [vinces]
into this Country.

There have been for quite some time
frequent Councils at the Court, in
which it is claimed that the King [sonde]
D'Orelli: proposed a Plan, to
[tranir] and maintain in this Country
a formidable Army, as well as
to establish and [fortenir] continuously
in all the Ports squadrons and vessels
respectable: and it is said that this Plan
has been approved.

                                                                        Oris.
