The latest Letters from Berlin
in confirming the imminent Departure
of Prince Henry for Petersburg,
give to understand that he is going there for
private Affairs, and that he is going
to request to succeed the Duke of
Courland, who is preparing to leave
Courland.

The King having wished to draw to
himself, and reserve to himself the Knowledge of
the Affair of Mr. de St. Troy, has sent to
his Parliament Letters of evocation
or abolition with the Clause that
Mr. de St. Troy, shall not be required to
present himself for them to have their
full and entire effect. The Parliament
has refused the registration of these Letters
by 40 Votes, 38 were for complying.
The First President went to Versailles
to make Representations
