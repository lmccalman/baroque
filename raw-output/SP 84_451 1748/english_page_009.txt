My Log Book

We also fortunately took advantage of the occasion and we
received, besides a letter from Monsieur Dolomieu which was
delivered to us by Captain J. A. Perier, letters from our Friends
and from our families by this ship, the first which has appeared in these
seas since we have traveled through them. This happy event has given us
much pleasure and we will long preserve the memory of it. After
having taken on water and wood, and having procured refreshments
of which we have had great need, we have continued our operations
which are now concluding in all these seas. Captain
Cook formerly had in these Waters the misfortune of
losing some sailors, whom islanders killed.
all three.

1787

nocturne
