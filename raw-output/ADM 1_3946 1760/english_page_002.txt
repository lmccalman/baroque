R. N. Fal.                 2
                    in Paris the 11 February 1780

       The latest letters from Brest
continue to announce that There is question in this Port
of final operations and armaments as of now
       The Disunion which reigns between United States, the [Villanos],
the Continental officers, between the officers themselves,
and the Troops that one would wish to at bottom establish alone
but as he Proved the Corps not marking them
point the ones the others. The Knight fortunately
hopes that this disunion will cease.
       One announces to be anxious at Orient to not
see arrive the three Vessels of the Company departed
before the Massipar. One also retains with anxiety
the travailleur, which at the Departure of the Massipar the Town of
France awaited only the Deputies of the [Corde] of
Government to form.
       One has shared equally in all our Ports
no Armament but that of Rochefort for
Martinique, and some frigates from Toulon which
go to St Domingo, and namely particular from
Bordeaux for Canada.
       There would be at Orient the 74 about thirty [Balletrop√©]
ready to depart for the Indies, but it is outside
that the [espelle] and the travailleur which are in Roadstead at
Brest, go to the great Indies; one judges this, because
they have taken Eight Months of Provisions, and by
other Dispositions.
       One says at Court that Father Boscote Confessor of
the Queen Queen of Spain, has been arrested, and that he will be
