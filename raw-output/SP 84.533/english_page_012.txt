Private Letter

                                    Paris, June 24, 1775.

                                    The Shareholders
of the Company of the Indies, by their
Assembly of the 19th 7ber, in the Assembly
of April 7, 1770, had been adjourned,
as much as it was in them, the [préliélifs]
of their Creditors, & unveiled the
wounds, that in Compensation for the
immense Sacrifices that they made
to the Royalty, one would have regard for
the agreed Conditions: Today
they want to take no Account of them
of the clauses of the Contract, by the Decree
of the Council of State of the 30th of this Month:
which orders in Contravention of the
treaty; 1st. that there will be paid [l'lear]
Contracts on the [Remettes] at [y add] 5.
percent to the Bearer / of the
Company of the Indies. 2nd. that the
Payment of the Arrears of the said
Contracts will be made by means of the [Reeu
deIgny], on which one will levy
                                    henceforth
