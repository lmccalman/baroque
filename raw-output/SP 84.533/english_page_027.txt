Brussels the 4th July 1779.

          The high price of Grains
begins to make itself felt throughout all the
Lands of Belgium in general failing on
the proportion of Money, has just been
renewed, and published yesterday, the high prices
which reign in the Prices of the environs.
Antwerp, Liège, and in Luxembourg.
It is incredible, every day one
sees here to seek from [tri le month]
to Liège, Carts and Carts
filled with Bread and other Foods,
for need and the poor Villagers.

One fears that the Brabant [Autrisie]
soon the same Fate, Barley sells in
Louvain three florins, wheat 4 florins
30 Sols, and Rye 2 florins 20 Sols. And
other Foodstuffs in proportion.
There is to be feared, for the [Maïsfour]. If
the continual Rains do not cease.

The Mortality which still reigns among the
large Cattle, is such that the [Benewautre]
Village has risen in Price, that one can
hardly have one's necessary.
                                      Etc.
