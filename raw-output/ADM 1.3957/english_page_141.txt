                                                B. N. Royal

                            Paris the 9th April 1770.

                The King declared
the Unity of Parliament in the City and Paris.
All the Parliament at night at one o'clock at
Versailles, the Princes and Peers being already
there in advance; [familles] to a Hall, the
Mr. the Chancellor gave reading of the Edict
and Declaration by a Speech that pronounced
Mr. Seguier before the assembly, on the object of the
Edicts deposited in the Registry, the Commissioners
have been to examine the Procedures made
at Rennes, as incompatible to
be named in Parliament according to
commence the Investigation; and these
Conclusions have been followed. They have named
Messieurs of Brittany to Mr. Monmartel,
to hear the people of Rennes who will be made
to come.

One of the chiefs for the last Action Speech
very strong against the Parliaments, the conduct,
and the Investigations to be well made,
had published by Montaran; the King bears
the Word and says, that the Monitories in
these Circumstances were not necessary,
that they only served to intimidate the
Consciences, and that the Conclusions
are good suitably and very good,
however it was necessary at the Threshold to go to the
