207.

Pelerinouth. There were found this time so many
People at the Palace, that they were
obliged to triple the Guard. The
Marquis de Noailles lent his
House, & they lodged there the [Patte] of those
who left Paris until further
Order.

The great Affair of the Farmers
of Basel against the Administrators
of the Posts, far from its [jaunaison] of being
at Parliament, it awaits nothing more
than the Ratification of the Council,
than that of M. de Noailles;
It is equally M. de Vennes who
must judge in this Affair.

The Benedictines Sent for a
Brief from the Pope, were sent to the
last eight, & all three arrived
Dead in their Cells, & have it at
the Abbey where they represent them.

Divorce in England. Everything
is done, as everything was
beautiful and rare there, so also is it
sold very dearly there. Divorce
for a Married person is so also.
