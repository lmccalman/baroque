                                                G 11 Response

                        Paris the 3rd April 1783

        It is reported from Rochefort
that the frigate l'Expérience, of the [équation] 2d, to
go to [la Hâle de Bennes], transport the Regiment
of Clare [blendien]

        They are also preparing in this Port, the
Count d'Émoroy, who returns from Martinique,
M. de Delecht replaces him.

        It is written from Brest, that they continue there to
dismiss daily the Workers from the
ports, and that there remain no more than...

        The King appoints [Audience Marée] to the
[Embaucheurs] and Foreign Ministers;
& [Menjette] has not left the assembly this
week, She must go Monday to sleep
at [la Meutte], and which gives reason to think that
the King's ministers summon the
[Conséquens] to Paris to install the New
Parliament.

        They do not cease to spread rumors of
every kind, one does not know where one is going;
one hopes for changes among the
Generals, One will see presently formed, &
so near; it is said today that there are
many Members who have written that
They may have their [borde].

        However, & this is certain, that the King
visits all the countryside & common places of
the Work begun. [l'Been] of the People,
                                                [portes]
