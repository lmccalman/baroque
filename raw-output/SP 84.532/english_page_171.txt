N° 12.                                                Paris the 4th February 1773.

                    I have not found them
agreeably new the Mr de Bras; The
Prejudices went yesterday to Marby for
three days, one claims to have seen depart
this evening a carriage for Le Havre with
Fencing Masters and one from the Marine;
It is always the question of the Duke of Aiguillon
for [l'erin], and the treaties of Broglie for
Austria; these two carriages would be
compared to the greatest of the Chamber
of [Arpin] instead of the Duke of Aiguillon,
from [Galarie] to the Nation.

Our Parliament has always made
The Exterior of the Table at the Court the [Raffines]
to The State [amancent], but The [arnet] which
resumes their Service, have obtained, a
Seal until the present has not caused to be [éspoquer] an
Affair, The attacks by the [Biblorates]
Say that they are not ready, that one
awaits for them Pieces, or that These
[Patois] are to which [eteles].

This Parliament by [chantent] all
The [Encherement] of Lamy and others
that [Defenne], but this [minceus].
