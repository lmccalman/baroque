N.º 6.

Paris the 13. January 1776.

Our Letters from Sweden
speak only of Construction Timber
drawn from Corsica, which equals that of
the North; but the Exploitation of it is
[pr.] difficult, one regards it
as impossible. For the rest,
nothing Military in our Ports.

One is still in Expectation
of the Operations of M.r de St Germain;
which one believes very advanced.

The Committee of [Meuvres] not seen
[imprimément] less, [j'ai] has brought
to Congress a Memoir.

The Minister of finances was
in a State to bring there a [9bre]
in the [Vacgge], who have retained
in his Apartment.

[Lès]
