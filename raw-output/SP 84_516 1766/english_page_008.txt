We must all exhort one another
to Moderation and to Prudence,
provision has been made besides as much as
it has been possible, for our tranquility. Let us be
persuaded of the Vigilance that has been employed
so that nothing disquiets us, and does not give
birth to antipathetic Ideas which could
prevent the Effect of good Intentions.
If some noise, of whatever nature it
may be, should make itself heard in the district
of St Pierre, it will be very prudent to pay
no Attention to it, because it
could be premeditated by Design to
turn us away from the unique Operation which
must occupy us. Nothing finally will
distract us from this Moment, which must
decide our Fate and that of our
Posterity.

It is of an indispensable Consequence
Gentlemen our dear Compatriots, not to
lend an easy Credence to false
