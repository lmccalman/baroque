243                                R 15 Ep 1782.

                                                Paris the 8th April 1775.

                                                To my friend, Sir,

         As is Usual, the News
are very sparse, but they are ending, and
it is tomorrow that all the Peers must
reconvene.
         A Master Flambus charged with the Oratory is
for the King so that His Majesty may name the
clerks. They set sail for the Isle of
Havres.
         The Abbot of Espagnes, who was to depart
the 30th, or the 31st, of this Month, will carry the Rest.
         Nothing Departs by the Roussillon nor
[Breue] since the question of my last
of the 1st of the Month that the question of the two young people,
[tenances], that after the Establishments
of the Parliament.
         The King is still at Versailles, His Majesty
is hoping today to go to the Hunt,
and it will not yet be to come to Paris
for the Installation of the Parliament is
delayed for some time, because of the
Parties, who only arrived this day from
Flanders with the King. It is necessary Paris;
The Envoys and the Lieutenant Generals
of the Provinces have arrived there, which joined
to all the Merchants of Frames come
