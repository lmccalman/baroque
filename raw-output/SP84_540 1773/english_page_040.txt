Rotterdam 19 february 1770.

Sir

I have had the honour to communicate
to you the Contents of a Letter from an
Officer which he had from the a
women in a Prussian Camp near
Liepfig. It is desired him to be
discreet, and to continue a his
Correspondence with me; which,
as he was in the Prussian territory,
I have is not very easy, nor very
safe for him, however he has sent
me the following Billet, wrote to me,
as an Englishman.

"Think yourselves well firm on your
"legs in the matter of Hanover.
"Do you understand? Perhaps they
"only want to frighten you; and
"oblige you to keep yourselves quiet.
"I am not yet worried about it,
"and [touche a bien], but you will not carry them
"far, because they appear to me
