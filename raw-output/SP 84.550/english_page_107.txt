During the Session of the Lit de
Justice, the Prince de Conti gave
to the Keeper of the Seals some Protestations
which he delivered to the King. The
Edicts have been able to be [pouvoir] since the day before yesterday,
but the Lit de Justice which will contain
the Speeches, will not appear until Monday.

This is the most essential one and is that
for the Suppression of [Vacandes]
and annual dues of Commerce,
Arts and Trades; it permits any
Individual whatsoever, even
Foreigners, to exercise the Professions
they wish, even several;
All formality required, is to
register oneself with the Police:
Without fees. This Article is much criticized,
in that it is claimed
that we are going to be flooded
with [Etaifs], who will bring us
all sorts of bad
Merchandise, but the purchasers
who will wish.
