in Paris the 2nd October 1708.

Since the Letters from London
announce to us a new Visit from our Enemies for
this Season, we cannot believe it.

There is nothing from Rochefort nor from Toulon today.
The Marshal de Bellisle and M. Phelid de Bagnes are
both sick at Versailles; but it is not believed
that it is mortal, everywhere work is being done with activity
at Mets, to replace and perfectly their Functions for
the War and for the State; the latter especially makes
himself very valuable in the present Circumstances.

We have nothing very interesting from Mr de
Subase; as for Mr de Contades, by the Letters of the 28th
he was contemplating some Enterprise either on Lignitz,
or on a Corps of Allies posted near Paderborn
there was a fine movement in our posted Corps.

According to the general Courier of the
Republic of Holland it was done at Brignales, Monsieur
the Ch. of Cavalry; Here is what is said to hold the
Prince Ferdinand de la Payliere gives us
thirteen [esfranches] for the winter Quarters and provisions
It appears to me that some great
event is feared in Saxony; as for the Russians we
are flattered with great Things and the Emperor they
will be joined by the Swedes and by 30 Thousand
Men who will await them.
