H. D. ch. 102.

                    Paris the 5th October 1778.

                                        Word from Lorient
of the 5th of October, that the 3 Vessels armed
for the Southern lands, have not yet
departed, but that they await only
the lifting of the embargo which was put
in place the day before.

Our Letters from Brest of the same time,
speak only of the Conferences which
continue at the Count of Bettmieux's,
where everyone Agrees that
everything is settled in Parts to the taste of the
Navy, but that one awaits overall
a Decision from the Court, which will render it
all is tranquil at this moment.

It is the same at Cadiz on the 29th
Septr., and it is in the same State of
tranquility, and waiting for what must
be determined for the Department of Brest:
which will serve as a Rule for this one.

The Armaments ordered at Rochefort
continue, all is in full
accord and Contentment, there will depart Monday
the 22nd of this month, a very large Convoy
of all the Vessels which will compose it,
