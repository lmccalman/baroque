177.                                                    Reg. nro No 2.

                                           Paris the 22. March 1775.

                    No News from our
Ports of Brest and Toulon as ordinary.
Our Letters from Rochefort inform only that Messrs
de Ravinat, commanding the Ursus and l'Equipolent,
the Roy having arrived, that departing from Lisbon and Cadiz,
           to proceed to Close the Squadrons.
    Nothing New for our finances; The
Edits registered in the Council; they will not appear
until after the King is of Age of Majority;
and that it will only be at Saint Michel at Mr. M. Dames son,
Controller of finances, a general assembly
of First Bureaus, and of Receivers general
of finances. One would have Worked there on the art
of returning this form to the Commissioner.
    The Chancellor presented to the King last Saturday
the Letter of Parlement which was very Satisfied in
Paris its few goods, and it is said to be of the best
composed.
    The current Parlement was invited by the
City Corps, to find itself today 9 April,
at the Procession which is done every Year the so-
Years, in honor of the Vow that the City has
undertaken, to make every Year in honor of the King.
Difficulty: One does not go out of the Court of Aides
and the Chamber of Accounts to find itself there,
as they have and have had there.
    For 8. Years one had not had to apply
at the Palace, there being several Courts
whose judgments have been applied.
