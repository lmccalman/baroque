Paris the 22nd April 1776.

The King worked,
last Friday with M. the Count Thumer,
then with M. Sr Germain, for the
Military arrangements.

Two Regiments, and 3 Battalions, are
split; that is to say, that outside the
Infantry Regiments in the manner of this
3 Battalions, with the exception of that of the
King which will always remain at 4, there is
not right that changes be made to them.

The Regiment of Provence, 2 become that
of that of Picardy, was given to the
Marquis de Melonchelle.

That of Poitou, 2 become that of
Champagne, to the Baron de Viomenil.

The Regiment of Armagnac, drawn from
that of Navarre, to the Count de Livontheil.

Blaisois, drawn from the Regiment of this
Piedmont, to the Count de l'Isel.

Meurie, drawn from the Regiment of this
Normandy, to the Count de Guilbert.

Maconnois, drawn from the Regiment of
Marche, to the Viscount de Haurroy.

[Sorbi]!
