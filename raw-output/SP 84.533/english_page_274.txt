Brest the 20th October 1779.

The Corvettes the Postillon &
commanded by Mr de Kersaint & the
Perle by Mr de la Marteliere, set
Sail on the 20th, with a good Wind; the 1st for
Martinique, and the 2nd for St Domingue;
this last is charged with bringing back
Mr de Volvire, summoned to give Account
of His Conduct.

The Flore is in the Roads, and is going to depart
commanded by Mr de Verdun; she
will go first to our Colonies of
America, and to Miquelon, and then
to the North of Iceland. M. the Abbé
Pingré is embarked, with a Dozen
Scholars, to make Observations,
and among others the Trial of a new
Clock to discover Longitudes.

The Amphitrite by Mr le Couvrg.
departs & Mr de Volvire Commander
at Guadeloupe.

The Zephir by Mr des Effarts, and the
Blonde & the Etoile by Mr Lombards,
depart similarly for our
Isles of America.
