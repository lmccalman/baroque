17. 26 april N° 1.

                        Brest the 11. April 1770.

                                          We have been waiting
for a long time for Arrangements for the Port
of Lorient, they have arrived.
         Mr. Heygan Engineer in charge leaves consequently
for the Province: They assign Another Officer,
and the Workers are only what they would be.
         We have Marine Infantry in the
Port: They judge them necessary and
the [epalet]. These are the Sage and the Rencontre, &
that the Plans have been changed. There is no
Appearance of any Armament; not even
Cabins.
         It is not at all fortunate for the Trade that the
Seamen are waiting for; for I understand the
splendor; he has the City of Guinea.
         They work on the Wood Storehouses, No. 8 fixes
the middle Building; and they propose to the
grounds to make the [Malaise] of the Sculptors.
         Mr. Duhamel directs his views toward Brest.
         Lorient. There will be Three the one Officers and
Two Aides.
         The Student begins there by Mr. Bacquet, and
Continued by Mr. de Rosy; all departed the Month
of May from Brest.
         The Regiment of [en Poche], & its part by
Mr. Douellière; it is Mr. de la Brighelie who
commands it.
                                                           Paris.
