June 24th (Postscript)
19 May 1817.

I am just informed that orders have been
already sent for equipaging fourteen Sail of the
Line in the Ports of Ferrol and Biscay.

General Reed
19 May B.P.

From my own correspondence and that of
my neighbours in the mercantile Line there is
evident proof that Spain has been armed
in such a manner for a long time past, as
it was not to come forward with a desire to preserve
Peace, and there circumstances have of late
been pushed forward lately with more vigour
than usual, several letters from Cadiz about
that their Ships of the Line were ready for
Sea in that Port, the latter end of last month,
among which the San Salvador of 120 Guns, 8
twelve or fourteen more in such a state of
forwardness as to be ready on a few days
notice. A still greater number were said
to be nearly so at Ferrol & Carthagena, so
that in about a month forty Spanish Ships
of war might be completely equipped for Sea
& twenty more in another month. This
