    The Clergy has built an opening

    The Parliament of Rouen sent to the
King, and to the Parliament of Paris, the Depositions
made against the Duke of Aiguillon. The King
did not wish to see them, this Parliament was
censured by the King, and the ministers differed
Only that it had not sent, nor communicated on the 9th.
to the Council, which by its decree had
annulled all the Proceedings including the judgment by this
Parliament, as well as the Informations
or the Information of [Remestre], with
Prohibition to the Procureur General from speaking
of an honest man in such affairs,
or that it appear or flourish to the Senators
to [lyncher] this Decree, and make the
[Roncience] of the Kings our, and inscribe in
the name of this Sovereign of the Council. One awaits
the posting of the same grievously among the
Bretons.
