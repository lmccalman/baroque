R.48     N.1

Paris the 3rd of January 1778.

The Count of St Germain,
to protect himself from the politics of the
Parliaments, always has a marshal's
baton from the King, and to keep
the Count of Maurepas [en Itam]
in their Operations; he submits
the latter to [la Berle], He wanted
to communicate everything to him, He
had his Approbation, in order
to make His Majesty judge
that He does not want to undertake anything against
his Trust. This Conduct is
all the more adroit, that the
Count of St Germain does not risk
much with regard to the
Count of Maurepas, too little
acquainted with the Matter, to
contradict him much; except for
some Dissatisfactions of
