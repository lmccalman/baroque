Paris the 23rd February 1778.

Our Letters from Toulon,
from Brest &c. announce that there has been
an Order to cease the Preparations, and to
keep the armament, only in case it were all an-
nounced. The sailors all the
Professors, and a heap of others always
in a State, to be armed in first
Order; the Stores had furnished them
with all the necessary.

They sent from Brest a
Corvette to go announce to our
Colonies the Convention signed this
Monday the 6th January.

Our Letters from [touche] 29 January
report, that one is not tranquil there;
there are, as for the present, few of these
ordinary Rejoicings in the
of Squares &c. like Balls,
and [Bâles] in this Carnival;
that amuses after the [Corsés], but
one never perceives too much the advantage
of these People there, no more than in
the Army, the manual activity Requisition
