Drafted No. y

                    Paris, February 2, 1776

                    Our letters from Toulon
and from Brest have announced nothing
of interest.
     The frigate was going to depart from
Toulon, to go disarmed
to Brest, after its return from
the Levant.
     The [Prames et Bois] were
assembled last Tuesday at the Palace
on the affair of the Marshal des
Rochelois, The Prince of Conti,
who protects Marshal Muy is
out! Various petitions have been
withdrawn, it has been decided that the
report on the affair would begin
to be made at the resumption. It is
thought that this could [s'appuyer]
before the Easter vacations,
The First President has at least promised it.
