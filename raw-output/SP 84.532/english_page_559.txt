Letter from the Correspondent
of Sieur Le Comtes.

Paris the 6th May 1773.

The New
Parliament has just wanted to render Justice,
people are beginning to compliment it from
all sides, and the Lawyers and Attorneys
are returning from the hope of making fortune
that they had for the Old Parliament,
and are all delivering themselves to the New one.

Here and in the Expectation of the Resolutions
of this Court, relative to Its
Decree rendered by the Hand of the Executioner,
and to the Council's Edict, which imposes
Silence upon it.

The Duke of Penthievre serves, it is said,
at the King's a Kind of Mediator, between
the King and the Princes of the Blood, it is thought
that the Princes are weary of being
long absent from the Court, and that the
King himself is impatient to see them there
again.
