                                    A le Oct. N° B.

                        Paris the 5th October 1770.

                        According to the Letters
from Toulon of the 22nd arrived yesterday, the Squadron
of Ferrol had not yet returned; they
[fonçonne] them Quarantine.
     No Letter came yesterday
from Corsica.
     Those from Brest of the 30th say, that
since the Letter left yesterday the Frigates
[milles de l'Escu], which have been [déblés] because
[lien] of Vessels at the Coast, even
in the Port the Vessels are not
sheltered from this wind's brutality.
     The 30 Warships and of Cores, and to
Africa, they embark 300 men
all first, and 200 on the Second, as soon as
the two Frigates from Brest, the Zephir, and the
Berre will have arrived, these last two
will bring to 100 men to the Island to
the hotel.
     There is still talk of different [l'oggts]
of the Minister of Finance, but there is
nothing fixed yet. It will only be time
that it is no longer possible to [en avenir], without
anyone knowing what it is about.
     It is still claimed that the King has much
Gold, and that it is the Provinces that lack
[uttement] that he can, and this Rumor does not end
