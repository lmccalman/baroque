Rotterdam the 28th October 1768.

My Lord

I have received the Honor of your
Lordships Circular of the 21st Inst and I
take this Opportunity of Congratulating
Your Lordship upon the Choice which it has
pleased His Majesty to make of your Lordship
for Principal Secretary of State for the Northern
Departments.

I have a particular Satisfaction to think
that His Majesty's Choice is fallen upon a
Minister, to whose Care the Interests of
Religion are Confided.

I hope that the same Zeal and Attention
to His Majesty's Service that have merited
me the Approbation of the Successive
Secretaries of State for 37 Years past,
will procure me Your Lordship's Protection
and Countenance.

I have nothing more at heart than to
improve my Foreign Correspondences, which
have been of great Use in two successive Wars.
Any Command that your Lordships will
design, I humbly beg to be favored, will
be executed with great Care and Punctuality
