                           Toulon the 7th February 1773.

                The [Pluettes] formed of the
Batteries of the Great Tower, or Yellow Place
by Hand, the 4 Companies of Artillery
coming from Grenoble, form for us
19 Companies of the Batteries of the
Gate of [Inflandries], and of the Beach of St Elmes
with 300 Men of the Regiment
of Aquitaine. The Rest of [Mal]
Bourquet above Missiessy, is
well assembled.

                Paris the 15th February 1773.

                They write from Brest on the 4th that
Mr. de Mauris, the new Intendant of
Maritime Classes, that it is ordered
[nel] Order for the Vessels the [Lysis/tres],
the Gloire, and the Belle Poule, always
armed in the Port.
       They have stopped the Towers, but all
the other Preparations continue, and
all to finish putting the
Vessels in State, than to
supply them, [nous douter les]
Port and on the Roads.

                                   N Dury,
