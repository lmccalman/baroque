Madrid the 7th January 1773.

I am assured that the
King of Rome has the charge to terminate
the Disputes with England and
the Couriers are so frequent from
Paris, that one can augur from it a
Negotiation of Conciliation.

For some days several
Couriers have arrived from Versailles,
one of them brought the news
of the Disgrace of the Duke of Choiseul.
There are among these Appearances,
the Dispositions to reconcile
destroy themselves [colterement] with
the same Vicissitude.

They have placed at Cartagena and at
Alicante an Embargo on all
Foreign Vessels, except
the English, to transport
to America a Battalion of the
Regiment of Flanders, and one
from Murcia.

They write to me from [Rorol], that 13
Ships of the Line are found
