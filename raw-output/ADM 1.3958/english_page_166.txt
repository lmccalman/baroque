B 19 April 1821

Geneva the 5th April 1773.

The Letters from Paris
indicate to me, that the Finances being entirely
exhausted, and doubting well that the Parliament
Would oppose with all its forces the Edicts and
the Systems of the Court, to raise Sums
in the Kingdom, for the provision of Money;
[on y fonda la Cour à se le preférer des Parlements];
the effect of it is, that the Parliament of Paris holds a
lit de Justice; that the Chancellor [s'est devenu]
with many; and that they have a considerable small act
at the Court, who work for
them all true

The Polish Roman Affairs are not
the Only ones which occupy the Ministry of
Versailles; one is very attentive there, to the
Proceedings of the courts of one and the other.
Berlin, sees that certainly that the [Suere]
between [Ottley letours] and Spain, is
only a flat [Suere]: and that it becomes
only as much as the [Hommes propritiormes],
continue to be victorious in
those of the lands.
