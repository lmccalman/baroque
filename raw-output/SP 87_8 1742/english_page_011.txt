to have recourse to her in all that she will prescribe him to

you sir that she leaves nothing to be desired, except for
stronger health: her gentleness, her docility, her application
to study, and her happy dispositions for perfection
make her loved and esteemed by all those who have the happiness
to know her: I do not doubt that one day she will become
a subject of consolation for you and for your family, and
a true bride of J. Christ: we pray you sir,
to leave her with us some time longer to give
place for her vocation to develop and to strengthen itself.
I have the honor to be with profound respect
                                    Sir
                        your very humble and very obedient servant
                                    Sister St Louis
                        Superior of the Ursuline nuns of quebec

of the 3rd 8ber 1760

I have finally received sir the letter that you did me
the honor of writing to me on the 15th 7ber 1759. I dare not flatter
myself that it was addressed to me by your dear daughter, whatever
the case may be I am very sensitive to the testimonies of friendship
that you give me: you can count on my zeal
and my affection for all that interests you: I hope
that you will have received the two letters that I have written to you
