H 25 May N° 2

Furnes the 19th May 1770.

There is nothing
new at Dunkirk, except that the
Port deteriorates day by day.

The Two Battalions which were there, [s'con] next
Week [l'argées] for
Two others.


Brussels the 21st May 1770.

Upon the Report made
by Engineers, one must soon
proceed to clean and to improve the Port
of Nieuport, they would like, profiting
from the circumstance in which that of
Dunkirk finds itself, to make it a Commercial Port.

There are Letters here which say, that
the King of Prussia falls so much, that
he remained [Eveque de] was not at all in
a State to dismount from Horse, as
he always did previously.
