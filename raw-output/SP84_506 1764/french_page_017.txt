Whitehall July 25th 1761.

Sir

Having received the King's
Pleasure upon the present state of
matters prepared pursuant
to H.M. Commands of the
of the last Orders, I loose no
time in transmitting them to
you.

In extending the Commerce
with France upon the most
just & equitable footing for the
advantage of the fair Trader, &
the prejudice of the Publick
Revenue, are Objects which
particularly engage the
consideration of his Majty

I am Sir, I earnestly &
recommend his Majty has been tra-
strongly in your Serious
Consideration, not doubting but
you will, with your usual real
[zeal], take the best method
you can, capable of putting it
immediately into execution.

Sam'l Marchwick
