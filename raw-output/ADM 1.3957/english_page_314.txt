18 21 Aug.

Paris the 15th August 1770.

The letters from our
Ports announce nothing of interest.
Some have been received from Martinique which say,
that one is not badly deprived there;
sustenance is quite difficult to provide;
but that there is a [fleuve] which will extend
presumably of Ships; The Dying are
the Sick recover health; The number
of [Suvernes] and the Trees &c. One has been obliged
already to abandon several Districts.
The Count d'Ennery has sent to the neighboring
Colonies, to acquire livestock and some
Sort of Subsistence these Gazette.

That remains there are many Mulattoes, the
Regiments the [Brigans] and of Bourbon which
are in this Island, are reduced by half.

The Fleet of the Street is composed, from
Versailles and from Marly, of 12 or 14 [Jolies],
ready to go incessantly. The [Megeortes]
hunt the 26th in the Plain of St Denis;
went to M. Creon at the Marshal of
Cambrai. Nothing of interest has happened;
The Royal family is well.

A Delegation from the Assembly of the Clergy
was yesterday presented to the King; The Orators
have prayed The King The Recall and The establishment of
Priests banished and detained. It is the 26th that this
