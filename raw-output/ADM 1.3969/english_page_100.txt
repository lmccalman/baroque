the 2nd June 1783.

Paris the 23rd May 1783.

The Disarmament of my
Hostilities does not make one forget the Precautions
to take for the future, so that they
do not surprise at all, nor the State of the Navy at
first Order, the Moment of Peace
will be that of the greatest [Apprécisiations]
-[nement], and of new Constructions.
Already several [Cabinets] are commissioned
at Brest, to go to the North, to
seek the Woods that we have
purchased there, and there will always be
Naval Guards on these [Cabinets], and
such Voyages, can only
serve their Instruction.

As for the Trial of Mr de
Vigny, it is terminated, and the Judgment
has been sent to the King. Mr de Vigny
has not appeared at Versailles, it is said that he
has gone to Portugal.

We are learning to learn that
Mr Suffren has been at Heidelberg in
the Isle de France, and that he must
Rendezvous with Mr de Bussy at Malta

Rayneval.
