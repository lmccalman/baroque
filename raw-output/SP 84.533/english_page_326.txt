Private Letter

Paris, November 18, 1775

The Bishop of the Patriarchal
Environs presently, & to oppose him
to the Project of the Chancellors, which began to
[s'affoetiser] for the [Doblacé] of certain
Rights [troidy] that would be necessary
In a Letter to my [framing] to the
Victims of [l'Evrain], in October of October 1775
the Matter was treated very amply & it
bore as epigraph never is
[Atheme]; our Chancellors resolved truly
they will have

1st. That the [Evêde] in Council, which ordered the
[Reconviction], and that the
Orders of the King would be deployed for the expedient
is not held to it at all, that it was rendered to me
exactly, and that when he would do it, he
should not have more good support
than one, than the Letters of [Paglier], to which
they did not comply in December
and last January; that in a Word, this
instant is the critical Moment, where by
a firm Step [s'offusomationte]
& [l'Evire] on one side the [fou demier] until
present.

L. e.
