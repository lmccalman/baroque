At Brest the 23rd of April at noon.

Our fleet has not
yet departed, and it is the fair weather that
holds it back; there is an almost
continual Calm, and the little Wind there is,
is from the West.

Besides the Inconveniences of a
long roadstead by [l'Omay], the Departure
and the Illnesses, it is feared that
the English might be given the time to leave
and to meet us on the route
with advantage.
