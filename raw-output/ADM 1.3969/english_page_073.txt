Paris the 21st April 1783.

A Courier arrived the day before yesterday
to Mr. Tott Herbert, who
brings him News from India.

The first Courier from the Orient, has
brought only news from Cadiz and Arabia.
More than 20 Vessels have returned from
India to That of France, and the majority
are Officers from Mr. de
Suffren's Squadron. The Battas has brought back to France
Messieurs de Forbin, de Marseilles, Captains
of infantry, and Mr. de La Combe, Lieutenant
of Vessel.

The War Council for Messrs. Ciron
will assemble at Morbihan, because it will be
presided over by Mr. de Guichen.

They are expecting here for next
Saturday, the Count d'Estaing, who has
left Madrid, nothing is yet known
of the result of the Conferences that he has had
with the King of Spain, it is supposed that they
concern the Cession of a Part of
the Island of St. Domingue, or the Island of
Trinidad.
