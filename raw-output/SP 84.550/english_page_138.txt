N.° 28.

                    Paris the 5th April 1776.

                                   I am informed from Brest
of the 27th March, that the Volontaires, the 2nd and the Artillerie,
and the Solitaire, are believed in the Roadstead of the most [tardés]
the 6th April, the other Vessels armed
for war will be armed in the Month of June.
        The Minister has appointed the Officers who must
serve at [Puple generalle] and executed
to the State of all the Vessels of the Port,
to refit them, and in a Word, to
put them in a State to be armed at the
first Moment, the Minister has
appointed the Officers who will draw up
For the Part of the current State of the
Vessels of the Port, this Choice has made
some Discontents.
        Nothing interesting is reported to me
from Toulon
        The last work of the Committee of
St Germain with the King, has fixed
definitively the Bearing of these
functions to the Service and Individuals,
that of the Major Officers of the Places,
                                   &c.
