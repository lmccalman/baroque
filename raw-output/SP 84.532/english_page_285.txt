Whitehall March 12. 1771.

Mrs Neltune                             Madam,

                                        I have received Mrs
                                        Stated Letter of the 5th Instant
                                        &, in Answer to Your Request of
                                        Orders upon St Pierre's last
                                        Proposals, I take the first
                                        Opportunity of acquainting You
                                        that You may by three for once
                                        deliver Drawings from after
                                        Sorting, on Port of the Lat Subs he
                                        asks for that Port, &, promising
                                        him a reasonable Recompence
                                        for any Intelligence of disturbing
                                        Circumstances. You may also give
                                        him Hopes that a moderate
                                        Augmentation of his Allowance,
                                        after the End of the first Year,
                                        will not be objected to, if your
                                        Honours shall appear to deserve
                                        it. But You will at the same
                                        time charge him, that if his
                                        Intelligence are of little, or
                                        no Value, He will not be
                                        continued at any Rate after
                                        the three Months Trial.

                                        I am &c
                                        Dunk Halifax
