Versailles the 23rd Xber 1776.

Mr. de Maurepas

has portrayed me in the Heart of the King, as
the Demon D'Anthoullet par excellence;
the Majorite has [fait feine le Bullo] of
Mentor and Marbro [dans], and to him
made Report, one depicts to him things
the most flattering.

Mr. de Sartines, informed in time,
that Mr. D'Ennery, Governor of St.
Domingue, wanted to submit to the Regime
[de able voit] the Coffee, minister of
the Marine, [afin par sa le Corps d'une]
honored Ranolin, and even flattering
for [feu fidealle]: He has represented to the
King, that the Government, [dans Marche]
of a Capacity too great for
the positions he has had, should
be [conforme la], at least in the
current Circumstances of America.
But that it would not be just to require
the Continuity of Services of this good
Servant,
