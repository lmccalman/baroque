Paris the 20 May 1776.

The letters from our
Ports [depost], had not brought inquiet-
ude about the fate of the Convoys, nor about
A Merchant Vessel arrived
at Marseille, encountered the Division
of Toulon, and brought News
of the good Health of the Duke of Chartres.
The New Minister of finances
arrived yesterday in Paris around noon;
he will be presented this Morning to the King
at Versailles, to take his
Oath.
The name of Monsieur has been
named chief of finances in
the Kingdom, and in this Quality he has
taken Oath; the term [olleur]
General would be [jaloux] given to him;
that is to say, that he could do nothing
without communicating it to his
Chief; which reassures the
Citizens, and revives Confidence;
