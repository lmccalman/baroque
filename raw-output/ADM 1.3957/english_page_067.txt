                    Paris the 10th of February 1770.

                    I send you the State
of our Navy up to the first Day
of the present year. You can count
on the Accuracy of this State, which must
be well distinguished from some others that
I have already communicated.

The Friend from whom I hold it, has promised
me to continue his Zeal to have for me the State of
our Vessels with Precision, but he
tells me that the Exactitude of their [Brodeur] makes
the [Anis] for the present very scrupulous.

My Envoy indicates to me on the first
of the present Month the State in question, in which
