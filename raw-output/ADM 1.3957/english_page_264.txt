265.                                    A.D 16 July.


                        Toulon the 2nd July 1770.

                                    We still advise by
News of the Junction of Messrs. de Broves
to Mr. de Grasse, we know only
by the Letters of the latter, that the King of
Tunis has not wanted since then to admit him into his
Ports upon request, and that the Appearances
announce to us the Full ready to do it.
    But this is not the only Obstruction
of our Squadron, Mr. de Grasse with his
Division re-engaged a Barbary Corvette
which carried sixty men Soldiers and Moors
and Turks [mourir] this Republic, which
was going to settle some Bombs
for having landed at la Cavale which was
carried given an Account of Twelve & Bombs
demanded, on the Subject of Commerce which she
wanted to conduct with the Bey of Tunis in
of our Notes, and the Engagements with Genoa.
    Time will teach us what will come of it.
    The [Commandé] de Fayettes and S. Chebucks
which serves here is at la Villete, [totant] the
Moorings, this Squadron will be ready to depart,
It will be composed of the Vessels The
Toulon and L'Achaque, [Commandé] and [Obliens]
for the Commander, all three carry
White Flag and Pennants, then The which,
Serving their Brigantines.
