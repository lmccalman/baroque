The second art of procedure, at every instant postpones the
end of their sufferings, & by the most fatal of abuses, the means
of securing their property becomes an additional means to con-
summate their ruin.

Finally the prosecution of crimes, the exercise of rigorous
but necessary power, which ensures the peace of the people, is, for
the High Justice Seigneurs, a particular burden. Fear,
compilation, more often still self-interest, suspend
the activity of their officers: hence the escape of the guilty &
the impunity which encourages them, multiplies them.

Witnesses to these disorders, born in it, often lamented at
the feet of the throne, you have His Majesty penetrated by both the
greatness of the evil & the necessity of the remedy.

But finally the moment has arrived when He yields at
least to a part of what Nature solicits science, gentleness, &
to free justice from the delays which arrest it in its progress.

One will see no more in the ministry of choice, than the free
choice of the Prince & of merit which has made them worthy: they
will exercise august functions with disinterestedness which
ennobles them further, & makes them more zealous. Courts
will rise in the provinces, & will present themselves to all
subjects by the impression of hatred, the procedure
will no longer be a destructive scourge: it will everywhere fear
the eye of the public Avenger.

After having been the ministers of the King's beneficence, you
will return, Gentlemen, near him to cooperate in new
designs, & to complete before your eyes the work most interesting
for the happiness of the peoples.
