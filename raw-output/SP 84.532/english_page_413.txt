Finally; if one continued to spread in the capital & in the Provinces; a multitude of Writings which, under the names of Letters, Remonstrances or Decrees; present only bitter censures; false & dangerous principles; & exaggerated pictures, capable of indisposing minds against the government; & the citizen who foresees the dangers with which his country is threatened, dared neither speak nor write in favor of royal authority, for fear of finding himself one day exposed to the resentment of the Magistrates:
