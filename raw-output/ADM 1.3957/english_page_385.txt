Paris the 20th September. 1770.

Our correspondent.

Tunis has not yet returned to Toulon, nor
consequently the French families of the Scales of the Bay that this Vessel had.
There is nothing new at Brest, except
the embarkation of 100 [trayglois] for St. Malo,
of 26 other Vessels from Rochefort.
which must carry [se] to [Sa. Casillomen]
in our Colonies.
The Rg. of Rouergue, following its
march to Toulon [sallonne].
It is claimed that many things must
depart for a [Hôp.] Cape of Good Hope
relating to the [Indianes]. The supplement of troops that are being sent there is attributed
to two diverse objects, either for State Bills,
or Bank Bills also the packet,
[le payon] all the [Pates] of the King. must
wait while trembling, but it would be quite
astonishing if Confidence were
totally lost.
The Duke of Choiseul has left Paris to
go spend 10 days at his beautiful estate of
Chantaloup, with fine Companies. The Duke
d'Aiguillon has made the same arrangements for
Rueil, the Duchess de Grammont 4. in
[tournées], in return for Days at Bourges.
