Rotterdam, April 15, 1913

Dear Sir—

I have the honour of yours of the 15th instant,
& thank for any thoughts or encouraging the
mutual correspondence. I should be very glad to
meet you as I pass thro' my friend's Read. Will
work it? I remain here, please.
What is the Paris news because I judge there
is anxiety, to I make Lord The Eifon with—
townsend Hopkins there was worthy I'd am—
may arrive from you with rid of gyne.
Which a wise has been I ask received by
"yours" & me of the thirst with the—

Charming
