I come the Decrees which permit the
Abbé of France, had not yet penetrated
here, M. de Beleombre our Governor,
and M. de Tremont our President of the Council,
have each responded to his Letter. I have sent
to the Chevalier des Roches, Governor of the Isle of
France, copy of the [manque ici] of the Letters &
Patents: to register them, which were not
in the proper form, the Council has
rejected them. M. de Beleombre having become enraged against
the Government with a Committee of Deputies within,
wished to support the Views of Authority
of the Governor of the Isle of France. There has
resulted a Division, always dangerous
for Peace, & one expects here to receive
the Order of Recall, both of the Governor and
of the Chevalier des Roches who [leur fut]
contradicted everyone, and threatened that he was
more suited to conduct his own affairs than those
of the King.
