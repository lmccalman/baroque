This Parliament being charged with making
an Inquiry and Investigation into the
expenses of M. de Harvilliers. M. [ce] de
Chastellux came absolutely his Payment
in form, and the Copies and [Recherchement]
that there were, brought with him his certain
[Cartelier] and Innocence, in which he
rendered himself with difficulty.

[Le Midy], [fon] the Estates of Brittany
were held. The King gave his Response
to one from the Duke of Aiguillon, to avenge
the Breton Nobility, who found itself
injured in the Memoirs of the Duke...
All that is not a finished affair;
The Duke of Aiguillon himself appears
to want his journey.

It is said that [Mesdames Mespoir] of
seeing [joli] Parliament of Paris, sent to
proceed with the probationary Estates for the Duke
of [Parthieure], and it is added that he is
charged with doing it.

The Abbot Thoras, has just had given
Two Million and a half to go to
pay the Salaries of those of the
King's Household; we will give
positions, and it will facilitate some
times or after to see in what
