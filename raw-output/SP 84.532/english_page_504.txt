The Chatelet to hold today the Election
of Paris; That of the Elections of Versailles
has been sent to that Paris [de l'ion], there one has
been sent also; for fairly [asses]
assembled Tuesday; to deliberate on it; one goes
the company finally resolved, to send
Deputies to the Attorney General, to
request until Monday to
decide; the Attorney General led
these Deputies to the First President
who did not want to take that upon himself, the
wrote about it to the Chancellor; from his place the
Justice replied that he consented to the
Request of the Chatelet. One does not yet know
what was deliberated yesterday.

The two New advocate generals
were received the day before yesterday; despite the 3rd
Members who left, and who are
exiled, the Parliament is complete;
and there are more than 40 Aspirants to
enter it.

I am extremely vexed at so many
those who procure it, there is always
a crowd of Curious persons in the Halls
and until now the Decrees that have
been rendered, have been much applauded by
the Spectators.
