N. 12 Janv. 1821.

Toulon, December 30, 1770

Mister Bourret,

Lieutenant General of the Galleys came
here by orders to have armed without
delay the Forts and Batteries
of the Port and Roads, which is being executed
in haste; 7 Artillery
Companies are being assembled there, there will be one of the
Ships to put in Chamorin.

We reassure, we assemble all our
Workers, [fa transporter à armer
sur armee barre], [on falotte au
premier jour] to raise the
barrels of powder and to see the
Heavy artillery descended from the Warehouses of
the Sailors and [bonte ce que vous
neressayoire].

The General of the Beach [deparitrenal],
and will position himself more on the East.

The embarkation from Paris is
[autre icy], [seulesse] the orders to
go to Marseille.

[Mois ne nous de porter à Chamborde
l'arquire commandé dans Cateda].

Brest.
