Upon his arrival on this Island under the pretext of some tricks of his profession which he applied himself to making mysterious, he soon raised up a Troupe of young boys & girls of the vilest rabble from which he formed himself a troupe, he said, of actors, where the women were the greatest number, & from there he begins his game which he continued until perceiving the risks he took the decision to put himself in chain mail. This character does not limit himself to these debaucheries & employs in addition other crimes among which one can count that he knew how to play the honest citizen, and take into his employ a great number of people & at good price the indocile and the pure ones that good people have known him for several years, and one knows that with the remnants of an enterprise of purchasing tithes which he had established for two or three years, he found himself at the end of the year one thousand seven hundred sixty & eleven embarked in a certain number of operations & accounts of war which it is fitting to say with a precaution of the success of forming accounts of this commerce, and of receiving his reward to the detriment of a commerce of war, and of inciting and detaching to this retreat with rights & means of his doing alone what the service and exercise of petty women can have, for the harm of affairs: His capacity as he supposes, if one can say in this may be he would not abuse it and he gives place to a very great mistrust one says particularly the times are consequently infinite or as assuredly, such is the situation where he finds himself thus avowed so that his induction has not given him time to gain a foothold
