No 4

                                          Paris the 7th January 1775.

                                          Order of Marshal de Broglie

that the 4 Line Orders depart for Brest in the Current
course of December. H.M. has them Very ready.
    We will continue to have the Battalions of R.ds
depart from the Garrisons to have Echeloning on the Route
up to and in parts up to the Isle of Groumme
Embarked in the vessels and at Toulon.
The Batteries are in Order; there are many
Cannoneers and About 50000 men of troops without
any Orders to arm themselves. The [Paqueries]
and the Rigging are going to depart for the Levant.
    Request from Brest of the 2nd, that this same
Jourdain has been the Support of the 64 vessels there
to arrive; it is this Corps which is charged
with the Land Batteries that are going to be armed, they
strike its 2 forts.
    The Opinion where the Queen would await me that the
Departure to leave, the Zephire, the [theve], and the
Belle Poule are in the Roads.
    The Army has just arrived in a
great Subjection. The Duke of Clyston
is by [riguette], on the Contrary. There is Port
in abundance. There are only like the [Espotles]
and the [Royalties].
    We are going to begin to prepare the
forts of the Vessels.
    We have News from Martinique,
that the Prize, departed from Brest to bring
to us the to prepare them for War, has made
