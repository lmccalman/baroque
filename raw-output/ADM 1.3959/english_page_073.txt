R 20-392 No 2.

By the New Correspondent.

                                                    Brest, September 6, 1778.

                                The Count d'Estaing has
arrived here on the 4th of this Month, he entered
our Harbor, followed only by his Ship
the Languedoc, which brought him the [Zuaux].
  The last one who will leave for France, if the
Minister, in his Recall, approves of him for
affairs, will have without the news in which
we are, I will send it to him, but his destination
is likely to command the squadron
of the [Cabales]. Indeed we have had requested
from this squadron the rumors that hold, until
our firmness is sufficient.
  M. the Count d'Estaing went with difficulty
To bring to the Ordinance, which offers him
the Liberty to innovate, he is truly
the most eloquent of temperaments, he knows
thoroughly M. Morris, he hopes confidently
To know his Relations, in which the Count will be
in a State, that one practiced in any other
Port, which must cost all the fields that
interest his Person: - One is full of
what contains here only, that the [Couroncey] facilitated
to learn our annals & perhaps
