DETAIL of what took place at Besançon
concerning the suppression of the Parlement
& the creation of a new one.

ON August 4, 1771, around eleven o'clock in the morning,
it was learned in Besançon that M. d'Ait Grosbois who
had been for two days in one of his Estates near
Auxonne, had received there a lettre de cachet which
exiled him to Grosbois.

It was learned at the same time that M. le Maréchal-Duc
de Lorges, who had been at Balançon for several
days, was arriving in Besançon with M. de Ballard Commissioner of the King, to suppress the Parlement there the
next morning.

On the 5th, from four o'clock in the morning until seven,
the Major Officers of the Place distributed to all
the Presidents, Councillors & King's Men, a lettre
de cachet which ordered them to go to the Palace
at eight o'clock, & forbade them to undertake any
deliberation there before having heard the King's orders.

At eight o'clock everyone having gone to the Palace & the
Chambers having been assembled, M. le Maréchal &
M. de Ballard entered & took their seats there. Then M.
le Maréchal taking the floor said: Gentlemen we
come to bring you the orders of the King, our sovereign Lord & Master; M. de Ballard will
explain them to you. One after the other each of the Councillors
asked if any of MM. the Councillors had
