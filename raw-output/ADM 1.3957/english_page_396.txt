                    Paris the 1st October 1770.

                                        Here is the extract of a
Letter written from Lisbon to Paons, & which
arrived by way of England, relative
to the Count of Lima & Carlos, of which you
the Reader Curno favors Phelippi.

The Maurice Francois le P. Jean Baptiste,
commanded by the Sieur de Livredec, Knight
of St. Lazarus, of 300 tons & of 10 [pres]
of crew; left our ports, at the end of
January, for the French [Pagavements];
the Governor of Pondicherry, and two
Englishmen who had declared to him & the Treaty
of Commerce that the Subjects of His
Great Britain favor with the Coast
of Coromandel; the Sieur d'Orvilliers, being
in the Gulf of Bengal the 2nd March 1769;
landed at Tanaorville (Mayfield, Jupiter and
Pondicherry,) passed the Strait of Malacca
to the North Islands Philippines, to
go to the Discovery of the Islands of
Zealand. He discovered some
which were deserts, and others
populated by black Islanders &
savages, then he continued to run from
Strait which he inhabits to the winds & to the others,
behold him at least from the Indians in the
Design to discover other Islet of David,
