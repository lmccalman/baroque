Private Letter

Paris, March 10, 1775.

It [bredhasitherera]
that the Edicts hasten the Edict of Paris, but the fact
is that they have Jostled the Parliament
General, without one having brought there powerfully
what one has arrived, one claims that the King
will come to make them foot in his bed
of Justice.

One speaks anew of the Reform of
the Hotel [termy] of the Street of Comptroller
General, One adds that the Bishop [former]
against him from the Beginning and some
Certain, is ready to burst, that one accuses him
of being the author and the Edict would have had
some Hundred Millions, as much for the
Reimbursement of the charges of the Officers
of the Parliament, as for the Payment
of the new Magistrates, and to
[rejouitte] money among those
upon whom he has cast his eyes for the
formation of his new Parliament,
the Comptroller General has declared to him
having no such extraordinary money Millions
to give him.
The Chancellor [revienthont] from this Refusal, has
