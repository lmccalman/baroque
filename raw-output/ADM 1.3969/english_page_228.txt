R 2 Oct. N.Y.

At Philadelphia the 12th September 1782.

There will depart from Cartagena
Two Ships of the Line under the Orders of
Brigadier Instonabal, charged with carrying
to Constantinople the customary Presents
after a treaty of Friendship, such as we
have just made with the Sublime Porte.
Ship's Ensign Beaulieu
must have been received. You will await another
General, who will be no less well
received, it is Don B. de Galvez; a
Courier informs us that he has just
anchored at Cadiz, with the army from Havana,
from which he had departed the 16th July, On the
Ship the St John. Shortly will embark
the greater Part of the Troops On
36 Transports. A Vessel arrived
a few Days before Him, had
announced to us His Departure, and had
given us the unfortunate News, that the
Dragon, a Vessel of 64 Guns, had been
lost On the Shoals of the South of Campeche.
