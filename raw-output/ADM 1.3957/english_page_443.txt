18th Nov. No. 1.

                                    Brest the 20th October 1770.

                                            The Frigates the Flora
to apply that which had been planned to be
maintained at sea, and new
orders which arrived since yesterday:
carry to arm with diligence, and in
war, these two frigates, thus as one
works on them Night and Day.
        The Plans that the Spaniard made to [contreuser]
in this Port; 18 Thousand Sailors of which
commanded, they arrive there from all the
Ports; one works without Respite to
prepare the Provisions for a Cruise
of 20 Vessels and 8 Frigates. The two
Small Cruisers await only the Order
to depart.        The King assigns
a Vessel to the Marquis de Durforth, and
the Flora for his younger son.
        All the French frigates,
and notably in this Port, are at
sea with the [flammes jaals] at the
Dignity.
                                                                        Parvis
