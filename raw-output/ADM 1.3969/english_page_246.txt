                                Hy 12 deb.

Paris the 29th September 1780.

They are definitely arming
at Toulon, and Mr. Mithaud Lieutenant of
this Port, must leave from here today.
They are recommending at Cherbourg
to throw by Sea the [Caufes] which must
serve for the Foundations of the Forts; they
dare not yet promise a full
success, they fear that the wind may not
be good enough to push the Masses
so heavy, and that the first Blow
of Sea, may destroy again these great
Works.

All the Officers who were to
assemble at St. Brieux, for the
Council of War, had arrived before
this Time, but Mr de Guichen having
been obliged to return to Morlaix,
to review the Trial of Mr de Viguy,
the first session of the Council of
War, only opened at St Brieuc on
the 10th October. Awaiting the Nomination
