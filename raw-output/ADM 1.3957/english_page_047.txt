Paris the 29th January 1770.

                           The two Documents on
council are making the greatest Sensations, and I
send them to you. Tomorrow will be sent to you one
of the last Fridays in Parliament the
Chambers assembled, Commissioners will be proposed
to settle the Measures that must be
taken to arrive at Peace. I depart
Tomorrow Tuesday.

The Chamber of Accounts and the Court of Aides
will also assemble Tomorrow for the same
object; but the new Letters received today are
not too favorable to such the two
objects.

This Reduction does not furnish an Object
as important as it had been believed, and it
has been said that the 48 millions that the council of
the late King had evaluated made [faisait] the sums that
have many of our Offers.

Representations of the Parlement on the Subject of the
two Edicts to levy states in Substance:

Parlement has proceeded to the Registration of
