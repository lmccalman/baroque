Vienna, December 11, 1784.

Last Friday the 3rd of the current month,
arrived here a Courier from Paris,
dispatched by the Count of Mercy;
I am assured that he brought new
Proposals for an accommodation,
namely, that the Dutch must reopen
the Scheldt to all Nations; that the
bordering Provinces will be free; but that
Foreigners will pay to the Company
a modest Toll; without express condition,
that the Imperial Court commits not
to send any Merchant Vessels to the
East Indies, and that it renounces
this Commerce.

Monday the 6th of the current month, departed
from here a Courier for Paris, with the
Emperor's Response; one awaits that
