The Count de Maillebois, named Commander
in Guyenne, is not yet declared; it is the
Marquis His Brother who will be, and they will believe the
Parlements and the nobility at that Bordeaux.

They report from Clermont, that all the
[paillages respettajesutis] of the Superior Council
of Auvergne, Are [vagaronne].

The Bailiwicks of Angers, and of [Segoyets]
are also equally nothing, they were supported
by an Edict, and rendered new.

They were to do as much in the Country
of Guienne, but in Consequence of the
Remonstrances of l'Hôpital and, they no longer have
need to make them obey.

The Duke de Duras Solicited at the Court,
though in vain, the Survivorship of the Position
of First Gentleman of the Chamber
for His Son; they say on the contrary, that they
take from him the Command of Brittany,
and that it Will be filled by the Count
de Brülles, Brother of the Marshal of that Name.

The Government of Paris, should it
become vacant, Will be given to the Marshal de Richelieu,
in Place of the General Government of
Guyenne.
