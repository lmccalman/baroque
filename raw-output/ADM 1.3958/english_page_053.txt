21.                                                  17 9 Feby No 2

                        Paris the 5th February 1773.

                                    There is nothing from Marly
nor from Rochefort nor from Orleans nor; we
still only speak of the departure and of the Chancellor.

It is assured that the Duke of Aiguillon
received, yesterday evening, a Courier from London
but the King forced him to, that is to say that one
makes jokes about it because His Majesty
appeared

The great Project of the Chancellor for
the restoration, so unfortunately inspired
and driven by this Project of having
another three years, for which all the rights
of the People were reduced under the sole
Council of the Sovereign, reduced in
every Parlement, which according to Barrin,
according to the Court, assizes of the Sovereign
Councils in all the great
Cities of the Kingdom.

The pardon given shortly to the new
consistory, has dismissed the Solicitors,
one will no longer need Solicitors,
each Party will furnish the Memoirs
defended by an Advocate; there will only be
provincially three Courts in the Kingdom,
by this means the Lawsuits will not last
long, they will no longer ruin the
Parties                                                   One
