17 July No2.

                                    Paris the 29th March 1770.

                                        Henry we have received from
our good friends
    The Aldermen and Peers were assembled Tuesday
solely for the matter of property; but there
was nothing decided, it is expected that Mr.
Hulet of the City administration will give sight
of the Memoirs that he must present to the Council
and that it will be held on April 3rd to deliberate.

    At the Moment that the Chambers were about to
Separate, the Grand Master of Ceremonies
entered with orders from the King for the Letters
of Justice for the next day, and indeed
they were held The Parlement at this
News remained frightened, and there was Protestation
against all that could be done there &
against the Letters &c.

    The next day Wednesday they assembled in the
[grs] at eight o'clock, and Commissioners were named,
two ordinary Councillors were sent to Versailles.
The Letter of Justice began
at 10 o'clock, the Chancellor announced there the
Will of the King on the affairs Raised with
Duke and Peers, and Peerage, three
Edicts were then read, The first Contains only the
recall of the reading, and its registration
& Sending strictly, the King orders it,
and They were registered.
