18 the Aug.t N.o 1

Cadiz the 25th July 1782.

Dom Antonio Osorno,
Lieutenant General of the Naval
Armies, has just brought to this
Bay, with Twelve Ships of
the Line, some light Vessels
and three Fireships. He must follow the
Instructions to a certain Height.
This Fleet has taken for Ballast one
Month of Provisions, and yet it is
claimed that it will be disarmed
at Ferrol.

At the moment when Don Alexo
was about to leave Livorno, they
were sending two Ships of the Line
to the Bay to fetch there the Cadiz
and the Troops which must be even
more considerable, than what we
have just seen arrive; why
