EXTRACT
FROM THE REGISTERS
OF THE PARLIAMENT
SEATED IN ROUEN.

From Tuesday 5. February 1771.

THIS day, the Court, all the Chambers
assembled, deliberating on the occasion of
the current state of the Parliament seated in Paris;
Considering that the conformity of duties
which unites all the Parliaments, & which ins-
pires in them uniform sentiments for the main-
tenance of the Laws & the constitution of the State, would
have led the Magistrates who compose the said Court
to protest sooner against the acts of violen-
ce exercised toward the Parliament of Paris,
if it had not hoped that the multiplied efforts
of this Parliament would have finally dispelled
the surprises made to the religion of the Lord
King, & stopped the effects of the evident project
