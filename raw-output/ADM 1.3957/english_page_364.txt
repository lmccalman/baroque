September 24, 1770

                    Paris, September 24, 1770.

        The letters from our Ports
announce nothing of interest; It is the same
There is no question of any Finance Projects
up to now.
        Despite the abandoned Houses that there
are in Bayonne, those that remain do
commerce well there, Wine continues to increase,
it has returned here to 18 and the Islands, and it
is probable that it will go even further there, so much does it flourish.
        The King has ordered [a malversation] in
the [magazin malle] of Pachas, they had
announced in Bayonne the names of Villegas
for the Bonde and Mazel project, and one fears
that she has gone to Bayonne until now. Madame
to the King orders.
        The King has disposed of several vacant Benefices
in favor of the Members who
held the last Assembly of the Clergy
of which you will see the List in the Gazettes.
        Madame the Dauphine, Madame the Countess
of Provence, and Madame d'Artois, came last
