ON THE EDICT OF DECEMBER 1770.

THIS Edict is only the faithful
expression of the political constitution
of the Kingdom. One sees a Monarch
Legislator who is willing to per-
mit that the laws be examined
before their publication. The body of
Magistrates to whom he addresses them is
their depository; it is charged with having
them executed, it must render justice. It
can enlighten, remonstrate; but its
power always remains subor-
dinate & dependent on the Prince. These
are the essential characteristics of a
moderate Monarchy the most perfect,
such as it subsists in France for
several centuries.
