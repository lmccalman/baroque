18 20th October 1790

Paris the 18th October 1790.

Our two Galiots,
with Bombs, and the Flute la Franjonne Have arrived
from Tunis at Toulon on the 8th of this Month; L'Hirondelle,
le Singe, le Surveillant, have anchored on the 2nd in the
Roadstead, their Crews expire on the 11th. November.
La Bellone will arrive on the 9th or 10th, she will only take on
[Cènes]. L'Atalante will enter on the 12th.
into the Port, If she does not return to carry
the Orders of the Court to Mr de Bordes remained
before Cadiz. I am unaware What Mr de Bordes will do
being Alone for the Coasts, Have returned
home.

The Regiment of Castillon Swiss, has refused
to cross to Corsica, it remains at Toulon. [Me y allesa]
tomorrow with Mr du Roy; we will spend
eight days there at Avignon to refine it.

We are advancing greatly the Lists of Reclassification.
The Marquis de Verrilles Being in London
has been Charged with negotiating. If the talks with Spain
through England succeed; The King of Spain
and England [établirosement] terminated.
O'Kellet[ableau], O'Kelle[se] the Dispute
will remain neutral.

The [Paullele] of England in this
affair, is to bring the United States of America
to maintain the Poverty, of the less poor
[le sçéur].

An extraordinary Courier from Corsica
departed on Tuesday the 29th September has reported to the
Court
