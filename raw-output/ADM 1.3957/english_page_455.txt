337.
A 9 Nov.r N.o 1

Madrid the 30th October 1770.

I am writing to you from my
bed, where I am confined, to tell you what
I have learned.
Orders have been dispatched to Ferrol and
to Cartagena, as well as to Cadix, to arm
Without Delay all the Warships
that can be armed, and already named persons will be sent there
on the 17th of September.
Quantities of troops are being assembled in Catalonia, in Galicia,
in Valencia, and at Fort St. Marie to be quartered there, this
Number is reported to be nearly 40 Thousand
Men in all. Four Governors
to command them are already appointed.
One Battalion of the Spanish Guards
and one of the Walloon Guards departed
from here Monday and Tuesday for the Port of
Ste. Marie, with Orders to make all
Preparations to embark at the first
Moment of Royal Carabiniers.
Although these orders in the Manner.
Eight Thousand Grenadiers of Militia have
Orders to proceed to Galicia, and all
Officers on Semester have Orders to
Join their Regiments.
On.
