VI.
The Officers of the Bailiwicks shall be received & shall take oath
in those of our Superior Councils to which they shall be subject.

VII.
The finance of the offices of Clerks, Attorneys, Bailiffs,
shall be fixed by rolls determined in our Council.

VIII.
Our Ordinances, Edicts, Declarations, Letters patent
shall be, following custom, addressed to our Parliament of Paris, to
be verified there, & after their registration sent, by our
Procuror general in our said Parliament, to our Procurors at said Superior Councils, to be by them published at the hearing,
without in any case being able to deliberate upon them, nor to dispense themselves from executing them. Our Procurors at said
Councils shall be bound to send said Ordinances, Edicts, Declarations
& Letters patent, to the bailiwicks & royal seats of their jurisdiction,
which shall certify said Superior Councils of their publication, & our said Procurors at said Superior Councils, shall give
notice, to our Procuror general in our Parliament of
Paris, of the publication made both at said Councils and at the lower
seats.

IX.
The delays & forms of proceeding at said Superior Councils,
shall be the same as those which are observed in our Parliament
of Paris; & which are prescribed by our Ordinances, until the
regulation that we propose to give on this subject.

X.
We reserve to ourselves to provide, by particular regulations & by way of evocation, for the subject & discipline of said
Superior Councils, & the good & prompt dispatch
of Justice.
