Paris, February 10th, 1784.

There is nothing new
from our Seaports.
Mr. de Markoff, Minister of
the Empress of Russia, who had
come to sign the Peace Treaties has
taken leave of the King, and is preparing
to depart. The Persons who know
that there has been a Commercial Treaty
between Russia and France, seeing
that even after the Signing of the
Treaties, Mr. de Markoff was leaving here,
imagined that he would be charged
with this great Work, but it appears
that there was no question of it,
and we will perhaps remain, for a long time
in the same Situation, and with
