private letter

Paris, December 17, 1775.

Sieur Deshayès
General Secretary of the Farms, is made
First Clerk of Finance, with
15 to 18,000 £ in salary; he has
all the confidence of Mr. Turgot. The Sieur
General, has delegated to him the department
which is that of Commerce: he is regarded
as a Chief. [Courcre], it is said that there will be
reforms in the assembly
a Multitude of Employees, whose
position would have been formerly the
Masters of a particular Registry,
and would have formed and would have [fortit] rights,
of new ones, and of [d'Oûd]; the [t'en]
different ones.

The Legislations of the Affairs of the
Parliament of Paris, are not here
except from the Knight, to see the King has
deigned to, he has imagined exiling
the Prosecutors, and intimidating the
[aviliès], finally the Parliament is spared
that it has orders to receive [limp]
[recevoits] and to close the Robes.

[signature mark]
