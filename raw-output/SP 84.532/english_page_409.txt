If in a monarchical State, one were to see the intermediate rank disappear, if there were no longer a depository of laws, nor a political body to announce them when they are made, & to recall them when they are published; if the Prince no longer knew any other rule than his caprices, & the favor of the moment; if the Monarchy would change into despotism, & the State would become a theater of revolutions.

Monarchical power would break against another reef, if, under pretext of preventing the abuses of absolute power, the Monarch allowed the least portion of his supreme authority to be usurped. This fatal condescension would infallibly introduce trouble & confusion into the State; one would begin by disputing over more & less; one would attempt the royal prerogative; then, cabals & factions would form which always degenerate into civil wars, & at the end

If a body of Magistrates, which in a moderate Monarchy, has been established by the Prince, only to help him & to obey him, did not render him the title of sole Legislator, yet claimed to possess, in fact, the legislative power, from the simple right to propose the law, if by Polish authority, or the faculty of the [Ara] of the Poles, or the faculty of hindering or preventing the legal publication & execution, this faculty could doubtless only be exercised with knowledge; but it would be necessary, as for [olérée-le-confident] Henault, that the sovereign reveal the secrets of an administration of which he is the sole depository; this would be an evident attack on the constitution of the State.
