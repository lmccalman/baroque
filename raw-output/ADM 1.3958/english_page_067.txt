78

It is shown from [loubon], that the two
labeled parties have the order to touch
at Malta in passing.

Nothing has happened in our
new Parliament, it still does
nothing, or little; the crowd of
Curious people who went to the Palace has diminished,
they have apparently vaulted the [Persles].

It must be taken as certain that the King
will come soon to the Palace, to
have the new Code registered which he
awaits impatiently.

The Parliament of Rouen was received
on the 4th; the chambers assembled
that day in the morning; the Postponement which
is long, was continued to the next day.

What has transpired until now, is
that there was a long and vigorous
speech by which the 2
Remonstrances in the form of Letters
to the King were determined, in which they protest
against all that has just happened
with regard to the Parliament of Paris
both in substance and in form
they end by requesting a
Convocation
