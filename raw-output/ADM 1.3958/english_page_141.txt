Brest March 19, 1771.

We are currently
in fact quiet, there are only the Ships
that are working, for the Provisions, it is
seen that we have a great quantity
of Salted Meats and Biscuits. In fact, only
Beer, in very small quantity.
We have ordered a new distribution
of 600 [Mulattes] from the Garrison
of Roscoff.

A Frigate is being sent which will work
at the King's [escarmouche], and that is all their
Work; this [escarmouche] is
in [Plangues], which will be between two
foreign vessels and the Merchant Frigate,
destined for Merchant prizes, these Foreign vessels
Will serve to make the Topmasts
and Yards, they each have 20 Feet
of Length.

There are no more here, the Workers
are in the Harbor, our Vessel
[devance] long [Solemnes] take
the [esclut] of the troops, and these people [deſ
rivement], it caused the Workers to be removed
in [Pierne]
