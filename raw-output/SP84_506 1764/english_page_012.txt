Paris, June 29, 1784.

the embarkation in our Ports.
The King had recognized a Charter of
Horse of the Hunt. He has [Classe] legally
at the general Chamber and at the Chamber, that has not
prevented him from coming to go to the Salutation.
M. Bruslé D'Alison, Gen. Syn. has composed for
a Burn that happened to him at carnival.

The Shareholders of the Company of the Indies
held on the 27th their General Assembly, and
have named 30 Syndics, who are the Duke of
Durot; the Marquis de Castries; the Count
d'Herouville; the Marquis de Saussay; M.
Boisson Councillor of the Parliament; Messieurs
the Controller de la Bouvrie; Deputy of Commerce
Paris; Delteil; Dupuis of Rouen;
Rouanet General Inspector of the Indies; Dubuisson
Syndic of the King; Béliard; and the Mayors
Ship Owners of Rouen. These Two Syndics
will choose some Directors who will do
all the work, and will give Account twice
a Week to the Syndics.
