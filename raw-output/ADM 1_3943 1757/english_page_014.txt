in Paris the 22nd July 1757.

The Letters from Toulon of the 18th confirm
the Capture of the frigate the Nymph. It is hoped that Spain
will procure for us the exchange of Captain Cartel. Three Frigates Three
the holes of a Port, more the ten or Majorca.
The Letters do not confirm the Capture of an English
Warship in the Channel of Malta.

The Intendant of Rochefort is working to show him
good clothing as soon as he has some.

The Letters from Rochefort of the 18th speak only of
the arrival of M. de Chaffault, and of the fourteen [brassés] in Africa
by a Vessel from Gibraltar. He proposes He awaits dear. This
O. Town of York that one has just sent through the [desserte] that one
believes today this small Squadron of the Islands of to [Eluer]
joined to M. des Borrée and Martinique for there an expedition
Against Montferrat of which to take some [Béguinages]
which intrigue & the Inhabitants for a long time the Islands of M.
Sea and Land to the great Coasts, the the [relaches] every
15. Days at Messina where they are instructed. Indeed in
the Forest of Creux a great quantity of ladders and
fascines, which leads one to believe that one wants also some from Jersey
and from Guernsey to retake this Island. In the
Times when the winds do not permit the vessels
to keep the Sea, they will go near the Coasts.

The Regiment of Bourbon of 3. Battalions had
arrived at Brest the 18th. This same Day the Batteries
of Ushant signaled an English Squadron, which
was cruising at this height.

The Garrison [d'Emis] that is being made at Le Havre and throughout
Normandy, [l'emblintiellement] the project Against the
Islands neighboring our Lands.
