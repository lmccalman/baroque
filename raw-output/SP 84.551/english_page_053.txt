DECREE
OF THE COUNCIL OF STATE
OF THE KING,

Ordering the suppression, & accounts for the 1st & 6th of next August, of the Lotteries
of the Royal Military School, of the City Hall of Paris, of the
General Association & that of the Religious Communities;
establishment of a new Lottery under the name of ROYAL LOTTERY
OF FRANCE, of which the first Drawing will be made on the 1st of September
next.

In union with the administration of the Royal Lottery, of the Lotteries of the Foundling
Children & of Piety which are preserved.

Of the 30th of June, 1776.

Extract from the Registers of the Council of State.

UPON what has been represented to the King, being in his Council;
that the different Lotteries established until now in the
kingdom, would not have been able to prevent subjects from taking their funds
to foreign Countries, to enjoy there the benefits & returns made
in the game of Lotteries which existed there: That the Lottery which His
Majesty had granted to the Royal Military School, although presenting
to the Public a game similar to those of Rome, Genoa, Venice,
Milan, Naples & Vienna in Austria, had not stopped this
