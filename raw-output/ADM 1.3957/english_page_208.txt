15 5 June 1781.

Toulon the 20. May 1780.

The Chevalier d'Oppede
with the Frigates Mesdames [Chabords], united
finally with the [Vente] the 18th has taken a small
[Chabord], and entered here the 19th, We
flatter ourselves that they will have to fall
together same [Monfsor]

The Courier from Versailles arrived day before
yesterday, has brought Orders which put
all our Port in Movement, also we
do not yet know what awaits us
[George] next.

These Orders are, to arm the [Chereteur]
the Puissant, the Sagittaire, The Magnanime,
the [Altionseille], the [Tamponne] from Brest,
which is found here, and two [Brillons] Ã 
[Bandas], which has everything almost ready to
leave by the 10. June.

The Champagne has brought back from Marseille
nine, which are excellent.

In the Project of the Minister for Mr.
de Bruyat our Commander, there
was a Letter for Mr. de [Roveas]
who is in the Country, and which has been
sent by an Express. He is expected
by which one sees that it is to him that is entrusted the
Command.
