                                                17 22 Sept. No 4

                    Toulon the 5th April 1770.

                         Two Vessels
and Two Frigates that are being put on the Shipyard,
one [Feuillette] at Sea, one [Sourgeon
d'Armement]. There in two Words are the
News from our Port.
   Two Vessels, four [Arbere] frigates
have been put at [Marthe], making [Reflette] for
Morea; their Crews are very numerous
in troops, they have not 200 Sailors On
a Vessel of 64, almost all are drawn from the
Navy in Morea, their Mission is
infinitely so.
   An English frigate anchored in
the Roadstead of Marseille, was surprised by a
Gust of Wind from the North West; she cut
all the Masts, chased Onto the Coast, lost
Her Rudder, she entered in
this State the next day into the Port of
Marseille, she is expected at Toulon to
be refitted.


                                                Paris
