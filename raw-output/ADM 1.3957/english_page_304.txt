The Parliament of Metz has written to the King about the
[Decret] which prevails in its District, & to
request that there be no Encampment
of Troops this Year.

The Tribunal of Auch, that a Capuchin
and a Recollet of Beziers have King, have
made their common cause before the Bishop, & the
peacefully the Gates had gone to Parity
of God, of the [Roqueur] that the Whites
executed these two, that it was time
to Deliver themselves from their tyranny. From the
Consequence all the [Religues] Had revolted
& that all was in Confusion in this
City. It is, they say, from a Vessel arrived
at Le Havre, that this News was known, which
is not guaranteed however.

Fiennes, August 3, 1770.
