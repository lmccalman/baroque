                                        18 30 Nov. 1773.

                    Brussels the 30 November 1770

                                It is announced from Barbier:
that the King has had the Cabinet Order
expedited to the Governors of Flanders,
of Tournay and Tournaisis, Namur and
Nombre de Boullenoys, as well as a
certain Quantity of Grain: It is
the War and Finance Councillor
of Arnheim who is charged with this
Detail. He is [Confès] Councillor of
the Order to furnish on his [expirens] to the Grand
Magistrate of Brussels and of Tourvelle and
Market of Borenceleury.

They were to march 2 Abbey, three
Artillery Companies, 4 as many to
Attre.

They are seeking in Bertin some Bourgeois
for all other Centre and [Brissous] of
Brusel to Dressé, they say this one
destined for the Royals.

It is learned from Verdun and from Clerue,
that an order from the King has been received
Express yesterday evening, though it is not yet opened,
it is said yesterday that the Commander of Ruremonde
who leaves for Princetteury to Bruckbourg
[instrigue] Bauveings.
