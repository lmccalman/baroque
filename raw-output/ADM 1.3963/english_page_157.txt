                    Paris the 2nd September 1778.

                    Our Letters from Brest
       of the 22nd, & of the 28th, announce no
       interesting News.
            Le Couatre has submitted his Statement
       of Lebaron, & believes to do
       with ease.
            The Journal of M. de Pereuc,
       has made [Banpêt], & is not absent
       neither at Court, nor in the City.
            The [Peutaine] of the Marshal
       Duke of Duras, is not arrested, & has
       [ma] at the Battle, by the arrival
       of Military Officers of a Place of
       Lyon, nor the Royal family itself
       is no longer exposed. The Marshal
       has not appeared at Court since,
       one [la scruit] of quarrel.
            The Mill at [l'acolas] of Lespine
       [offusté], a few days ago;
