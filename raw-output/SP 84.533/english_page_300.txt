Paris November 8, 1775.

We will have no other
News from our Ports, than the Deguenft of
the [Plose] of Brest.

The News interests and may interest a
public. Madame the Countess of
[Maronne] de Mirabeau, will give birth in
a few Days in Savoy; this Princess
will come Tomorrow to [la Meuttes], to
change Air, and stay there to do her
Churching. The Princess woman of
Chamber is dying of Smallpox,
which she caught from Her which
grieves her greatly.

The Camp will come on the 19th to Versailles:
the King will sleep that Day at Chery, and will
only hold it on the 20th at Versailles.
