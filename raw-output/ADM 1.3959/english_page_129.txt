643.

The Prince of Asturias comes to detain them
in Galicia with the exception of D. Fernando
of the Finance Committee, who has not until this hour
taken leave in particular.

The Spaniards often mistreat him
in the Cafés, as many great [Chanunes]
wearing English boots, and
Breeches over the Figuères for the
travelers.

The Duke of Ricones who was
Chief of the French Embassy at the Court
gave to H.M. the history of 13 hours,
traveling passing from Spain to France,
and which the Pope cannot refuse.

According to our letters from Rome of the 30th
September, the Jesuits have received there a
new Setback, the Pope has taken from them the
Germanic College; which, Besides the
places and the combats, the Pope appears from others
to preserve them. The new Ambassador
of Spain appears to have great
Credit in Rome.

Although the Spaniard has not yet
come, one sees annually to the
Pope what he will draw from it in
persecuting him.
