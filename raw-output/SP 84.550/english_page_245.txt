Paris, May 20, 1776.

The Letters from our
Children, here they are, [sçavent tranquiles]
until Departure of their last ones.
Our Merchant Vessel arrived from
its patrol, informs us [of] the Division of
Dilon, and reported News of
Good Health of the Duke of Chartres.

The New Minister of Finances
[dernier] yesterday in Paris around noon,
he will probably this Morning to the King at
Versailles, to take his Oath.

The Count of Maurepas has been
named Chief of finances of the
Kingdoms, and in that quality [le ce
très Bonnement]; the Controller
[moral] who will be publicly [demonné],
is from Rome, that he will [mourra] nothing
to make commerce navigate at its
[fort], which will well reassure
People, and revive Confidence.
That of the [Bourrée forte]
announced at 2028 Livres.
