One writes from Rennes of the 9th that the four
Members of the Parlement who were exiled,
returned on the 5th at the end of the Carcan, of the
Ladies, of the Peers &c.° The Parlement
paid them their visit in Robes, they presented
a Petition to request the release of the
other Members, & the Registration of the Letters of the
Charter. Since the Proscription of the Jesuits
from this Province, the Parlement wishes to make
use of the four Propositions against the Duke
d'Aiguillon, they are going to summon them to the King, & to the
Parlement of Paris, as it has just been answered
that the King had a plan all ready & that nothing can be done
in Brittany.

The King is going to appoint to 3. Bishoprics of the Court
which are vacant, one of which is worth 25. Thousand
Livres in Revenues.

It appears therefore that Avignon will not be returned,
because the Pope has promised the Dismissal
of the Society from his Court.
