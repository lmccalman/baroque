N° 30

                                           Paris the 8th April 1773.

                                           Because of the Vacations,
as is customary, the News
is very scarce; but they will end, and the
certainty that one will see the Parliaments
reopened.
    A Man Named Mercier exchanged at Rochefort
for the King, a Box of Snuff, which, gave him,
in a furnished Snuffbox, belonging
to Madame Clappier, which he had stolen
the Day of this Month; one brings his Complaint.
I know nothing until now of the abduction from
Brest since my last.
    I know nothing new for the
Finances, except that after the Establishment of the
Receipts, all will be settled.
    The King is still at Versailles, his
[Maître d'en espérance] is doing better. He is at
Muette, and [se ne targuera] not even to come
to Paris, in the Establishment of the Parliaments is
delayed by a few Days, Because of the
[Primes], who make some Difficulties of sty-
le. It would no longer be until the 16th, or 17th Current.
However, and current Letters of News
from the Provinces find nothing there, which would
make one believe the current Judges of Finance [vous
départir d'innombrable et brillante];
there will be represented in some way the
