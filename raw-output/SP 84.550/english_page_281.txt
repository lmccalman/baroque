First.

Aranjuez the 19th May 1776.

The Fleet, composed
of 20 Merchant Ships, and 2 war
[ships], departed the 2nd current [month] in the morning,
with a light favorable Wind; which
changed within Hours after,
[leforçe] that on the 9th at 8 o'Clock in the Morning,
they were only 30 Leagues from Cadiz;
they could not apparently be
at Mahón, which was judged, that they
took the [Loge].

Departed joining this Fleet, three
Ships for Cartagena, one
for Buenos Aires, and three for
Havana; which makes in all
24 Vessels.

On the 2nd current [month] left from Cadiz
a War Frigate, with a
[Yquette] of the Court, having for Callao
[Nois] of Provisions, it is supposed that it is
destined for Buenos Aires.

Etc.
