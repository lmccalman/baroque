Speech
made to the Doucillandres of the
Citizens and Bourgeois
assembled on the 13th and 14th of December 1766.

Gentlemen

We are on the Eve of a
Day, when it is urgent to Petition the State
and our Liberty. Each of us called
to reflect on the Project that must be delivered to
our Votes, after perceiving, that it tends
absolutely to deprive us of our Rights;
by rendering us the Preservers. Liberty of the
person, Liberty of Confidence, that of
Rule, of Order, and of Justice; no Citizen
among us is interested to make use of
this Liberty that our Ancestors
transmitted to us.

Two [Controprappans] will be offered
to our Gaze and to our Choice. On one side
the Senate of Bern watches, on the other Finance
and the Independence of the Fatherland, Economy
and the Liberty of each of Us. The
