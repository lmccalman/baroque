N° 62.

Paris the 29th July 1725.

                                                The Brigades of
Grenadiers, de la Mirche, and the Musketeers of
France, have arrived at the moment when the Brigades of
[Isguine], and Regency which were en route this
Part, arrived at Toulon on the 20th, they
encountered the two parties on the Coasts of
Sardinia. These two Brigades are commanded
by M. the Marshal of the camp of war, Count
[Camisson] forty [esperátion] Marie; they
lost only two men during their
Cruise, but they captured 32 Moors
in total, Algerians, and Tunisians, and among
others [Portants], and three the Crews with
their [vessels], which they took from Death and [nelles].

Two other [Brigantes] will be armed next
Month, to go relieve the two that are
stationed in the Archipelago, the King's Plan
is to maintain on this footing always four
Vessels for our Commerce. This is
all that there is in this Post of News.
Nothing at [Briltz], nor at Copenhagen.

Two Vessels returned from China, have
arrived safely at [Orient], richly
laden, these are those chartered for Messieurs
[Jamien] and [Rolh], two others are expected in
January 1726. The News that they have
