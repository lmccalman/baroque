Paris the 19th April 1771.

                            It is reported from Toulon
on the 11th, that the armed Frigates are still
in the Lazaret, awaiting Orders to
set Sail. Nothing from the other Ports.
     The Duke of Praslin quits Versailles, &
the Duke of Choiseul the Governorships in
which remains la Beaune, he a
[Jeunesse Afféidante] to the work with
the Ministers.
     The Duke of la vrillière ought to be
to the first Ministers of
Foreign Affairs, but the King wants
Action, [jusqu'avoir] his affairs with
monsieur Brulemente, to [acondler]
the [Douceure].
     The King [haiifait] to carry the Parliament to
[Palentant], and has the King made silent
to the Princes, that he [fuffrois fean beaux]
would have found to all Orders.
     The Defense [pr.les] to the Princes of
families at the Court, but there are still:
there is however a Representation
[Etiennois] for the advantages of
renewing their Protestation, they do not
find themselves [lassés].
                                                        Freiges
