Seen That the Cherreté informed that this
Maneuver was being done, wrote to Abbé Terray to
complain that in the Offices they
were making Salt Taxes among Themselves; That is to say
that some Ministers among them acted upon
the same Securities, would not combine
the Salt Taxes against anyone; but that He
personally would make a necessary
Maneuver on the [tounix] of the Ministers,
but that before presenting it to the King,
he would communicate it to Him.

Today this Response could not
take place, since they had his advice
some Absences.

The Reduction of two and a half Millions
made on the Bridges and roads, made everyone
cry out, they had the
Receipts renewed to maintain the roads, which
are already not too good, provided that the
monies destined for the Bridges and roads
amount to 600 Thousand Livres, which is still
much; it is necessary to keep the
Routes in Condition for the good of Commerce &c.

Mr. de Trudaine, Intendant of finances
whose poor health obliged him to go
spend the Winter in Provence at Aix des Fonts
brought back a Woman married to Him, where he
will remain
