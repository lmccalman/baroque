                                                R 117 envoy

                        Toulon the 2 May 1770.

                                                The work on the
hull of Atalante has been completed, her Armament and the [fonde]
at the Forms of the Merchant Ships of Marseille
which requested it to sustain it in the
Coasts of [Saragne] and Barbary.
     [On Destitue] today from Corsica, they
must transport there the Count of Marbeuf who
has remained numerous the Count of Vence.
     One gives to Mr. Oppede for the Atalante
Officers, and 2 of 50 men from the Medusa
with the agreement of Mr. de la Tour.
     To the Hundred Bandits conducted for our Bridges,
which are still in Corsica, they are caught every
Day, to whom no Grace is given, one has
said sent there are a few Days ago, in Bastia,
who were with these [Affichiers]. One assembles
the [Roile] to formulate & enter into the Citadel.

                        Paris the 11. May 1770.

                                                The fleet of Brest
Will be commanded by the Chevalier de Beaumont,
he is appointed the 7th of this month, but as
it must depart the 15th for its cruise [fuste finie].
     One reports from Rochef. & Several Vessels
that much Rigging has been stolen in the Port
and Equipments of the Vessels.
     The Letters from Brest announce nothing of interest.
                                                Sig.
