There is still question of a great
Decree of the Council, which will contain less the
Provisions of Reduction and Reform
than being of a Nature not to be
registered in Parliament.
It is said that the Estates of Languedoc before their
disbandment, [averes] of the King [la], will render to [Donne],
to make the Request of the [Deux Gratuit],
it is known that this is agreed, and [l'aver nee]
the Clergy has sent an [Evoyement], which has
not taken long to be settled.
The Estates of Languedoc have made
one of 8 Millions at 4 Per Cent, which has been
settled in [les Manieres]; they have received upon it
Rescripts and Notes from the Tax Farms
general.
There is still fermentation in
the Council, the Queen is interested in a great
Party, and Mr de La Vrillière in another;
it is not known which of the Two will prevail,
but it is believed that there will be nothing
before the Marriage of the Dauphin. The
[Pugeault] has for this Marriage the [rentriment]
in [fours] of the [Déportez] in common.

L. [Heugle]
