DECREE
OF THE COUNCIL OF STATE
OF THE KING,
Of January 2, 1771.

Extract from the Registers of the Council of State.

UPON the account which was given to the King, being in his Council, of a printed Memoir, entitled: Response of the Estates of Brittany to the Memoir of the Duke d'Aiguillon; His Majesty would have had difficulty persuading himself that any of the Orders of said Estates had taken part in a Writing which bears all the characteristics of a libel & an insult against all the enemies of good order & of public tranquility have infected the kingdom during the troubles which have agitated said province: And His Majesty would have seen with the greatest surprise, by a similar Writing, said in December last, in consequence of the opinions of the Nobility & the Third Estate, said Estates, assembled by his orders in the city of Rennes, have adopted said Memoir, made by the Commission which they had named to examine that formed & printed, & to respond to it, & ordered that three thousand copies would be incessantly printed thereof; to be distributed in said session, & that the same Commission
