Mr Turgot left in the Council a
Project, tending to clear all the Rights
of the Clergy against the [Moines du Papi],
who engage to pay these
Pensions to Archbishops, Bishops,
and Abbots, whom they had named
holders of the Benefices. The highest
Pension was 60,000 pounds,
and the most modest 1000 pounds. This
Project will be [Bilan] for 15 or 20 years.

They are going to establish, [faute] a great
change in the Edict of 20
Corvées, they will consult for this
all the Intendants; and they are thinking
of putting Barriers along the
Roads, to make Passersby pay
a Tax for the Maintenance
of the Routes, and [l'approvisionner] the Tax
ordered by the Edict. Seeing there
that little by little they are undoing what
Mr Turgot has done.
