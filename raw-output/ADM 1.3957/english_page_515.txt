Brest, November 25, 1710.

The Zephir in the Roadstead
has not yet departed. the Belle Poule
and the Elve Son, ready to depart immediately.
Orders were received yesterday, to arm
the Union of 64, which will be commanded
by Mr de Goijon, Ship Captain,
the Bleue the Seine and the O Hervet
of 20. Cannons. The Hermine is
armed.
There came to us from Vannes yesterday
20. chattes-marées, many others are expected,
and Merchant Vessels that the King has taken
The chattes-marées will serve to
go on reconnaissance, and make
the transports.
The Elevation of Mr de Chasny creates
here the most agreeable Sensation.
