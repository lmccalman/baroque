Report made to the Merchants of the City of
Amsterdam assembled on the 11th day of July 1748.

Gentlemen!

In consequence of the Commission which you have
intrusted us with on the 3d instant, the Merchants ap-
pointed to that purpose by the major part of Merchants
of this City, have desired and obtained a particular Audience
of His Most Serene Highness the Prince of Orange
& Nassau, our honoured Stadtholder &c. &c. &c.
at which time one of them has had the honour to
propose H. S. Highness in the following Words;

Illustrious Prince and Lord!

Commissioned by the greatest part of the Merchants
of this City we take the Liberty to approach Your M.H.
with the address of our most humble thanks for the high
favour done to us by granting us this most gracious
Audience.

The Body of Merchants to whom is intrusted
our M.H. in his very important occupation, has and
wishes the opportunity of Your Highness's renewing
the City with his presence without testifying their
satisfaction and sincere joy of this favour, which is true
love the honour to do in their name.
