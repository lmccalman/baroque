107.

Forty-two Troops, and the General
Officers by ordering to send them
a Peace establishment, as well as to
suppress 6 Men per Company
of the Infantry, of the Cavalry, and
the Dragoons; which made 16 Thousand
Men, in order to provide by increasing
by 600 per establishment the Pay of the Infantry.

M. Gay is First Clerk of the
War, having the Peace and the Movement
of the King's Thousands, the retirement to Bayeur,
the King grants him 24 Thousand livres of
Pension, the king always [recompt] the [de].

The Royal Carabiniers Regiment,
vacant for the Re[signation] of the Count
of Beurre, is given to the Marquis
d'Esgreville, son.

The [Embarquement] of Bethune;
which the Marshal de Bourglier had,
is given to the Chevalier de Maupeon,
currently General, Relative of the
Chancellor.

The Duke of Orleans has given then a
Supper, and a feast to the two Princes
of Bavaria.
