Extract from the Registers of the Council of State of the King

The King having had represented to him the decree rendered by the council in favor of Sieur de la Combe Counselor and attorney general in parliament of Auvergne and by which his said charter has been reviewed approved and confirmed, and having judged it appropriate to reconfirm it anew in all his rights, and in all his powers that they draw from it in conformity with his letters, namely, that of taking commissions from parties.

His Majesty having heard the report, the King being in his Council has ordered and orders, that the titles proposed under the conditions hereinbefore specified shall be executed and shall have their entire and full Effect according to their form and tenor, notwithstanding any oppositions or appeals whatsoever and without prejudice to them, done in the Council of State of the King, his Majesty being there, held at Versailles the twenty eighth of April one thousand seven hundred forty one. Signed Phelypeaux.

For copy conforming to the original deposited at the registry of the court of parliament.

The undersigned Chief Clerk of the Council certifies that he has collated the present copy on the original of said register existing in said registry.

In Paris the fifth of April one thousand seven hundred forty five.

De Fontaine, sole and only heir of the late De la Combe
