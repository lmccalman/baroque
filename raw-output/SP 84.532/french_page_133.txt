Troops
assembled, embarqued,
or intended to be embarqued
in the Ports of Spain
in 1776.

Places and Dates.  |  Troops, Number of Men, &c.  |  Destinations &c.

Cadiz. November 7th  |  On Board the Convoy and Garrison 800. Men of the 2d Regiment of Arragon, Malaga, & Company of Artillery, and Other Man Band.  |  Said to be Artillery men in the West Indies...

ditto......  |  Arrived 800. Men of the Regiment of the Prince of the Walloon Guard  |  To carry out Embarking, intended for embarkation...

ditto... 18th  |  3900. Men of Volunteers of Catalonia  |  Said to be destined for Pensacola and Embarkation the Army the letters will speak of Oran...

Carthagena ditto...  |  The Murcian and the Italian Regiments destined for Oran...  |  They will stay a great part when stay a great destined to Oran & the Balearics...

Barcelona Nov.r 18th  |  Troops Exercised, and Embarked...  |  Embarked for Algiers, Two Battalions of Men Catalonia, Artillery, and Stores Four Battalions of Italian Garrisons...

Madrid, November 9th  |  Major de Ville from St. Martin to Cadiz...  |  Thought to be destined for Embarkation...

ditto... 16th  |  A regiment of Ireland 800 Batalions...  |  Supposed to 800 Men destined to be embarked for the Havanna...
