Private Letter

Paris the 29th of July 1775.

One is finally
generally persuaded, that the
Remonstrances that the new Parliament
owes to the King, as much on the Gabelle as on
[l'ancelles]. It is claimed that the [Butit] of the
heads of the Magistracy is solely
to make known to the Public, that this tribunal
has no less Right than the old
Parliament, to make Representations,
that it uses them; but that one must stop
at the Point where one must, and without binding in the
ancient [formites], [avis Bornes] on
the Matter, when it presents in the
forms of regulations, Similar to
those of our Fathers Heads of the Magistracy.
but they carry Principles too
different, for it to be a
formal Opposition upon them.

It is desired, that after having worked
on the Creation of the Departments, one
finally comes to a Suppression and to
an effective union on the Parishes.
