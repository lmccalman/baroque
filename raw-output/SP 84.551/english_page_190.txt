To the Iroquois, which marked the
jealousy of the Royal Treasury, and what must
increase the Distrust of the Public,
It is that the Comptroller General
has already put his Hand on these funds in
advance, which should remain
in the treasury for the [hequillement] of
the prizes, there are some of the Brier.

The King had sent orders
to Mr. d'Abmory, who will begin at
St. Domingue, to remain still
in the Colonies, but there is reason
to believe that he will leave when
this Order arrives at the Cape, The
cold [lui fans la Bleaphere] which
occurred by the effect of thunder.

The Spanish have sent off
a frigate, and troops, to
go besiege & take Possession
of the Posts which had been
taken from them by the Portuguese. The [Deffend]
has completely fallen, but
the consequences of this
Armament are not yet known.
