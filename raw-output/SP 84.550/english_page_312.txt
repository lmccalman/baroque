Vienna June 5, 1776.

One congratulates, [promis]
the Principles of the current Ministry
of France, to see the good
order of internal affairs reborn,
and the fatal fermentation of Corruption
of this Kingdom abroad. For
the ambition of Mr Turgot has made seen
the greatest Confusion, and it is
feared that it may be followed by the
Dismissal of some other Ministers,
all the more so, as one does not
doubt, that Spain intrigues
much to bring about a
change in the current Ministry
of France, which, to its liking,
is too moderate and too pacific;
to which must be added the Party
