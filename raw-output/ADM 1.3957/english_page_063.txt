                        Paris the 12th February 1770.

                                        Our different Ports
are tranquil, there is no armament;
the funds have been consideration well only and the
small anxiety of the Parlements, which presented to them
the Remonstrances employed on the
Rights of the Council, however we have soon seen these
Reflections, that they did not expect to see
the Danger of what has newly arrived
[parle] and joins us.

This Minister is always occupied in seeking
Means of Economies, we expect to see
appear an Edict [leur] the King's Pensions,
we also believe that 300 Abbeys that hold
Foreigners would be [Cantonnellee] all
[a que l'Abbette il deleur] Kingdom will only be at
two and a half per Cent, we retain [davan]
except the [Bondes] not considerable that they are.

The Minister's Project is to increase
the number of [Bailleables], to increase
the Militias.

and Expenses of the Department and of War,
