705.                                    the 10th Xbre 1790

                    Brest the 28th December 1790.

        The Vessel Olivier, and the
flute the Fanny with a merchant Vessel loaded
with Munitions of war and Provisions arrived
yesterday. The unique intention of [l'enfourner] to carry these
[portées] of other lower Sailors, of the Bayard, and
Beauregard and Hoynant.
        The Lenoir distributed [ou exacte] of the others
the muskets of the other [portées] to the 3rd to the 4th and
Orders that he has [lien faire] his Cap [Ponéstaines].
        There are no new Orders from
the Court, all seen in good order.


                    Paris the 5th December 1790.

        They report from Toulon
from the 28th that Mr des Brunots, after having made
work before him at the Arsenal, retired
very tired, that new prayers were needed
to go fulfill his Account to the King of good work.
        No Order yet in this Port for an
Armament, [entre que retray stes quinze volés
Pasteur].
        A Letter from Martinique from 7 October
says that a [Belle] has arrived a frigate
to announce the war against the
English and the Spanish.
        The Displacement of two very
important Masters and some people [le engendre]
and News of the Day
