M. des Fossins, Colonel of the Grenadiers
of France, has obtained the Regiment of
Beauvoisis, from which Mr. le Clegny remains
dismissed.

An Edict has been published, which suppresses the
public Cellars, the Furnished Hotels, and
those of the Ports l'envoyée. There will be
only one in the Kingdom.

The Affair of Belle-Isle today holds
the Attention of all France. The [Charmes]
of the Posts have just published a
new Memoir against him, which amounts
to nothing less than having him hanged.

This Memoir is what one expects.

The Parliament assembled on Tuesday at 7 o'clock
in the evening, examined the Affair, and heard
the Report, an Decree was issued, which [dévaste]
a [Particulier d'Affaire] to be heard,

interrogated anew; that there will be
