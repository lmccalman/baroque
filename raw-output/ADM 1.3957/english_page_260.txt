4. 13. july

                        Paris the 8th July 1770.

                                    We received yesterday
letters from Toulon of the 29th June, which announced
others, dated aboard the Atalanta the 3rd June
before Tunis, this frigate the Artemis the Atalanta
were keeping the Pirates blockaded, the Bey has made known
to the Captains of our Vessels, that he had sent
treaty to the Ottoman Porte, but the Vessa.
failed. They await there the [Chevre de Toutous].
    The 17th there was one of our master Pilots.
    The Parliament which had postponed the Session
to Thursday 5th July, on the affair of Duke
d'Aiguillon, and because this and the [Deference] of the votes
old and younger Counselors was too
great, that you see in the Gazettes.
    He cannot remain in the exercise of the
Councils, she exposes the [lancerment]. These
Two Pieces have been neither posted nor published.
    The King demands that the King has that of doing
with ropes, the fund which is of King By the
Parliament assembled.
    One regards that by the Decree of the Parliament
the Duke d'Aiguillon is [tioche] in his Honor.
    The Places in Paris have been extremely dear
the Duke loves the Parliament and the King. Here is what
[dessent] that they approve of the assembly of the
Parliaments, others suffer that they will
