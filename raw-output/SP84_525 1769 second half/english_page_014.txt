Copy of the Letter from Mr de Bougny

of November 27, 1769.

I learned last Friday at the
Moment of Mr Evelen's Affair, and his
Liberation, the [anéantissement] of having a tranquil
Head, and scarcely have I even the same hour between
four and Five thirty, that someone
came up to the Door to give me the News;
but I have learned and if what he would have
previously either in the Interrogatories.

I have the honor to send you a Copy
of the [Depositif] of the Judgments rendered against him.
The Pilot of Bidston was judged to be hanged;
and was executed the same Day. The other
Doctor Evrard, of whom you have also
heard spoken, and for whom one [m'a mon]
of Mr Evelen's Voyage, was banished and [opiné]
first for the same Punishment: but some
as attenuating of his wrongs one has only that he
deserved death, I have not found as
Judge after Good lighter [demander].
I have opened the three of a little [annonce] man
[indefintes], and the Judges have [encouru],
on Condition however, that he would remain
Prisoner for a Year.
