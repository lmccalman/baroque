Versailles [la Rault] of the Year Two of His Majesty
it must be agreed that some extraordinary
Affairs have hastened his Return
      Being Counselor of the Parlement, he could not
disavow the Firebrands of this Company who
had the audacity to go to Versailles.
They arrived there Tuesday, they presented themselves
at the home of Mr. the Count of St Florentin, to tell
him that they were ready to receive the
orders of the King: the Minister responded to them
that His Majesty offended by their Conduct
did not wish to see them, and that he
ordered them to leave immediately to
go to Sens, and to remain there until further
order.
      They have just printed the Remonstrances
of the Parlement of Toulouse of the 13th of May of the 4th
of June and last, they tend to place again
before the Eyes of His Majesty the Motives
that lead this Court to beseech the King
not to continue the Command
of the Province of Languedoc to the Duke
of Fitz James, whose
new [la Conduite qu'elle tient l'Armée
dernière] they suppose toward all the Members of the
Parlement.
