                                                     14. 1 Oct. N° 3.

                           Paris the 24th September 1770.

                                    It is assured that Mr
Walpole charged with the Affairs of England
in Poland has orders to no longer meddle in them
and that he must soon return, but this is not [de fonds]
the Conditions, without doubt that the Spanish
will retain the Port that they took from an
Englishman a Malvine Town.
     The arrival of the French from all the decreed [nous
vois Polt], but there are orders and to extend the
Vessels ready to be armed. Our Navy
combined with that of Spain, form 100 or
120 large Warships which puts our
2 Courts in a position to struggle against our enemies
at least as many.
     They are leaving from England to L'Orient, of which
2 only for China, the 20 others to
go find Mr de Bougainville, for
[aux Isquettes] of the King, [Chilés], and three large Maurice
Merchants, on the Isle of Bourbon, and the Marquis
de Valbonne.
     The Position, and the Thetis armed as Privateers;
or here other Warships of the
Royal Navy, to provide the Escort.
     This small Fleet which must depart within a
Month, will carry 1500 Men of Troops, both
for the Isle de France, and for the Isles of
[Caramades], these troops are ready to be
embarked.
                                                                   Sie.
