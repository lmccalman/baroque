Paris the 27th August 1788.

According to the Letters from
Madrid, the journal Announced
from Mr. Barrulo, [attocit] until the 28th
July at that time he had made
Seven attacks, and Often bad
weather had hindered him, thus nothing
very important happened
in these different cannonades.

And according to news from Cartagena
of the 28th July, that we received here the day
before yesterday, Mr Barcello had arrived in
that port, driven from the bay of Algiers
by contrary winds. It is added
that the galleys of Melilla, were to
separate from the Spanish squadron,
to cruise more Alicante, and Over
Minorca.

It is assured that Mr d'Entrecasteaux
has been arrested in Lisbon.
