Summary

Madrid, October 29, 1734.

The continual arrival of
vessels from America in our
ports is immense; and we should
notice consequently,
a reduction in the prices
of goods that they bring us
from their side, but the large duties
that are required on all entries
whatsoever means that everything remains as
expensive, as during the war.

The latest news that
we have had from the squadron commander
Mr d'Amfreville indicates, that he was
struggling against the contrary winds
in sight of Constantinople.
