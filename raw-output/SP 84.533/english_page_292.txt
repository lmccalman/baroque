Private Letter

Paris, November 5, 1775.

People no longer speak
of the dismissal of the Controller General,
it is even claimed that this Minister
maintains how attached he is in
his ambition to remain in office
to gain time, and await
favorable circumstances, after
the wager of having to all the
modifications that he could bring
on his part to all the precautions
and tax increases that people
wanted to make, and to give Madame
the Countess of Barry a wine allowance
of 50 thousand livres per year, so that she
would be willing to continue her protection of him,
and oppose his disgrace.
It is always certain, [derivée] to the
orders of this lady, that the proofs
she gives, serve for Abbé
Terray as the King's vouchers, and that he
receives them as such. Several
