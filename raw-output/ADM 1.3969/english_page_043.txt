Paris the 21st March 1783.

The Couriers were still
detained at Calais on the 8th of the Current [month] by
contrary winds. Mr de La Motte, [démet]
conducts the Division of Brest and
Mr de La Clue, that of Toulon.
Mr de Vaudreuil, takes to the Antilles
with the [Respect de Crs Cannonnes], which R.
boards: a Frigate, a Cutter, and
some [Bâtires Hottailleur].
Mr d'Estaing is not expected in Paris
until next Month, because he
must stop at the Court of Madrid.
The uneasiness that had been felt
about the armament that the English
seemed to prepare for India,
appears to be dispelled. The day before yesterday
