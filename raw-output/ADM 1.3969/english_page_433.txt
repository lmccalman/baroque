Madrid, October 18, 1786.

They write from Cadiz that from
the 18th to the 24th of last month, seventeen
vessels entered this port coming
from Montevideo, from Vera Cruz, from
La Guayra, and from Havana, all extremely
loaded.

On the 3rd of the current month arrived at
Cadiz the Saint Mary and Saint[e], coming
from the South Sea, richly loaded
but especially the war frigate
Our Lady of [C], arrived the same
day, and departed from Cartagena on August 5th
last; she carries, both for the King
and for commerce, about one
and a half million strong piastres, in
gold and silver, four [centsons] of raw
emerald, and quantity of other goods.
