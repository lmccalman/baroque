Paris the 22nd October 1770.

The Letters from Mulhouse
report, that the Protestants & Catholics
of Rouen, and that a certain Dr Quarantaine, have
brought yesterday to the Council the Treaty of 1648, and have
received all at once a revision of the revision.
It is rather singular that while Lord Mac-
cartney was going to review, while everything is
in Movement at Bas-B[?].

The King worked Thursday the 20th of this month
with Monsieur the Duke of Choiseul, and He had
today Tuesday with the Duke of Praslin,
which spoke of the Letters arrived from Provence.

Nothing transpires from the Dispatches brought
by different Couriers to Fontainebleau.

The rumor that England has become even more for
War than for Peace.

Merchants sent from all the Ports
of England, one knows where. Does She want them? one
knows nothing of it here, our Speculators do not
even guess it. They want however
that the English go establish themselves in some
Corner of the Archipelago. If that is so, farewell our
Trade of the Levant, they will do us much
more harm by this means, than by making
pass the Conquest of Corsica. So much so that
they perhaps wanting some other
