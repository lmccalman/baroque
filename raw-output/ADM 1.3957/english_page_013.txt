                    Paris the 5th January 1790.

                                        A Vessel arrived
at the Isle of Rhé, has brought news
the arrival of Lafayette at Le Havre, and of his upcoming
contribution to the Revolution against the Spanish.
The latter have learned the freeing of this
Colony with expeditious forces to make it
respected. One does not know if one will make the
King to the French who would have some.
    There is nothing yet for our finances;
and nothing from our Ports. Eyes are fixed
on the new Comptroller General &
on what he will do. It is much to be wished
that Credit and public Confidence
be restored.
    The Chamberlain who has taken great Credit
in the Councils of His Majesty, must,
it is said, enter into the Council of State.
Tomorrow, one always speaks that he will be
made First Minister, and in this case
one thinks that the Duke of Choiseul will leave
the Ministry.
