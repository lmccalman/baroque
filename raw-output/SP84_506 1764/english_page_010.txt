17.) There is a Parson here that has for some one
Years past been employed by a Merchant as
an Interpreter to make Remittances to America,
it is therefore an able of the Demand of these
Remittances, and of the Prospect of Emigration.
The bearing his Expences, and a small Gratuity
if it be not thought proper to employ him any
further, would bring him over to be expected
immediately; all the Merchants who know him
give him the Character of an honest active and
intelligent Man.
