Paris the 7th June 1778.

The Duke of Aiguillon:
appointed inspector for a long time Minister of
Foreign Affairs, finally was declared
unfit to serve after Tuesday, a Member
of the Council was appointed in his place
for Marly; It is said that he must also
[deboucher] the General Intendant of Posts.

The Partisans of the Duke of Choiseul, and
those of the Old Parliament, take on all
appearance, the Chancellor must enter the
Council tomorrow: here is everything for.

The King sent to the Princes the Grand
Marshal of lodgings of the Household, to request
the keys of their Apartments at Marly -
The Ladies will be exiled Tuesday with the
Children to St Denis, to see Madame
Louise, the Dukes of Orleans and Chartres:
The Prince of Cond√©, &c, are sent back to
their homes, the King not having looked at
them today as well as the Chancellor.

The Chamber of Accounts of Paris is making
difficulties to register the Letters Patent
that fix the Treasury to the County of
Provinces.
