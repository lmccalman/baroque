N° 24 bis

Journal of Brest
from the 21st of March to the 10th of April 1758.

From the 21st     We have just learned through M. du Boisvray that
        there has been an engagement at the height of Cape Ortegal between the Squadron of Vice Admiral
        Boscawen, and the fleet [le Maftager], of the Division of
        Montvilles, [bas par] the Tonnant that he has [pates bouler] this
        flight, and that the Raisonnable was obliged to return to
        Port Louis to be refitted; M. Boisart [d'entendan] believed it
        [pratique], that this makes a [Parisquit] the war of hands for
        Cadiz, he had sailed from Port 2100 tons course
        very precious for the Subsistence of this part of the
        Indies of which it was loaded for Louisbourg.

From the 22nd.    The [Guerre d'amiral] of the seas has brought the appointment
        of 25 Vessels from Brest and from the fleet L.D. It is necessary to send to
        The Squadron which must be under the Orders of M. the Marshal
        de Conflans

From the 24th.    The Division of Beaufreue awaits only the wind
        to put out, as well as the Robuste, the Pleiade, the Calypso,
        the Eclair, the Anemone, the Ecurueuil which will escort and
        seek a fleet at Rochefort and at Bordeaux.

        [Cen affaire] the Boscawen destined for Quebec, and which
        [prevoit] will establish to assign moreover the Rampart of
        this important, it is said 28 Cannons that the King has bought from
        Private individuals, but the Commanders of these Vessels [n'ayant mê]
        are not yet [vremes], although we work with all diligence
        on their Arming

        There is an order to prepare nine the Formidable, Intrepide,
        Superbe of which we recognized the part of [Politique] of Port necessitated
        that we will form a Squadron to M. de Bompart, in order to
