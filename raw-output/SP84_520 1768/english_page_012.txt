Paris June 10, 1760.

The Letters from
Rochefort inform us, that they continue
to assemble there an army destined to embark
in that Port.

They write from Toulon and Marseille, that the
troops transported from Provence, have arrived
happily, and without opposition, in Corsica:
but that Paoli and the Corsicans neither
burst out nor mutter. Fearing the united Efforts, the
movements and the well assured; If we
want the force to Submit them to the Genoese:
They speak of the arrival of Mr. de Mirepoix in
the Mountains, and through Castles, where our
troops were not accustomed.

The next Drawing of the Lottery of Tickets
of the Discount Bank, which should only be
eighteen Hundred Thousand Livres, will be Two
Million 400 Thousand Livres: and the smallest
Prizes which were of each lottery, will be,
18 Livres, the Grand Prize is 200 Thousand:
If this increase in Benefits does not make these
changes, it must be said at least that they
increase greatly by the Rain.
