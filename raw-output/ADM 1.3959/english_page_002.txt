192.

A Questor of Cazars has learned, who
was to carry to Belleisle in Europe,
separately, & that he had it for France;
it is [entre] an Echo for our Commerce,
which had no need of it.

We saw the Ramille here, that one of the
French Officers who were at
Mr. the Baron de Castella Ambassador of Sweden.
The King came alone [l'encercit] last
two hours from [cheny] to Versailles;
His Majesty alone [ramelli] to Bellevue,
from where He went yesterday to St Denis, to see
Madame Louise: this tour was
[devant], it is said, very intriguing.

The Government of the Upper Rhine has
become vacant by the Death of the Marquis de la
Jannelle, has been given to Mr. the Duke of
Penthievre; Mr. de la Motte, & the
at Sea, a Marquis de Montmorin
Lieutenant General.

Madame du Barry came yesterday from
Bellevue to Paris to dine at the Duke
d'Aiguillon's, where were invited all
the Ambassadors. This Minister in a
[treille] compliance at the Court. One is better there
than the Duke of Choiseul ever was.
