Reg. Response

Toulon the 21st March 1793.

        The Transport Vessel
named the Three Lilies, departed from Genoa the
9th of this Month, on board which were embarked
and to which armament of the King, destined for
this Department, arrived the 21st of this
Month in our Roadstead; it had on board
about two Hundred Men of the
Regiment of Brittany, who [favorisent]
Depart from the French Troops, sent
successively to Mahon and to the Camp
of St. Rock. The Captain of this Transport
Vessel, has deposed having left the
11th of the Current, the Squadron of the King, in
the middle of the Strait, making Route
to come here. The Squadron has under
its Escort, a fairly large Number of
Merchant Vessels, destined for
different Ports of the Mediterranean,
and It is expected from one moment to the other.
The Chiefs of the Department have neglected nothing
so that It might find, upon its arrival, all that
the Land and Sea Troops will need.
