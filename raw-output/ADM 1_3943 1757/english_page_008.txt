In Paris, June 10, 1782.

A Ship Master
arrived at St. Malo, reports that Mr. DuBois de la Mothe
[a fuit] Mr. DuBois de la Mothe. It is believed that Mr.
DuBois has also arrived at Louisboury, where he
is a Passenger on our 75 of 74 Cannons if arrived
and well equipped.

He found himself with a Convoy which was believed that Mr.
DuBois de la Mothe would have intercepted in the
crossing an English Convoy escorted by three
Vessels, one of which would have been taken, and the two
others sunk.

The King has forced upon Marshal de Soubise
the Command of the army that he was having
assembled in Alsace. This Marshal continues
to enter into service, to carry it with
honor and with all in, to protect, and
serve by the Peoples. The Marquis du Mesle
has left for [tes on aller prevoir] the one after
the other. You see that we are going at it with good
fire good Money. One is all the more stung
against the King of Prussia, that by threats
he has made us fail for this year our
Projects, against England, the Monarchy
[d'vellens] becomes too dangerous. If he were
Master of Bohemia, he would be master of all Germany,
and would become the Powers of the Empire. He must fall
One has discovered Projects of the Emperor and one has taken
