Madrid, June 24, 1776.

Your vessels arrived
from America at Cadix, were announced to me
in my previous [letter], from April, the
warships America,
and the frigates [Manchouèles] the
Victoire, Valès, Polo, from Vera Cruz,
Buenos Aires, and the Canaries,
independently the King, large
for Commerce, in addition to
6 Million strong Piastres.

I am informed from Barcelona,
dated the 18th current, that they
were working there on the embarkation, without
excepting Sundays, on
war Preparations of all
kinds, and that everything should be
ready at the end of this Month, to
embark for Cartagena.
