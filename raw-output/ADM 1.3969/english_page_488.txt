The Artillery, which had not yet
received Orders, has received them these last
two Months, although it was always hoped
that the Chiefs could
take a good turn through
the Mediation of the King, and his good
Offices, the Army will always be assembled,
so as not to be caught
Unprepared, and to effectively
[pourrir] Holland, if one cannot succeed
in reconciling it with the Emperor.

The Nomination of the General Staff
of the Army, being in Suspense several
Officers, brings us here many
Petitioners. One is no more
certain of the turn that could the
Regulations for the Campaign take, which the
Lower Officers await.
