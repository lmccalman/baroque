N. 50.

                    Paris June 11, 1776.

         The King found very
inconvenient the furnishing, to have
lost all the Reuils. The Doctors
97 allées, who walked from the Thieve,
a man from Corscan, who falls
by the Bretonne, and noise in the Throat;
it is presumed that His Majesty
might well have the Measles;
he is being treated accordingly for
the regimen and the Measles.
    The Count of Artois was purged
for the first time the day before yesterday.
He goes very well, and it is believed that he
[trainé à des levé] yesterday and for
the Day.
    The Duke of Choiseul returned
to Chanteloury. It has been a long time
indeed, since this man
received an order to stay there, and that he has not
listened to. The Courtiers, who are
                                numerous
