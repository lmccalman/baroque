N. 21 Def.

Paris, December 24, 1794.

The Marshal of France worked
yesterday with the King on the [exis] of the Generals
Replied. He proposed [de Elle a été
informé moyen toute la l'ouvreb]
with some chiefs of the Military Corps,
Contractors, &c; thus one no
longer doubts that the General Officers
have been well appointed.

The Captains and the other Officers
of the French Guards have made
the most earnest Entreaties to
their Colonel, so that he might testify
to the King the Desire they have to Serve
in the Armies that are going to be
assembled. His Majesty replied
that He was too persuaded of the
zeal of his Officers of the Guards, and of the
great Services that this Regiment could
render, not to employ it.
