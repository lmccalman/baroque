Warsaw, November 3, 1795.

The day before yesterday the King, while
returning from going to the castle of Lazienki, to
visit once more the Remains of a sad Grand
Chancellor of Lithuania, who was indisposed,
was assailed by 30 horsemen, in the
narrow streets corners between the Radziwill hotel
of Biacobie, & that of the late Grand General of
the Crown Branicki, after they had
stopped the Carriage, several of them fired
at the Doors, killed two of the Horses
which brought him to their Enterprise. He
fortunately had three gunshots, which struck
in [Bulles]. Finally the King after having been
disarmed & the [Volante] the Carriage having
walked several Hundred Paces on foot in
the Mud, surrounded by Horses & [Carlés]
on the Head. During the alternations of the snow
and troop with the star, they fired at him from three
& to a body from Below, which missed, but
whose Heat he felt, without counting in
several other Pistol shots of Sabers
which only broke the Membranes & the forces.
After having made him mount the [Seime]
he reached the front before the door where he extends left
& one being & [Boulé] that one surrounded the Cell.
The King fell twice with the Horse.
He got up from it, & then the Sovereign of himself
lost
