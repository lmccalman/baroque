                                                        Krafftey N.3

                            Paris the 4th January 1773.

          The King was working
presently with Marshal Muy, the Minister
[d'Estrombie] yesterday at Marly, to bar
their passage and tell them that it is always
imprudent to speak of Foreign
Affairs, and to M. the Marshal Duke
there is always question of the Duke d'Aiguillon
for one, and of the Count de Broglio for
the other, but for some time now,
one also hears speak of the Count
de Castries, and of the Duke de Guines.
The balance in the [Penture].

Our Parliament must always be
the Conversation of all the World. The
[Grossiers] and the Executioners having already
finished reporting, their work is
established, some hold that it could be
soon official, the others for
[des Battants] say that it must not
depart, when it would be the Palaces,
one could bury them as Yesterday.

The Duke de Choiseul has been dangerously
ill for a Long time and one
is astonished, but he is better.

The young Duke de Lizon des Bournes
has returned from Chanteloup to Versailles;
